Unrendered craft/why for `scenes/peekaboo.md`. Scene compaction lives in [[meta-condensed-peekaboo]]; this holds only the decisions the prose and the condensed don't carry on their face.

## The patent-register rhyme is deliberate

At the door Pace breaks off the near-sex *grinning,* boyish, eyes already past her to the thing he'd made. That reads in the same register as the {{Two Towels}} patent moment — voluble childish wonder, eyes on the work rather than on her face for praise. The echo is a **feature**: eyes-on-the-thing is exactly what keeps his pride from reading as boastful. Don't "fix" the resemblance in a later pass. The only real risks were word-density (grin/smile) and the child-simile vehicle, both already dialed on the page.

## Taste-thread: intentionally not seeded

Per the sex-scene default the taste thread was *considered*. This scene cuts at the threshold ("nothing left for either of them to decide"), so the taste beat is **deferred, not missed** — no seed here is the correct call, not an oversight. [thread: `meta-note-taste-thread.md`]

## Two-tier shame guard

The scene's shame is a **deliberate act owned as heat**, not an involuntary bodily tell — she slides the underwear off *on purpose to be looked at*, and the shame crosses into wanting before it's fully shame. This is her first initiation. Keep it distinct from the involuntary-tell shame the earlier scenes run on; don't re-light it at that register.

## Frictionless by design

The scene is joyful and near-shameless at the close on purpose — First Night spent the fitting's deferred tension, so this runs a different engine (vary heat by *kind*). Its purity is the high-water mark the spring later corrupts; Pace's all-night devotion is real and uncalculated *on the page* — the tenderness that is also instrumental, never tipped here.
