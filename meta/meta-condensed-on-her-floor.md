# Condensed — On Her Floor

*SCENE · Unwritten · late January · Vee POV · FB (Randi/Vee format-break)*

Randi invites Vee as her plus-one to a sorority dance party. Vee — who wanted Greek life but couldn't afford to pledge — is thrilled, and doesn't have the right dress; Randi loans her a stretchy-knit skirt that fits both of them but behaves differently on Vee. At the party Vee is warmly welcomed, until Brooke surfaces and prices Randi without ever dropping the smile. Dancing leaves both girls excited and sweaty; the skirt rides up. Randi spots Harrison, says *"help me put on a show,"* and under that cover kisses Vee — long, slow, open-mouthed, to the tongue when Vee doesn't resist. Then she takes it back: *"You really did a great job putting on a show."* Vee is left unable to ask to repeat it.

The whole evening is **borrowed belonging**: Vee gets the sorority world on loan, in borrowed clothes that don't quite fit and ride up, and the kiss is given and then re-filed as "just the show." The evening rhymes with the garment.

## Beat order

1. **The invite.** Randi invites Vee as her plus-one — *"You're more fun than any guy — haha."* Vee, who couldn't afford to pledge, is thrilled. Seed the class wound *lightly* — belonging on loan, never underlined.
2. **Dress for the party.** Vee doesn't have the right dress; Randi loans the stretchy-knit, Newton-ring skirt. *"Not that short"* deploys twice (see Title Logic). The stretchy knit is the fit mechanism.
3. **Arrive — welcome, then teeth.** Vee generally welcomed, warm, inside the golden world. Then **Brooke** in her canon register (settled warmth, never a raised voice), costing Randi her frame-ownership because Brooke outranks her. Vee glimpses that even this world has teeth and that Randi isn't queen here. (Don't *also* have Brooke price Vee — that's her Smoothie-Shop move; one menace target per beat.)
4. **Dance.** Disco handholds, Randi leading **less capably than Pace** (technique-not-instinct; Vee's body notices the wrongness without naming it). Excited, sweaty. Touch-signature install + deniability mechanism, kept quiet under the fun.
5. **Skirt rides up.** Body-as-betrayer; fresh shame keyed to *public display in borrowed clothes* (distinct from any wetness-shame). Welcomed → exposed → caught.
6. **The show-frame.** *"Oh, there's Harrison. Help me put on a show."* — after they're already sweaty and lit up, so the kiss rides momentum, not a cold start.
7. **The kiss.** Long, slow, open-mouth, to the tongue when Vee doesn't resist. **Transgression in the major key** — the heat is *doing the forbidden thing under permission.* A big step forward.
8. **The pull-back.** *"You really did a great job putting on a show."* Randi re-files the kiss as pure performance and walks (grenade-and-walk; protect deniability). This is what *produces* the uncertainty.
9. **Uncertainty.** Vee can't ask to repeat it — repeating would mean naming what the show let her not name. She doesn't know what she wanted: the kiss, the joke, the attention, the safety of Harrison as excuse, or Randi.

## Title Logic

**On Her Floor** means Randi's sorority floor, the dance floor, and Randi's chosen ground. Vee is in Randi's clothes, among Randi's people, moving under Randi's lead, with Harrison as the public alibi.

The phrase "Not that short" remains inside the scene and deploys twice:

1. **First instance:** Randi offers to loan clothes. Vee mentions height difference. Randi: "Not that short." — warm reassurance, no double meaning yet.
2. **Second instance:** Vee puts on the skirt, worries about length. Randi, teasingly: "Not that short." — same words, now loaded. On Randi, it isn't. On Vee, it is.

The repeat is a tell on reread.

## Harrison / Show Frame

Randi sees Harrison and creates the alibi: "Oh, there's Harrison. Help me put on a show." The surface register is common college-girl performance: playful flirtation for a male audience, funny and deniable. The actual work is Randi choosing Vee in public and giving the kiss a frame Vee can use to avoid owning it.

The kiss is paused, framed, and sensual. They are not moving when it happens. The disco handholding gets them there; the kiss itself is the still point. It escalates beyond the established goodbye-kiss pattern because it is public, open-mouthed, and sustained under the cover of performance. The heat is the **transgression under permission** — how far a kiss can go when "it's just the show" licenses it.

The pull-back closes the frame: *"You really did a great job putting on a show."* Warm praise on first read; on reread, Randi slamming the frame back down so Vee can't own the kiss. It passes both registers with no word changing. Vee's after-state is uncertainty, not recognition: she does not know whether she wanted the kiss, the joke, the attention, the safety of Harrison as excuse, or Randi. She cannot repeat it because repeating would require naming what the show let her not name.
