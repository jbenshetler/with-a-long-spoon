# Condensed — On Her Floor

*SCENE · Draft complete (meet-up, pre-DJ/Brooke, dance/kiss, coda all drafted) · late January · Vee POV · FB (Randi/Vee format-break)*

Randi invites Vee as her plus-one to a sorority mixer, held at a rented bar just off campus. Vee — who wanted Greek life but couldn't afford to pledge — is thrilled, and doesn't have the right dress; Randi loans her a stretchy-knit skirt that fits both of them but behaves differently on Vee. At the party Vee is warmly welcomed, until Brooke surfaces and — by flattering Vee with total recall — warns Randi without ever dropping the smile. Dancing leaves both girls excited and sweaty; Randi runs the choreography until the skirt rides up and Vee's green thong shows to the room, then leans in: *"Your panties are showing."* Before Vee can recover, Randi spots Harrison, says *"let's put on a show,"* and under that cover kisses Vee — long, slow, open-mouthed, to the tongue when Vee doesn't resist. Then she takes it back: *"You really did a great job putting on a show."* Vee is left unable to ask to repeat it.

The whole evening is **borrowed belonging**: Vee gets the sorority world on loan, in borrowed clothes that don't quite fit and ride up, and the kiss is given and then re-filed as "just the show." The evening rhymes with the garment.

## Title Logic

**On Her Floor** means the dance floor and Randi's chosen ground — she picked the venue, she runs the floor. (The literal *sorority floor* sense is retired with the venue change to a rented bar; the dance-floor and chosen-ground senses carry the title.) Vee is in Randi's clothes, among Randi's people, moving under Randi's lead, with Harrison as the public alibi.

The phrase "Not that short" remains inside the scene and deploys twice:

1. **First instance:** Randi offers to loan clothes. Vee mentions height difference. Randi: "Not that short." — warm reassurance, no double meaning yet.
2. **Second instance:** Vee puts on the skirt, worries about length. Randi, teasingly: "Not that short." — same words, now loaded. On Randi, it isn't. On Vee, it is.

The repeat is a tell on reread.

## Harrison / Show Frame

Randi sees Harrison and creates the alibi: "Oh, there's Harrison. Help me put on a show." The surface register is common college-girl performance: playful flirtation for a male audience, funny and deniable. The actual work is Randi choosing Vee in public and giving the kiss a frame Vee can use to avoid owning it.

The kiss is paused, framed, and sensual. They are not moving when it happens. The disco handholding gets them there; the kiss itself is the still point. It escalates beyond the established goodbye-kiss pattern because it is public, open-mouthed, and sustained under the cover of performance. The heat is the **transgression under permission** — how far a kiss can go when "it's just the show" licenses it.

The pull-back closes the frame: *"You really did a great job putting on a show. I almost believed it."* Warm praise on first read; on reread, Randi slamming the frame back down so Vee can't own the kiss — while *"I almost believed it"* leaks that Randi is the one who nearly dropped it (she comes back a beat behind, clears her head before the grin returns; affect shown on her surface only, interior withheld). It passes both registers with no word changing. Vee's after-state is uncertainty, not recognition: she does not know whether she wanted the kiss, the joke, the attention, the safety of Harrison as excuse, or Randi. She cannot repeat it because repeating would require naming what the show let her not name.
