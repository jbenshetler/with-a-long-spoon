# Note — Secret Plans (The Princess / Vee's Adventure)

*Diptych · Architecture complete, prose not drafted · ~late March–early April · replaces Nipple Clamps · companion to `meta-condensed-secret-plans.md`*

A workshop-committed scene. The diptych is **The Ask** (Vee asks) and **The Princess** (the scene itself), with Pace's planning/building as an **off-page [EVENT]** between them. It replaces the planned Nipple Clamps scene (the clamp *sensation* survives inside this one; see Continuity below).

## The spine (the one thing to internalize)

This is **the first scene Vee authors.** Canon has her *initiating* before — striding to him bare at the {{Dress Pickup}}, texting first at {{We Find Out}}, electing her own exposure at the cabin — but never *imagining a whole scene and handing it to him to stage.* Scheming requires imaginative authorship; that doesn't appear earlier. So "first time Vee schemes" holds, and it's a real new rung in her agency.

Why it's more than "she gets confident": the whole book runs on Pace and Randi orchestrating Vee — she's the one who cannot see the shape of what she's in. This is the **one time she holds the pen.** And what does she write? A scenario in which she is *chased, captured, stripped, and broken.* The first time she gets to direct, she casts herself as the captive and hands Pace the controls. **Her authorship and the machinery are the same act** — first read, brave and fun; reread, she authored her own deeper surrender. This is the "adventure"-word logic made literal (she coined the joy-word that later walks her in blind), and a **rehearsal of the rationalization-frame** she'll use to walk into the threesome (build a fiction that lets you want what you can't say plainly). Never stated on the page — resonance only.

**Hold the asymmetry hard:** she schemes it but does **not run it.** She commissions; he executes. She designs the *wish*; he engineers the *scene*. That gap is the point — the one time she authors, she authors her own handing-over.

## Panel 1 — The Ask

- A quiet, warm domestic beat (afterglow, or a low-key evening). Small and brave, not a production.
- **She hands Pace the premise, not a script.** She names the shape — space princess, chased, caught, stripped, "interrogated" for **secret plans she'll never reveal** — and leaves the **"torture" unspecified** (*you decide*). Pace designs the rest. (This is also the practical hinge for drafting: *what* the torture is can be tuned to wherever the physical staircase actually stands when the scene is written.)
- **The bidirectional "adventure" pays off here.** She coined it ({{Still Life}}); Pace pointed it forward as a consent-gateway (the cabin); now **she** uses it to invite *him*. Keep it a handshake, not a leash — her agency. Sparse, unwinking; never named as a code.
- **Voice guard (the Peaches trap).** The roleplay is "easy for Vee" — but *easy* means she inhabits it **bodily and emotionally**, not that she gets verbally clever. Witty banter is borrowed register and shows as a seam. The persona is easy at *entry* because it is **cover** — as "the princess" she can be brazen, can play at wanting, in a way she can't as herself (same machinery as "it's about adventure": a frame that lets her permit herself). Keep the ask shy-brave and plain; let the blush carry it. She's confessing a fantasy, not pitching a screenplay.

## [EVENT] Pace Builds (off-page, never rendered)

Like the Drawer's first visit, this lives off-page — it protects the surprise for **both Vee and the reader**, and keeps his planning unperformed (the strongest version stages nothing).

- **The costume.** Barely covers her when she's still; **highly revealing in motion**; seamed with **few stitches so it tears away a piece at a time.** This is canon costume-logic: Pace gifts garments (the dress, the plum lingerie, the tap pants), and *building the failure into the seams* is a Pace-precision tell — he crafted her a gorgeous thing **designed to come apart.** Tender AND instrumental, unresolved: a labor of love that is also an exposure-engine.
- **The "alien tech."** The sci-fi frame **licenses devices normally outside his register** — a **vibrator** and a **nipple corrector** (a suction device, real-world use on inverted nipples, here run in **cycles/pulses** for pleasure or light pain). The license is diegetic (the overlord's interrogation hardware); structurally the devices **fold into his signature** rather than violating it — each is another lever for him to control *when* relief arrives (the bench's deny-the-grind, sci-fi-skinned). See **The devices** below for the register guard.
- **Making-as-love.** The hours of labor are the *I love you* he can't speak (rhymes the oiled-wood key box, the dress). Submerged: he is *engineering the costume and the apparatus* of her own undoing — sweet on the surface, instrumental underneath.

## Panel 2 — The Princess (the scene)

**POV:** close third on Vee (erotic scene, reader-ahead). **Heat-kind:** opens on a register the book hasn't used — **kinetic / athletic play** — then ratchets to **psychologically-intense** at the capture. (Vary heat by *kind*, not just intensity; this earns tonal contrast for the dark ending.)

### The chase

- **Outdoors on his private land, a warm afternoon, full daylight** (the property is established as private; warmth is why it's late-March/April). Continues the **daylight-hinge** thread — full-sun exposure on open ground, the same "no darkness to hide in" the cabin blazon used.
- **The running is the first exposure.** The costume reveals in motion, so she undresses to his eye *by fleeing*, before he lays a hand on her. Pieces torn loose as he grabs and comes away with fabric, not her — exposure starts mid-flight, a staircase before the capture.
- **A swat or two landed in passing** jolts her stride and lets him gain — his signature ({{A Recognized Method}}, the bench) deployed in a brand-new kinetic occasion.
- **Pace stiff → warming.** Canon-true twice: his power is *real*, not theatrical, so of course he's stiff at performance; and his warming is his signature responsiveness (craft doc: "something moves in him, his attention slides toward what she just produced"). He catches the bit **because she's lit by it.** This inverts the usual lead/follow — she's fluent in the fiction, he catches up — the agency-inversion rendered in the texture of the scene.
- **Early near-escape → her interior disappointment.** She thinks she's getting away; inside, a disappointment she can't account for — **render it in the body** (she eases up, doesn't run her hardest), never named. *The easing is the tell.* Then **the surge**: he reveals he was only toying — her flight was always on his leash. First read: hot power-reveal. Reread: her authored control was always his, the whole book in one move. **The narration must not comment on the parallel** — sexy dominance on the surface, the structural rhyme felt only later.
- The capture's shame: caught having *wanted* the chase to continue — given away by easing up.

### The interrogation

- **Device alternated with hands.** Device sets/holds the edge (the deny-the-grind lever); hands collect the confession; he watches her face throughout. **Guard:** even mid-machine, his *attention on her* stays the center of gravity — the overlord can be clinical on the surface, but the reading-her must leak, or the device reads cold and un-Pace (and tips toward Randi's register; see guards).
- **Two synchronized staircases, keyed to each escalation:**
  1. **Costume** — torn piece-by-piece; exposure ratchets *up*.
  2. **Persona** — the **recurring failed grab**: each time her body confesses, she reaches back for "the defiant princess," and the grab fails harder; composure ratchets *down*. The persona that was *easy* at entry gets impossible to hold — **the cover she walked in under is the thing taken away from her.** Garment and composure come apart in the same motion.
- **Symmetrical dissolve (the scene's quiet architecture).** Her princess burns off → pure wanting Vee. His stiff-overlord burns off → real Pace-control. Both started in fiction; the scene cooks both frames away and leaves the dynamic they always actually are. When his mask slips it isn't that he drops character — the realness was always under it. **This is the answer to the theatricality risk:** nobody's winking by the end because the theater is gone.

### The fresh indignity (shame engine)

Shame is load-bearing, and the *occasion* must be new (never re-light a prior one at the same intensity). The new occasion here: **being caught wanting it while performing resistance** — the gap between her scripted *"you'll get nothing from me"* and her body's immediate, involuntary yes. Not the spanking-shame, not the watched-shame — a register of being-seen-wanting the book hasn't fired. The persona-as-shield *fails* (like the tap-pants on the bench), leaving her more exposed than a straight scene could, *because* she tried to playact through it. Never resolves (shame stays gasoline).

### The afterglow

The tender-domestic exhale the scene needs after the intensity (heat-by-kind, restful close). Pace teases *"So — what were the secret plans?"* → *"There were never any plans."* Funny on the surface; quietly exact underneath — the entire interrogation extracted a confession with no content except *yes.* Never explained. Arms the phrase for Randi later (see The phrase chain).

## The devices (register guard — load-bearing)

Pace's entire erotic power in canon is **real-time, hand-to-skin, reading-her-body** control. Powered devices are categorically *absent* from his register — and *theatrical, prop-named toys are **Randi's** register* (her loud, scripted fake-BDSM, which the book deliberately keeps **un-matchable to Pace** so Vee's non-recognition stays plausible on reread). A buzzing vibrator in a costume scene is therefore a live seam-risk. Two things hold it:

1. **Fold the device into his signature.** He holds it, sets the corrector's pulse cycles and the vibrator's timing, and **times every change to what her body does.** The machine becomes one more way for *him* to own the *when*. "Alien tech" skins it; the mechanic is pure Pace. The device never becomes the center of gravity — his attention does. (Test: if you can delete his face from a beat and it still works, you've written a Randi prop, not a Pace one.)
2. **The frame-dissolve protects the retell** (see guards). Even theatrical, with a device in hand, Pace is devastatingly *real* — which, narrated to Randi, *reinforces* the quiet-vs-theatrical distinction rather than collapsing it.

**The stats mirror moves here, upgraded.** Nipple Clamps was the planned home of the **continuous-vs-discrete** class-mirror; the corrector running in **cycles/pulses (discrete)** against the **vibrator (continuous)** carries it more cleanly than clamps ever did. Subtle, never explained (per the class-texture rule and flag 10).

## Craft rules in play (guards)

- **Earn the dark by being light.** This is the most *fun* beat in the book; the cold (self-authored cage; the configuration in miniature) is reread-only. If you can feel the author signaling the cage, cut the signal.
- **Body before mind.** Her near-escape disappointment, her body's yes under the scripted no — rendered, never narrated as deduction.
- **Never explain.** The authorship/configuration rhyme, the MacGuffin-as-yes, the rationalization-frame pre-echo — all resonance, never exposition.
- **Pace's tenderness real AND instrumental, unresolved.** The lovingly made costume is built to tear; the indulgent devices advance his control vocabulary and feed the season's compounding appetite. On the page it's only love.
- **The seam-guard for the retell.** Pace using *devices in costume* is, on its face, Randi's register — the most-protected distinction in the book. The **frame-dissolve** is what protects you: in the retell Vee conveys not "Pace did a fun prop scene" but *"I thought it would be a game, and then he—"* — the realness that undid her *through* the costume. Keep the retell foregrounding the **dissolve**, not the gadgets, and the seam holds (and is reinforced: even theatrical, Pace was real; Randi never could be).
- **No telegraphing Randi.** The brunch and the joke must read as warm/fun/flirty first; suspicion kills arousal.
