# Note — Old Acquaintances (the cost-weighing; the beige plant)

*Vignette · Drafted · rendered on **New Year's Eve**, Christmas break · Vee solo, at her parents' home (Ohio) · companion to the chronology's [VIGNETTE] Old Acquaintances · one panel of the break triptych ({{Fairytale}} / {{The Usual}})*

## What this scene is now

The one time the novel renders Vee away from school, and the place the book's bargain is first *weighed* in her interior. Sleepless in her childhood bed on New Year's Eve, she holds two intolerables and can set neither down: a life **without Pace** (this — the flat, safe, unseen home she has reverted into) and **Pace without a name** (the wound she carried home from {{Believe Me}}, his silence over the break, and — its sharpest form — the jealousy). The scene **sets the scale; it does not tip it** — First Weekend Back tips it (`meta-note-first-weekend-back.md`). Here she only feels the weight.

It also **plants the beige-life thread** (registry: `meta-arch-bible.md`): the home is rendered as the concrete cost of the without-Pace life, so that at the very end — after the reveal — when she **chooses that same beige life knowingly**, the callback lands. The canon line *she is somewhere else without knowing she's left* is the seed the ending inverts: at Christmas she leaves without knowing; at the end she leaves knowing. Payoff: `meta-arch-vivienne.md` → The Cassie Scene.

## Form (the one rule that shapes everything)

**One location, one night, all interior: alone in her childhood bed, sleepless, New Year's Eve.** The parents never appear on camera — home arrives only in recollection and on her phone. This keeps the discipline that *only school-people are present in a scene* (the same move as {{Cropped}}'s off-page table), and it structurally mirrors {{Bare}}: one bed, one night, interior. Everything beige — the tree, the wrapping, Wheel of Fortune, the questions — is memory, already flat-tinted.

## The New Year's frame (the clock and the turn)

- **The renunciation is now literally a resolution she doesn't believe** — *I did it before, I can do it again* — and doesn't.
- **The missing midnight kiss is the naming-wound made ritual:** the one night the whole culture is about someone's arms at midnight, and she has none but the imagined.
- **The calendar turns under her:** the year flips to one where Randi's penciled in (school resumes, the brunches resume) and Pace is a blank — the scheduling-asymmetry closer lands on an actual turning page.
- It gives a flat, plotless vignette a **clock** — counts down to midnight and past it into restless sleep.
- The house is beige-quiet: a nurse and a mechanic asleep early; she's alone in her room as midnight comes.

## The callback to Bare (structural, not literal)

The shirt stays at school — **left on purpose**, a post-Christmas-fight renunciation (*she doesn't need Pace or his shirt*): the same gesture that closed {{Bare}} (she took it off into the worse cold) now hardened into a resolution and carried 500 miles. So the callback is **the scent channel inverted.** {{Bare}} was saturated with *him* — the sawdust and the salt of the work; this room is saturated with *her mother* — fabric-softener sheets, plug-in lavender in every outlet, *like an old lady* — and no Pace-scent in the field at all. Same register (smell, sleepless, in bed), opposite content. **Do not name the parallel;** let the room's smell simply be there. **Do not re-use the shirt object** — the rhyme is the bed, the sleeplessness, the empty reach.

## The room-museum (reversion; seen vs. safe in objects)

Academic ribbons (the round ones, bunting around the edges), synchronized-swimming trophies, photos of high-school friends, the clarinet from band. The room sees old-Vivienne in high resolution and has no idea who's in the bed now — it loves a girl who no longer exists. The twin bed too small. This is the seen/safe axis rendered in objects.

## The two poles (the scale, embodied — never a ledger)

- **Without Pace = the home tableau, made concrete.** The beige food that was *never enough* (Ohio casserole/potato/meat — love that isn't the thing she wants now); the couch and Wheel of Fortune; the childhood bed; Dad's Corolla in the drive. Safe, small, **unseen** — her parents love *Vivienne*, the daughter-role, not the her that Pace and Randi see. This is what a life without him looks like, as a place.
- **Pace without a name = the leak** (his silence, the fume, the failing renunciation) — and its sharpest form, the jealousy (below).
- **Sideways leak:** she thinks she's just restless, homesick-in-reverse, and cannot feel that the flatness *is* the weighing. Body before mind; she oscillates, she never tabulates.
- **The brother is absent** — spending the break at his girlfriend's. Everyone pairs off and leaves the childhood house except Vee, sent back into it; with one kid home, the parents' focus (*when do we meet him*) concentrates entirely on her.

## The phone — the Pace cluster

- Checks whether he's written (nothing). Scrolls his old texts.
- **Writes a message, deletes it** — relieved he doesn't sit on his phone, probably didn't see the abortive attempt (Randi's *he doesn't sulk, he leaves you the room* proven from the inside; keep her relief real, the loneliness the reader's).
- Photos of the body he loved: her and Pace being silly on his couch; her in the **borrowed clothes before the CW Dance** (borrowed off the dorm, reader-only, unremarked); her in **the dress he made** — the uncropped induction candid, whole and thrilling on her phone while her mother has the **cropped** print somewhere downstairs. Same photo, whole and cropped, one house — seen/unseen in a single object.

## The Instagram — the postcard (reader-hollow; the envy, corrected)

Randi's Gstaad feed: ski-slope summit, palace facade, dressed-for-dinner. **Two phones, two lonelinesses:** Vee's holds people (relationship); Randi's holds places (performance).

**The envy is knowing, not innocent.** Vee *knows* the trip is poisoned — Outlier gave her the dirty money and a grieving friend — and envies the postcard **anyway**, because from the couch and Wheel of Fortune even a poisoned Gstaad looks like more life than she has. The reader-only layer narrows to the one thing Vee can't see: the **{{Fairytale}}** — that the glamour and the perfect man produce *nothing* in Randi. Vee sees the poisoned surface; only the reader sees the hollow center. (The *dressed-for-dinner* post is {{Fairytale}}'s dinner — Vee envies the emptiest evening of Randi's break.)

## The texts — Randi, reaching and sealed

- Vee holds only what **Outlier** gave her: the dad thing hit something real, and Randi covered it fast. Motivated by that (and by her own loneliness), she reaches out.
- **Constraint (Outlier canon):** Vee never reveals she saw the screen — *"didn't tell Randi, then or after, what she'd seen."* So the **sent text is oblique and deniable; the narration carries her knowledge** — but **pinned to the single Outlier observation**, no extrapolation into Randi's interior (that would be Vee seeing the machinery). Vee is *uncertain*, not insightful.
- **Randi seals the door with fairytale** — warm, real affection, no way in.
- **The *gorgeous* rule (verified against the drafts, 9 uses):** *gorgeous* is Randi's genuine-affection word for Vee — she spends it freely whenever she's warm, **including mid-operation** (*your gorgeous chest* while extracting in {{How It's Done}}) and while deflecting at a goodbye (*See you soon, gorgeous*). Here, wrapped inside the false *fairytale* gloss she performs in {{Fairytale}}, *gorgeous* is **the one true line that breaks through** — *"the one line she meant, …gorgeous 💋"* (`scenes/fairytale.md`). The affection is real even when the self-report around it is a lie. So the tell is **not** a withheld word — it is that everything *around* the *gorgeous* is performance while the endearment itself is the truth surfacing. She opens on a cheaper coin (*ANGEL*, the gloss) and lands on *gorgeous* (the truth she can't help spending). (Reread instrument, like *fine* is a reserved word around Pace — but here the reserved word marks the *truth*, not the lie.)
- **Vee takes the shine clean** — she believes it (Randi's cover is good; her own envy wants the glam real). The depth stays entirely reader-side; Vee doesn't even fully know she offered a door (the could-have-been foreclosed, reader-only).
- **Randi answers near-real-time** — same night, ~6h ahead and already into New Year's Day — warm and instant, yet it leaves Vee lonelier, not less. **Both aliveness-channels dark the same week:** Pace won't reach at all, and Randi — reachable, warm — still seals the door with fairytale; *the absence of the one and the closed door of the other*, in a device in her hands.
- Cruelest layer, never on the page: Vee is this good a friend to Randi (protecting her wound, asking nothing) at the exact time Randi runs the apparatus on her.

Tuned exchange (calibration reference, not locked):

> She'd typed it three times before she sent it. Never the thing itself — she'd never said the thing, not in the glass room when the name came up and the color went out of Randi's face, not after. Just: *thinking about you 💛 hope everything's good out there. here if you want to talk. or not.* She put the phone face-down, then turned it back over to be sure it had gone.
>
> *(the reply comes back almost at once — she's already six hours into tomorrow)*
>
> **Randi:** *you ANGEL 😇 it's honestly a fairytale out here — dad's got us in this insane chalet, mountains for days, i'm never coming home. miss your face most though, gorgeous 💋*
>
> So she was okay. Better than okay — a fairy tale, chalets and mountains, while Vee lay in her childhood bed under a shelf of swimming trophies. How fast it came, warm and instant and still a whole world away, sat in the gap like the distance it was.

## The jealousy — New Year's (the knife)

- **The without-a-name pole made flesh.** No status = no standing = he could be anything to anyone and she can't know or object. The namelessness is the void the jealousy grows in. *Did we break up? Does he even think we did?* — she replays {{Believe Me}} to determine her own relationship status and **cannot** (it ruptured into silence — no answer, no reunion — and there's been no contact since to read); leave it open.
- **Cooking = intimacy.** The one fact she holds: he cooked for Sheri at Thanksgiving ({{In His Hands}}, *"she's alone"*). His cooking is his care-with-his-hands, the same current as the sex (the plate before hunger). Defused in November (pity, not romance); **festers now** — the jealousy reaches back and poisons a thing she'd forgiven. Christmas is a **vacuum** (he never told her his plans); she supplies him at the stove, for someone, free now.
- **The rival is Vee's own negative.** She fills the Sheri-void with the prettiest of the CW dance girls (the blonde who taunted her — *all us girls love him*): blonde where Vee is **red**, tanned where she is **pale**, sleek and model-cut where she is **curvy**. She hands her own negation to Pace as his true appetite *now that he's free.* Not describing a rival — confessing her body-shame and calling it his desire. The shame arc's **fresh occasion** (body imagined as *insufficient* — a new riser, not a re-lit one).
- **The First Night hijack (the cruelest image).** She reaches for her fondest memory — First Night's afterglow: herself wrapped in his top sheet at the table, the wine she hadn't drunk, watching him build the fra diavolo (the oil, the garlic and pepper blooming, the tomatoes going in), the kitchen smelling right, her body quiet, the evening having used her well; the sheet slipping, unguarded, neither picking it up — and the jealousy **hijacks it in real time**: the figure in the sheet stops being her and becomes the blonde. She can't keep her own best night; the rival colonizes it while she watches. The thing she reached for to feel close to him is the thing that guts her. **Named nowhere; the reader remembers.** *Drafter: echo the sensory sequence — sheet, table, waiting wine, oil/garlic/pepper/tomatoes — never a label; the recognition does the work.*
- **The antidote wars and loses.** The bartender (*he doesn't take advantage; Pace don't bring dates; wasn't him they were jealous of, honey*) and Randi's *he doesn't sulk* push back — but they had a floor only while she was the exception and knew it. Unnamed, maybe broken up, the floor's gone; the antidote can't take. Unresolved.
- **Reader sees through.** The blonde is Vee's distortion. The reader knows Pace *made her a dress with his hands* to fit her body, praises her lushness on the bench, reads her *curvy-not-heavy* as the thing he wants more of, keeps her pale skin — he wants the precise reality she's decided he's escaping. She invents the anti-Vee and assigns it his love, unable to see how wanted she is. Same-week gap (reader-only): while she imagines a floozie tryst, the reality is **{{The Usual}}** — Pace at a diner counter with a lonely platonic friend, carrying her unanswered question.
- **Register guard: jealousy-in-the-flat** — bitter, circular, helpless (she can't act, can't claim standing, can't resolve). Body before mind: pictures, not forensics. **Keep Sheri a cipher** — the rival is invented, Sheri-named and CW-blonde-faced, which protects Sheri's real (safe) self for the spring disarm at Another Round. Vee never clocks that she's drawing her own negation.

## The closer (names them; scale left balanced)

The night has circled *who*, so the closer says it: **she'll have Randi back with the calendar** (school resumes, the brunches resume), and **Pace comes with nothing** — unscheduled, unpromised, unnamed. The wound as a scheduling asymmetry: a date to see the one, none with the other. *What about Pace?* **Leave the scale balanced** — if she resolves anything here, First Weekend Back has nothing to enact.

## Midnight & landing

- **Midnight carries both at once:** his strong arms around *her* (the longing) shadowed by the doubt of *whose* he's got them around tonight (the blonde). The wanted image and the feared image in the same beat, and no way to know which is real.
- **Landing:** exhausted, still reaching, down into restless sleep — diverging from {{Bare}}'s stay-awake grey window. The flatness takes even her vigil.
