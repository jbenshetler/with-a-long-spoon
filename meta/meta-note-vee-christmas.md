# Note — Vee — Christmas (the cost-weighing; the beige plant)

*Vignette · Unwritten · ~late December, Christmas break · Vee solo, at her parents' home (Ohio) · companion to the chronology's [VIGNETTE] Vee — Christmas · one panel of the break triptych (Flat Date / Sheri — Cheeseburgers)*

## What this scene is now

The one time the novel renders Vee away from school, and the place the book's bargain is first *weighed* in her interior. Sleepless in her childhood bed, she holds two intolerables and can set neither down: a life **without Pace** (this — the flat, safe, unseen home she has reverted into) and **Pace without a name** (the wound she carried home from the Christmas Fight, and his silence over the break). The scene **sets the scale; it does not tip it** — First Weekend Back tips it, where she trades pride for aliveness (`meta-note-first-weekend-back.md`). Here she only feels the weight.

It also **plants the beige-life thread** (registry: `meta-arch-bible.md`): the home is rendered as the concrete cost of the without-Pace life, so that at the very end — after the reveal — when she **chooses that same beige life knowingly**, the callback lands. The canon line *she is somewhere else without knowing she's left* is the seed the ending inverts: at Christmas she leaves without knowing; at the end she leaves knowing. Payoff lives in `meta-arch-vivienne.md` → The Cassie Scene.

## The callback to His Shirt (structural, not literal)

The shirt stays at school — **left on purpose**, a post-Christmas-Fight renunciation (*she doesn't need Pace or his shirt*): the same gesture that closed His Shirt (she took it off into the worse cold) now hardened into a resolution and carried 500 miles. So the callback is **the scent channel inverted.** His Shirt was saturated with *him* — the sawdust and the salt of the work; this room is saturated with *her mother* — fabric-softener sheets, plug-in lavender in every outlet, *like an old lady* — and there is no Pace-scent in the field at all. Same sensory register (smell, sleepless, in bed), opposite content. **Do not name the parallel;** let the room's smell simply be there, before she misses him.

Guard: **do not re-use the shirt object.** The rhyme is the bed, the sleeplessness, the empty reach — not a second shirt beat.

## The two poles (the scale, embodied — never a ledger)

- **Without Pace = the home tableau, made concrete.** The beige food that was *never enough* (Ohio casserole/potato/meat — love that isn't the thing she wants now); the couch and Wheel of Fortune with her parents; the childhood bed; Dad's Corolla in the drive. Safe, small, **unseen** — her parents love *Vivienne*, the daughter-role, not the her that Pace and Randi see. This is what a life without him looks like, rendered as a place.
- **Pace without a name = the leak.** His silence over the break; the fume that he hasn't reached out (the anger is the renunciation failing — she wouldn't be furious if she didn't need him). She reaches for Randi's CW-Dance-Debrief line — *he doesn't sulk, he leaves you the room* — to re-arm the decision (*it's not rejection, it's how he is*). What she misses is **the attention** — being *seen*, the plate set down before she said she was hungry — not the explicit heat (this is a flat, restful register; a lush sex-memory would break it).

The **sideways leak** is the method: she thinks she's just restless, homesick-in-reverse, and cannot feel that the flatness *is* the weighing. Body before mind; she oscillates, she never tabulates.

## The Gstaad postcard (reader-hollow; father as texture, not axis)

Vee knows — Randi told her, happily, before Outlier — that Randi's father took her skiing in **Gstaad** over break. At 3 a.m. Vee envies the postcard: the Alps, the palace hotel, the glamorous crowd she can picture without trying. **The reader holds the Flat Date and knows the postcard is empty** — Randi is inside that exact glamour feeling *nothing*, grieving a father whose money she now knows is dirty. **Vee envies Randi's nothing.** Two women flat in the same week, each certain the other's world is the full one: the book's half-seeing engine, run on the principal axis (Vee↔Randi), never the fathers.

Guards:
- **Envy, not detection.** Vee imagines the glamour; she does not read Randi's slow replies for clues, and nothing signals Randi is off.
- **Randi stays innocent on first read** — a rich girl on holiday, slow to answer. The *twelve-hours-later* texts get their innocent cover from the time zone (~6 hours ahead: Vee texts into her sleepless night, Randi answers half a day on). The cold is entirely reader-supplied.
- **The father is texture, not a mirror.** Dad's Corolla and the couch are the home's furniture, not a deliberate rhyme against Randi's father. Keep the two dads off the structural axis.

## The closer (names them; scale left balanced)

The night has circled *who*, so the closer says it: **she'll have Randi back with the calendar** (school resumes, the brunches resume), and **Pace comes with nothing** — unscheduled, unpromised, unnamed. The wound rendered as a scheduling asymmetry: a date to see the one, none with the other. *What about Pace?* **Leave the scale balanced** — if she resolves anything here, First Weekend Back has nothing left to enact.

## Continuity

- **Outlier now sits before Christmas** (author's branch): Randi already knows about her father in Gstaad — but Vee's last read of Randi-re-Gstaad is the *happy*, pre-Outlier telling, and Randi clamps (the *Cheating bastards* cover), so **Vee carries none of Randi's grief.** Keep her imagined-Randi the pure postcard. (Reconcile the stale "spring" Outlier reference in `meta-arch-randi.md` when merging.)
- **Not shame.** The disloyalty of finding home no longer enough is the ordinary ache of growing away, not shame (shame needs a witness; there is none here). Do not frame it as shame.
- **The beige objects are recallable seeds.** The ending reaches back for them (lavender, Wheel of Fortune, the never-enough food, the childhood bed). Choose them for retrieval.

## Beat sheet

- Sleepless in the childhood bed; the lavender / fabric-softener field (His Shirt's scent channel, inverted).
- The safe, unseen home leaking a longing she can't place: the never-enough food, the couch and Wheel of Fortune, the reversion to the daughter-role. The faint disloyal ache of growing away.
- The unanswerable questions — mother's *how's your boyfriend*, father's *when do I get to meet him* — both landing on the same locked door she couldn't open at the Christmas Fight.
- The fume: he hasn't reached out. The anger that gives away the failing renunciation; Randi's *he doesn't sulk* reached for as re-arming.
- What she misses: the attention, being seen; the Gstaad postcard she envies (reader-hollow).
- The slow twelve-hours-later texts; not the same.
- Closer: Randi returns with the calendar, Pace with nothing. *What about Pace?* — scale balanced.
