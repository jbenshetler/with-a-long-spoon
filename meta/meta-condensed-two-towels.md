# Condensed — Two Towels

*Scene · Draft complete · `two-towels.md` · date owned by chronology*

## Summary

Vee's first visit to Pace's house — a small post-war house at the top of a gravel road. He's turned the heat up hours early so she won't be cold. She asks for the whole tour, and the house reveals him room by room: the squared shoes, the working (not showpiece) kitchen, the plain bathroom with its two towels, the office dense with equations, the one closed door (his bedroom) he leaves closed, the handmade oak furniture and the patent behind it. The tour ends at the glassed-in sun porch, where a bolt of burgundy silk waits on the table — matched to *her* coloring, not the magazine shade she'd named on the hike. The no-tag shirt detonates: he made it. He offers to make her the induction dress; to do it right, he'd measure her first, in good light. She says yes. The fitting itself is **{{A Round}}** (`a-round.md`).

## Plants / Pays Off

- **The two towels** (bathroom, zero-dwell in the tour): innocuous domestic texture on first read; on reread, whose is the second? (the buried Randi thread). Title-bearing — the prose gives it no dwell so the cold read stays banked for reread.
- **The no-tag hiking shirt (from {{Leave No Trace}}) detonates here**: She noticed its absence once, guessed wrong (counterfeit, sample, off-brand), dropped the question. Now the silk — deliberately matched with care — makes the shirt's unlabeled quality a statement. Careful anonymity and careful personalization as twin forms of devotion.
- **The hike's silk-naming conversation**: The color she named — brick-red, from a magazine, the dress she couldn't afford — at the summit, unguarded. Pace's burgundy is his deeper variant, *not* the shade she named: he honors the want-to-be-beautiful beneath the color rather than matching the reference. He acts on the being-known-without-asking.
