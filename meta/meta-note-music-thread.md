# Notes — Music Thread (a style per character)

*Companion craft note. A cross-scene resonance: the house sounds like whichever woman the scene belongs to. Each character has a musical style; the reader assembles the pattern; it is never named on the page.*

---

## What it's for

Pace's house has a sound, and the sound keys the scene's dominant woman. Vee's scenes sound like **Vee**; Randi's scenes sound like **Randi**; when Pace is only himself, the house sounds like **Pace**. Across the book this becomes the most literal statement of the engine — *the same man, two tailored worlds, neither woman knowing the other's exists.* When Vee feels most known (the house sounds exactly like her), she is in fact most handled, because the house does this for Randi too. That turn is available only on reread, only in the pattern.

The aesthetic labels are the **key, not the surface**: Vee is Art Nouveau (organic, sinuous, sensuous, warm — the vine); Randi is Art Deco (geometric, streamlined, machine-age, glossy — the chrome). The labels never appear; only the music does, and only as ambient room-texture.

## The three lanes

Design movements do not map cleanly onto music; only one analogue is tight (Impressionism ≈ Art Nouveau). The other lanes are matched by **sensibility**, and each lane is internally coherent and mutually distinct on one axis: **Vee = nature/feeling · Pace = craft/aliveness · Randi = machine/control.**

- **Pace (himself)** — **jazz, all eras (~1920s–1960s+).** Ellington, Basie, Holiday, Monk, Miles, Coltrane, Bill Evans. Acoustic, improvised, alive — the sonic cousin of his joinery (jazz is, in ethos, the most "Arts & Crafts" music there is: hand-made, in-the-moment, anti-mechanical). Jazz belongs **entirely to Pace** — this is what clears Randi's Deco of the Jazz-Age collision.
- **Vee (Art Nouveau)** — Impressionist/late-Romantic composers (**Debussy, Ravel, Fauré, Grieg, Satie**) · 60s–70s warm soft rock (**Fleetwood Mac, Christopher Cross**) · 90s country (**Garth Brooks, Trisha Yearwood**). Organic, flowing, sincere, romantic, analog.
- **Randi (Art Deco)** — glossy/engineered, **no jazz, no country**: **Depeche Mode**, 80s synth (New Order, Yaz, Tears for Fears), **Steely Dan**. Cool, produced, sealed, dark-under-gloss. (Steely Dan carries jazz *harmony* but reads as machine-perfect studio rock; the line that keeps it clear of Pace's lane is **acoustic-improvised-alive (Pace) vs. produced-sealed-glossy (Randi).**)

**Debussy is Vee's** (it is Impressionist/Nouveau), which supersedes the old cabin-note line "*not Debussy, already Pace's*" — Pace's-own is now **jazz**. This reassignment is what makes the drafted `ignition-scalding.md` Debussy correct as-is (see below).

## The assignment principle

**Pace is not curating.** He does not select a record for a guest; he plays what he likes, and he likes all of it. The per-woman correlation is the **author's key to the reader**, realized diegetically as *ambient music that happens to be on* — never caused by, and never tracking, whom Pace expects.

So do **not** hunt for a diegetic rule ("he knew Randi was coming → Randi's music"); it does not hold. Key each scene by **whose scene/evening this is**:

- Randi's scene → Randi's sound.
- Vee's scene/evening → Vee's sound (even if Randi arrives into it — the sound *stays* Vee's; that is the poaching).
- Pace alone/himself → jazz.

This keeps Pace innocent (no calculation to expose) and puts all the pattern-work on the reader.

**How it lives — the playlist.** The music runs on Pace's playlists, which roll over on their own; he does not put a record on for a guest. This is the diegetic cover for the key: what's on is just what came up. When it matters, he *registers a switch* — one album ending, the next coming up — which lets a scene mark its sound as an event (see {{In Her Place}}) without Pace ever choosing it.

## Current placements (the corpus as it stands)

Story order of the three Randi-at-house scenes: **{{The Bench}} (early fall) → {{In Her Place}} (April) → Ignition-Scalding (June).**

- **{{The Bench}}** (`the-bench.md`, drafted) → **Steely Dan** (sourced from *A Decade of Steely Dan* — the '72–'80 run is seamless enough to read as one body; on the page just "Steely Dan," ambient). The reader's *first* taste of Randi's lane, so smooth and cool; keep it reading produced, never warm (or it bleeds toward Vee's Fleetwood-Mac register).
- **{{In Her Place}}** (`in-her-place.md`, drafted) → **Depeche Mode's *Music for the Masses*.** Colder/harder, matched to the usurpation — its sleek cold synth is the machine sound, and it carries the scene's own weather (*Strangelove*, *I Want You Now*, *Behind the Wheel*). (*Songs of Faith and Devotion* is the band's raw/warm grunge-gospel outlier — off-brand for the machine coding — and its title would telegraph the betrayal; rejected.) The playlist **rolls over into it while he waits** — the house going over to Randi before she's through the door — and he listens *past* the music for Vee's heels (heater cut; the stereo is the floor now). Pace made the bed with **green sheets for Vee**: the scene runs *green sheets under cold synth* — **visual Vee, sonic Randi** — latent, unremarked; sharpens the title (the room becomes *her* place) rather than betraying it.
- **Ignition-Scalding** (`ignition-scalding.md`, drafted) → **leave the Debussy.** It is a **Vee-then-Randi evening** — Vee is there until 11, Randi arrives at midnight into Vee's scent; the drafted line has Pace *"leave it"* on. Vee's music is correct for her evening and *stays* running through Randi's arrival: the **poaching beat.** It lands **last** (June), after the rule is established, so it reads as meaningful, not as noise. No edit.

**In `All the Time`** (the ~mid-Nov SATC brunch, `meta-note-all-the-time.md`): only the **Vee side** can be planted, and only as Vee's **report** — she describes the music of his evenings (Impressionist warmth on low while he cooks; a Fleetwood Mac record; a Garth song) as part of the tenor that winds her tighter. Optional **Randi wordless beat**: Vee glows about "her" music while Randi has sat in that same house hearing something else — a flicker, unreadable, the plan escaping her. Never tips.

## Guards

- **Never named, by anyone** — no character thought, no narrator line, no aesthetic label. Vee just loves what's on; Randi just moves through a house that sounds like her world. The reader assembles it across scenes. (`Never name, never explain.`)
- **Within any one scene it reads as attention / hospitality / his taste — never calculation.** The cold is the cross-scene pattern *only*. Never spotlight Pace *choosing per woman*; that makes him a puppeteer and kills *tenderness real AND instrumental, never resolved.* Music goes in **ambient**, low, unremarked — a detail of the room, not a decision.
- **Intent stays ambiguous, lean intuitive.** Consistent with *catches every signal, assembles none* — if any drift toward a guest's register reads at all, it reads as temperament, not strategy. Never resolve whether he knows.
- **POV carries the reveal.** Vee-POV scenes carry Vee's sound (she feels known); Pace-POV Randi-scenes carry Randi's. The reader hears both; the characters never do. Fits the book's POV architecture (Pace POV arms the irony).
- **The discipline — hold until scene #4.** With the current corpus you have **two clean Randi legs** (Bench, {{In Her Place}}) plus the huge contrast with every Vee-at-house scene; Ignition-Scalding's Debussy is the **one** exception, and it's earned by coming last and by being structurally forced (Vee was there). Keep the Bench and {{In Her Place}} *unambiguously* Randi — no cleverness — and **add no further Vee-music-in-a-Randi-scene exceptions until a fourth clean Randi-at-house scene exists.** You don't build subtleties on a two-instance base.
