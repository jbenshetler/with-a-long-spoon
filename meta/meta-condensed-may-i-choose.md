# Condensed — May I Choose

*SCENE · Draft complete · `may-i-choose.md` · keep to a half-scene in weight even though the interior runs deep; the restraint is in the plot (one dinner, one walk, one kiss), not the prose · ~a few days after the froyo walk*

## Key Beats

A Middle Eastern restaurant a few blocks off campus (small, family-run, the older careworn owner Christine knows him). The chivalric grammar runs in deeds — he comes to collect her, the considered order, the *may* (**"May I choose?"**, prompted by her floundering at the menu, never a host's routine). Vee gets courted in a register her mother would approve. 

**The scene's real, discovered job is the *first surrender*:** safety assembled brick by brick (he doesn't make her account for herself; doesn't perform; doesn't judge her hunger; carries the conversation) until her lifelong self-monitoring goes quiet and she goes *under* — rendered as symptom, never named (subspace by another name). The structure is a graduated yield: small (handing him the *choice*) → fed and safe → the governor lifting (she eats unmonitored, "didn't notice she'd stopped") → the **drip** (his oblivious palm-lick, the trigger) → losing the conversational thread *and not catching it the way she always does* → the dreamy under-state → the **mint tea** surfacing her gently, partial, the softness carried out. 

**Appetite arc:** she arrives hollow (nervous prep crowded out lunch), the hunger "wider than it had any right to be," "empty a lot longer than since noon" (the deniable lifelong-under-feeding reach). 

**Hand-motif payoff:** she watches his hands all night (tearing bread "with care for the heat"; the right-hand pattern; the drip) → he takes her hand for the first time on the walk back ("for want of his exactly") → the taut hand at the tree. 

**The kiss is the surrender's second occurrence, *elected*:** she states her mother's-register rule (*"I don't kiss a man I've known a few days,"* a rule "never breached before"), he declines to capitalize (gives her the out, walks them on), and *she* crosses against her own rule — **"But… I'm making an exception,"** the breach-verb echoing the dinner's surrender ("let the thread go"). First-read clean on Pace throughout (player-guarded hard: he's known here for the *food* and the old friend, never a man who *takes women* here); the apparatus — safety engineered to produce her surrender — is reread-only.
