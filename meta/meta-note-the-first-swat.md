# Note — The First Swat / The Setup

*Companion to the unwritten scene: beat structure and unrendered craft. The arrival / height-inversion beat, the canonical "leave them on" line, and the never-named rhyme/charge live in [[meta-condensed-the-first-swat]] and are not restated here.*

**POV:** close third on Vee throughout. Pace's reading of the borrowed shoes is shown only externally (his glance, his line); the reader decodes it, the narrator never does. **Heat-by-kind:** Movement I raw/tender; Movement II playful-discovering.

## Movement I — the apology (evening)

Engine: **her plan fails early so the real thing can surface** — the same shape as the swat next morning (managed Vee arrives prepared; something gets under it). Three layers of armor on arrival: Randi's "show up sure, not sorry" coaching, a rehearsed script, and the Humble Pie (store-bought, printed-sticker label). She is *certain she was wrong*, terrified only of how he will take it.

- **Arrival / height-inversion / the line:** see condensed. She changes into Randi's borrowed heels in the car, not on the porch.
- **The shoe-glance (Vee-only render).** Already off-balance looking down at him, she catches his eyes drop to her heels and reads it as a fresh disrespect: she came to apologize for disrespecting him and now stands over him in borrowed heels. Horror at the recursion. Render only the misread and the horror; the reader understands. (Reread-locked layer: Randi's jealousy. The procurement is already reader-known and can breathe; only the wound stays off the page. "The loan" in the canonical line carries the shoe reread for free.)
- **He takes the pie and steadies her** — the dish removed, not offered (she is too far into the fumble), his free hand bringing her upright. Disarmed before the real apology: pie gone, body steadied, frame reversed. Tender and instrumental at once, never tipped.
- **The polished WHAT.** The rehearsed apology, delivered clean and graceful: she was angry, disrespectful, he gave her nothing but kindness. Not reassurance-seeking — the bartender corrected her at the dance; she already knows the blonde lied.
- **Pace's question pulls the unplanned WHY:** *what happened, everything seemed fine, I went to get drinks and came back to her angry.* Triple duty, none named: genuine non-comprehension (the never-named neurodivergence), tenderness, extraction — the same engine Randi runs with words.
- **The crack.** No script for *why*; she breaks register into raw speech and retells the blonde. Confession clause: *I knew exactly what she was doing, and it got to me anyway.* Jealousy never named; that clause is the iceberg (how far gone she already is).
  - **Retelling fidelity:** barbs verbatim (two days of replay burn the exact wording in, especially the deniable euphemisms), sequence jumbled (she circles, repeats, cannot get it linear — reliving, not reciting). Keeps the WHY raw against the polished WHAT and reads as breakdown, not transcription. Source lines: the "gets a girl home safe" euphemism in [[cw-dance]]; pull verbatim at draft.
- **He takes it.** Received, not glibly absolved. The relief pre-loads the morning's brattiness.
- Fade. She stays the night.

## Movement II — the swat (morning)

Pace is an early riser; Vee sleeps in, two sleepless nights repaid at once. Wardrobe: his oversized white tee left out for her, the panties she wore in. No robe — it would armor the surface the return swat needs live. The brattiness reads as endearing because it is **relief discharging** now that the apology landed.

- Surfaces alone, gropes for him, cold sheet. Swims into his tee, goes looking.
- Finds him at the sink washing last night's dishes, sleeves shoved up. The running tap is the meter of his attention: live while he half-resists her, off when he commits.
- **Wordless cling** from behind — dead weight, forehead between his shoulder blades, a groggy groan.
- **The soaking runner** (her wit as armor, same instrument as the Humble Pie):
  1. "Let them soak." She is asking him to *grant* it (unnamed permission rhyme).
  2. The doctrine arrives with mock-rigor (a recognized method, the soak does the work, he is undermining the soak), pressed with sleepy kisses up his neck and her hands coming round to rub him through the shirt, working to excite him off the dishes. Words and hands, both instruments of persuasion.
  3. Compressed to its title: "Soaking, Pace." She grabs the wet arm and pulls.
- **The tickle** — wet hand under her arm, cold on warm sleep-skin, an involuntary jolt; "No — no —", doubling, twisting away.
- **Her swat = the invitation:** "No tickling!" and one swat to his backside, mock-stern, still laughing.
- **Tap off.** He shuts the water; the kitchen goes quiet; she feels his attention land fully.
- **She steps into the return.** Twisting to protect herself she dodges the wrong way and her own motion swings her into his hand. The swat lands damp and sharp on bare skin where the tee has ridden up. Wordless. **She authored it.**
- **The switch flips.** A faraway look, her mouth forming a soundless *oh.* The runner dies here: words were her armor all morning and the one wordless thing he does she cannot answer with a bit. Body before mind, unnamed. Inaugural **spanking-proof** indignity — her wanting shown, undeniable, real-time, witnessed.
- **She initiates on his jeans.** Presses in, kisses hard, hands to the buttons, slightly clumsy — covering the *oh*, not a practiced seductress. She reaches to undress the dressed man, inverting the Fitting.
- **Wordless button:** the pan left under the gray water as it moves elsewhere. The dishes soak after all. Reader supplies it; she says nothing.

- Shame staircase: this is the *first* of the spanking-proof class. Keep it the lightest riser; do not pre-spend later indignities.
- The runner breaking under the swat is the scene in miniature (words are her armor; his body gets under them). Never named.
