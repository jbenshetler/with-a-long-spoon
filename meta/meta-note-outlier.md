# Craft Note — The Outlier

*Companion to `meta-condensed-outlier.md`. Diptych, one file. Close third on Vee.*

## POV razor (the one thing that breaks the book if missed)
Vee's read must top out at *my friend is hurt by something her father did, that she had no hand in.* That reading is **true and shallow.** The instant her narration rises to *what Randi really is* — operator, the daughter who is her father — the engine collapses (Vee must never see the hunted woman; see `meta-plan-satc-tracks.md` DOs/DON'Ts and `meta-arch-randi.md` 🔒 THE NEED ENGINE). The operator dimension is **reader-only**, carried by the gap. Vee registers the *fact* (the name) and chooses kindness; she does not interpret Randi's character.

## The gaze (keystone + POV mechanism)
Randi's interior is withheld, so the only camera on her reaction is **Vee turning from the screen (the data) to Randi (the person).** This is both characteristic (Vee attends people; Cassie attends data — the novel's axis in miniature) and the device that lets the reader see Randi's face. Vee **keeps looking** (staying with her friend, witnessing without intruding), and **Randi never registers it** — her radar is down because the wound is real (the guard-down state is the measure, cf. the silence with Pace). Even a caught glance would read only as concern, never as *Vee decoded the name* — the recognition stays sealed.

## The hinge line
*"Cheating bastards"* — hold it to the two words, no clause. It carries four things at once (real conclusion / cover that avoids *my father* / forced agreement with Cassie / a daughter condemning the man she adores) **because** it doesn't explain itself. On first read it's generic PPP outrage and passes clean; on reread it's a girl denouncing her own father to keep from crying.

## Cassie — the true Cassandra
Physically blind to the screen (seating: laptop–Randi–Vee–Cassie), she speaks the *general* truth and doesn't know it lands on the woman beside her. The diffuseness is the power, not a weakness — dramatic irony, not a lecture. Cool certainty, never a crow (she never says *I told you so*). Keep any Randi/Cassie difference **intellectual, in the data**, never personal heat — a visible animus would tip the reader that Randi is the antagonist.

## MIRH
Auto-truncated label from **Randi's own** visualization (an LLC has no ticker) — her labeling convention renders her name as the villain-tag, and she recognizes it because she built it. Recognition arrives before she can manage it; she clicks anyway (compulsion / the assignment-motion finishing itself).

## "She didn't know"
She knows *Miranda Holdings* is her father's (named for her); she did **not** know it was the county's worst PPP-to-layoffs offender. The not-knowing is canon; only the target moves. Her advocacy (prove PPP saved jobs) is what drives her own hand to the dot that guts her — marketing's faith in the persuasive picture, turned on her.

## Register / discipline
Earn-the-dark-by-being-light: panel one buoyant, panel two genuinely warm (collaborative pleasure) before the drop. Underwrite the flinch and the sick — small, bodily, deniable; the restraint is the power. No editorializing, no telegraphing; the closing outlier-image carries the reader-ahead, not the narrator. Diptych rhymes with the *See You Later* diptych (stats-class panel → venue panel, each carrying a Cassie beat): that one buried her withdrawn warning, this one delivers her unwitting true verdict.

## Evidence, kept light
The documentary mechanics (PPP loan certifications against the layoffs — WARN-notice territory) stay gestural; this is a character/relationship scene, not a data procedural. Enough that the crime is clear; never a forensic dwell.
