## Why the scene exists (the spine)

The fall gave Randi and Pace no on-page meeting on purpose: fall stays pure Vee–Pace so the reader falls for Pace as hard as Vee does, and the dread rides on knowledge, not reminders (the Bench already meets Pace inside the configuration). Their machinery surfaces in **spring**, where the reader is bonded and the book is tilting dark. This is the spring Randi–Pace self-subjugation scene — and it does the one thing the Randi–Vee scenes structurally *cannot*: **render Randi's escalating obsession with, and usurpation of, Vee, from Pace's POV.** (In the Vee-POV confidante scenes her want can only appear as tells Vee misreads.)

The image is the whole book in one frame: **Randi takes the exact place Vee planned for herself.** But she is *seizing* here, not yet wrecked — an aggressive grab, still fighting for control. `scenes/ignition-scalding.md` (summer) is the **collapse**. Grab → collapse is the ordering; on the abjection axis this is *lesser* than Ignition (guard: don't let its recklessness read as *bigger* than Ignition).

## Setup — how Randi gets in (no forgery)

- **Green sheets, established earlier.** Vee gifts Pace green sheets in a prior scene (NEW, unplaced — see Placement): her **interior** color (per `meta-condensed-famished.md`, green is hers/interior, burgundy his/exterior — do not conflate) carried into his most private room. Not "matches her red hair," though it does — it's Vee claiming the bed as jointly hers, one rung past the cabin key.
- **Pace has been switching bedding — green for Vee, plain white for Randi.** Render as plain behavior, never named: ritual compartmentalization, a Pace-precision tell, and a **reread-detonator** (like the scrunchies — another sealed compartment the reader opens later).
- **Vee sets the stage herself.** At the Secret Plans brunch (Vee POV, `meta-note-secret-plans.md`), Vee confides tonight's plan — sneak in, surprise him — including that she has **already texted Pace** (her own, genuine text, sent before the mimosas): *wait, don't come to the door, I'll call you in, I have a surprise.* Then she drinks too many mimosas. **The reader loses Vee before the brunch ends** (let the POV go dark with her consciousness).
- **Randi does NOT get Vee drunk** — Vee overdid it herself. Randi drives drunk Vee safely home. On first read this is **pure care** (the responsible friend); on reread it is the enabling move. Keep it pure-warm — no signal it is calculated. Vee out cold; Randi knows the night is safe.
- **Randi drives out to Pace's** — the deliberate detour into the isolated dark (the plan escaping her; the effort is the tell). She rides in on the stage Vee built: no forged text, nothing staged — **Vee's own loving gesture is the trapdoor** (the good thing and the harm are the same act — more on-theme than any Randi forgery, and it keeps her hands clean on the page). Pace, per Vee's real text, stays put and waits, expecting Vee. Randi **throws the deadbolt behind her** (her signature; Vee, with a key, wouldn't) — a faint early tremor before Pace ever sees her.

## The scene (Pace's POV)

- **The opening — the bed, two texts, the wait.** Pace's POV opens on **text 1** (Vee: *coming this afternoon*): he strips the white he sleeps in and makes the bed up in the **green sheets Vee gave him** — the switching as pure behavior, never named (reread-detonator; his tenderness for Vee). **Text 2**, from the brunch (*don't come out when you hear me — wait till I call, I have a surprise*), keeps him from the door and sets the ambush. Both texts are Vee's genuine love → the rails Randi rides in on.
- **The ambush / substitution.** Randi arrives, **throws the deadbolt** (an unnamed snag — *she never bolts* is Vee-knowledge; the preserved *she always locks it* line relocates here, surfacing **after** the reveal), goes to the green bedroom, and gives a **wordless summons** Pace takes for Vee's call. He comes expecting naked-Vee **on the green** and finds Randi in Vee's exact planned position. **No identity deception:** he sees her on entry — informed throughout; consent his; thesis intact.
- **The green-wound arrives by touch, not a discovery-reaction.** Randi's first encounter with the green is off-page (she's in the bed before he enters); *seizing*, controlled, she shows nothing. The wound surfaces later **inside the comparison-fishing** — her hand on the green she's never been given while she begs to be told she's lesser, Pace watching. Body before mind; external render (containers); the sheets are the answer to her own question.
- **The bounded frame → the expansion.** Randi says Vee is delayed (out cold — but she gives him a bounded window: *an hour, gone before she comes*). The bounded promise is his **permission frame**; the tension is his **libido vs. his care for Vee / fear of discovery**. Randi **won't be hurried** — she imposes the slow pace against his urge to be quick and safe, expanding the bounded thing he consented to (consent-to-X becoming participation-in-Y, the book's engine turned *softly* toward Pace; he stays knowing, smart, sympathetic — his libido, once engaged, won't enforce the boundary he set).
- **The self-subjugation engine (preserved from the draft — the comparison-fishing).** On top, Randi fishes: *how good is it with Vee… is she better… tell me she's better than me* — volunteering the humiliation, arousal climbing on it. **In the new frame this does double duty:** it is also **the needle in the clock** — she keeps Vee *named* and present, working Pace's care against his hurry, not letting him forget "Vee is coming." Same dialogue, new charge.
- **Pace's move — the gift-conversion (a recurring signature).** He won't take the rank-down (*"I'm here. With you. Now."*), then reads her and **hands her the thing she can't say herself:** *"She's perfect."* — beat — *"For your first woman."* The frame stays **on** — Randi never declares the want; Pace wraps it (the your/our slippage the deniable margin). This is his constancy, the same gift he gives again at Ignition (about her breasts) — **the move is meant to recur**; what escalates between the two scenes is *Randi* (see Control axis), not Pace. The *same* kindness landing on a *seizing* Randi here vs. a *wrecked* Randi at Ignition is what makes her change legible. Render the gift **genuine and unremarked** (no halo); the instrumentality lives in the pattern, on reread — tenderness real AND instrumental, unresolved.
- **The uncovered orgasm (preserved, re-motivated).** Her want arrives uncovered and takes the plan out of his hands, pulling him over early. **RE-MOTIVATE:** in the draft he'd "meant to have the whole afternoon… to draw it long"; here he meant to be *quick and safe* (get her gone before Vee) — what her uncovered want undoes is his **control and his hurry**, not a long afternoon. The image survives; the stated intent flips.
- **The end (aftercare → after-telling → "secret plans too" — may be substantial, not a light add).**
  - Aftercare preserved (blanket, smoothing her hair) — minus the fall silk imagery.
  - **The after-telling:** Randi reveals Vee's out cold — that she drove her home, that there was never a real clock. This is how the reader learns (the reader lost Vee at brunch) AND it reveals to Pace that she **manufactured the deadline and let him sweat** — her control surfacing, the partnership fraying toward Ignition. Render as **Pace's dawning realization** (her dialogue is fine; her interior is not).
  - **"Secret plans too":** in the afterglow Randi turns the phrase back on Pace (absorbs the joke from `meta-note-secret-plans.md`) — jealousy-leak wrapped in wit, the orchestrator's confession to the one man who can't decode it. Double register (warm/witty first, cold second, **no word changing**); body-tell only; never explained. Consequence accepted: **Randi's wound-ignition is pulled earlier** than the old "small deniable leak" plan.

## Randi's want — the control axis (April vs. Ignition)

The escalation from this scene to Ignition is **control, not explicitness.** Here Randi's want is the most articulated the book has given it — but it stays **wrapped and managed** (she *chooses* to voice it, through Pace's gift-frame; she is seizing, in command of the frame). Randi structurally cannot voice an *unmanaged* want — the frame is load-bearing to her character, and "the frame hollows the real thing." The **uncovered, unmanaged slip** (*"I can't wait to taste her"*, the want escaping ahead of her plan) is **reserved for Ignition**. Keep the frame *on* here; reserve the frame coming *off* for summer. Do not pre-spend the taste-thread slip.

## Guards (craft rules in play)

- **Earn the dark by being light.** The drive-home especially must read as genuine care; cut any signal Randi is calculating. The cold is pattern-and-reread only.
- **Randi's hands stay clean on the page.** No forged text, no phone theft, nothing staged — her deception is passive and deniable (she rides Vee's real arrangement). **Rejected** (breaks her double register into legible villainy, and turns the book's *structural* betrayal into *personal* malice): staging Randi typing/deleting a message as Vee.
- **Thesis intact — Pace is never deceived into sex.** He sees it's Randi; he is informed. The consent-deception hole runs Pace→Vee and stays *Pace's* — he is its agent, never its victim (`meta-thesis.md`, `meta-arch-pace.md`). Only permitted echo toward Pace: the soft frame-expansion (the hour).
- **Containers rule (flag 15).** Pace's POV; Randi's interior never opens in his presence; realizations rendered externally, tells only.
- **Pace's tenderness real AND instrumental, unresolved.** Tonight Randi takes Vee's place **on the green** (the bed dressed for Vee) — the white is only the plant, what she normally gets. His tenderness for Vee (the bed made in her color) and his having of Randi on it are true at once, never resolved. The gift-conversion is rendered unremarked; its instrumentality is reread-only.
- **Heat by kind, not intensity.** A new kind — **seduction-under-threat / substitution** — distinct from Ignition's scalding abjection.
- **Vary the execution vs. Ignition.** Same *behavior* (the gift-conversion), new *occasion* — don't copy-paste the "She's perfect. / beat / For your X" cadence; give this instance its own staging (the shame-staircase rule: find the next occasion, don't re-light the last at the same intensity).
