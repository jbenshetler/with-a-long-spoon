# Condensed — School Nights

*VIGNETTE · Draft complete (`scenes/school-nights.md`) · ~mid November, the week between The Top Drawer's two weekends (after the mirror, before the drawer) · Vee POV · short, ~½ page*

The overnights that thicken the rhythm happen **off-page**; this is their only trace — the **greedy phase still running**, weeks in, when they still can't keep to weekends (it doesn't bank into the *regular pattern* until The New Ordinary, mid-to-late November — guideline, not guarantee). Vee relays the accumulating nights to Cassie in a warm, romantic register — the proud/sweet version — while the bodily shame stays walled off (it goes to Randi). The relay does what dramatizing the overnights would not: it keeps the off-page rhythm visible without spending scenes on it, *and* it shows the confidante-sort operating in miniature. It is also the one natural place Vee says, in words, what makes Pace different. Dorm room, Vawter Hall; the cold-window detail and shared-suite life already canon.

Intercalary in the Top Drawer diptych: she's just off the reunion weekend (the bed, the mirror, the *did he mind?* she's still carrying) and the wordless answer — the drawer — is still a weekend away. The naming to Cassie sits between the love she felt without words and the deed that will confirm it.

### Beat one — the warm telling
Vee comes in from another night out, lit up, and gives Cassie the sweet ledger: they keep ending up at his place. The version that asserts her own happiness in it — agency and romance, the register Cassie can hold. (She's also just weathered a week apart — his conference travel, her own brutal week on campus — so the wanting is fresh and loud.) Nothing edged; the wall around the appetite/shame is kind, not cold (the narrowing of what you share with someone who can't follow you there) — but it isn't airtight: a tender deed she's proud to relay carries the body in unguarded (the burn treated gently with not enough on; the warmth kept after), the leak riding the channel she isn't watching. This is the same sort that began in **Toenails** — the romance offered, the shame withheld — now running smooth.

### Beat two — what Cassie already knew
Cassie's dry, affectionate read: *"You don't come home school nights."* The friend who tracks the beloved's absence without judgment. Warm on first read — she's glad for her. On reread it's **Pace's feeling overflowing the weekend plan** (weeknights were never Randi's to lose) and it is *Cassie*, the one who notices Vee receding from her own life, who does the counting. The Cassandra seeing clearly, here only warmly.

### Beat three — why this one
Cassie's *why are you so gone over this one?* is the open door, and the only natural occasion for the comparison. Vee answers in **deeds, not thesis** — the men before were *fine*; Pace is not fine. Then Cassie asks it straight, *do you love him?*, and the talker goes small: *"Maybe. I think so. It's all so fast."* Not silence (silence would read as duress, which this isn't — Vee retreats to small words under strain, and she's nowhere near it) and not a declaration; the giddy too-soon hedge, cold on reread against the plain *she loved him* she'd already owned to herself at The Top Drawer's bed. The naming stays interior — the one place it's asked aloud, the word she'd freely think she won't quite hand over. Keep the deeds specific and embodied (what he does, how he is), never an abstract account of why he's better. (Prior-men canon checked: *fine men, fine pleasures, none formative* — the cracker-and-meal architecture in [[meta-arch-vivienne]]; no names, no inventory.)

### The double register (canonical)
First read is pure warmth, no signal — a roommate clocking her friend's happiness. The cold lives only in the pattern: that the weeknights are going to Pace, that Cassie is the one keeping count. **Cassandra device never named, never explained, never commented on — including by Cassie.** Cut any line that makes Cassie *suspicious* rather than simply *seeing*.

### The count (guard)
Prefer **rhythm phrasing** over a hard tally — Vee conveys frequency, Cassie's *"school nights"* does the counting — so there's no ledger to square. Scope any number to the casual gap-nights of early-to-mid November (the conference week excepted), and let Cassie's line carry it.

### What it installs
For the arc: the still-running greedy rhythm that keeps the relationship's daily-life intensity on the page and makes the regular pattern, when it banks at The New Ordinary, land as *earned* rather than a sudden cooling. For the relationship: the one place Vee sets Pace against the men before (the others *fine*, Pace not), and the load-bearing *do you love him?* asked aloud and met with a hedge — the word she owns to herself withheld from the one friend who asks. For the track: Cassie alive as the warmth/agency channel, and the sort (romance to Cassie, appetite to Randi) shown rather than asserted. For the pattern: Pace's feeling overflowing the weekend plan and Vee's recession from her own life, planted quietly under a warm line.
