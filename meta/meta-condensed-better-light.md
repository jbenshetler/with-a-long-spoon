# Condensed — Better Light

*Scene · Architecture complete, prose not drafted · `better-light.md` · date owned by chronology*

## Summary

A standalone dorm-friends dinner in the athletic dorm's dining hall — the one with the better food and the better light, which is why they eat there. Vee is out with Kayla and Meg, her Vawter-floor friends; no Cassie, no Pace. The register is light and un-probing, the ordinary aliveness of a normal girl's normal night, deliberately distinct from Cassie's confidante depth and Randi's performed intimacy. Mid-meal, Pace's spare invitation to his house lands on Vee's phone — it says nothing about the dress. The scene renders her *reaction*, not the message. Knowing mock-shock from the table — his house, for what? — and the tease is that the invite won't say. The scene seeds Kayla and Meg as individuated fall friends and plants the first-house-visit anticipation that opens the Fitting.

## Purpose

- **Seeds Kayla and Meg in the fall** (chronology flag 22): 2–3 of Vee's own friends must exist and read warm before Among Friends (~late Jan). Kayla = the warm chatterbox who becomes the desirability-seal voice at Nothing Underneath; Meg = coupled, easy, the ambient normalcy.
- **Houses the invitation-reaction** without a solo interior beat and without another Cassie telling (the Cassie cluster — Off Six-Fourteen, Rye, Turned Up — is already dense). Invitations stay off-page by the book's grammar; her *reaction* is what's rendered.
- **A different relationship register:** dorm-floor lightness, nobody reading Vee's face. The world that later contracts has to be felt alive first.

## Key Beats

- The good dining hall: better food, better light; the three of them in it, easy and loud.
- They already know the whole Pace saga — the Hallmark-warm dates, the wet-shirt gift on the trail as the raciest thing to date. This is the next installment, not the pilot.
- The text lands mid-meal. Render Vee's reaction — the buzz, the glance, the reread, the food forgotten — not the message.
- The invite is spare and logistics-only: his house. It says nothing about the dress (the Fitting's surprise depends on it).
- Knowing mock-shock: *"His house. For what?"* Vee is high-status and no innocent; the teasing is playful, not naive. The joke is the invite won't say. Mysterious.
- The novelty is the house — three dates, never his door. Vee flutters at the not-knowing more than the going.
- Kayla and Meg individuate: Kayla pounces (warm, nosy, delighted); Meg gentler, her own boyfriend a given.
