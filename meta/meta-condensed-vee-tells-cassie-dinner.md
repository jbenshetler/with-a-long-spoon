# Condensed — Vee Tells Cassie — Dinner

*VIGNETTE · Draft complete · `vee-tells-cassie-dinner.md` · the same evening as May I Choose · Vawter Hall, the dorm room · Vee's POV · Cassandra track*

## Summary

Vee comes in from the dinner still lit up and tells Cassie that same night. The clean baseline of her feeling for Pace, said aloud in the truest (unguarded-bawdy) register before Randi ever reshapes the material. Comes in **mid-recount** — the dinner is already mostly told, so the vignette does not re-tell what the reader just experienced in May I Choose; one brass-cage fragment-tail signals the mid-flow. Closes warm; the dread is entirely structural, carried by the reader, never on any character's face.

## Key Beats

**Staging / frame.** Window cracked two inches to the cold (Cassie's signature; the building runs too hot) — temperature grammar, never named: warm lit-up Vee comes into the cold the clear-eyed friend keeps. Cassie has already been **drawn out of the desk chair** by Vee's energy before we enter (highlighter down, over on the edge of Vee's bed, sock feet up, in it, grinning). The chair is the **frame**: pulled out by joy at the open, folded back into it at the close.

**Agency spine.** She claims the kiss as her own act: *I made him stop. And I kissed him… I pulled him back and I did it. Me.* "And I didn't go" goes **small and slow** — the voice regressing down (the master tell: plainest/smallest = the realest thing), drawing Cassie the last inch in. NOT defending Pace — claiming herself.

**Uncontainable-joy beat.** Off "Me," the body goes before the words: a **once-around dance** — clumsy, quick, arms up, hair everywhere, nearly into the closet door, the room too small to hold it (Pretty Woman bed-fling energy, but circular and self-generated — joy that needs no recipient, rhyming the agency). "*Look at me*" rides out of the spin, breathless.

**Drink-question = agency, established sober.** Cassie's dry floor under the gladness: *How much did he give you to drink.* Vee: *Nothing… we had tea… nobody did anything to me. I wasn't talked into it. I did it because I wanted to and I did it on purpose.* (The mint tea — on reread, the very thing that surfaced her from the under-state, cited as proof of sobriety. Unflagged.) This is the first question's job: she **chose** it; no chemical out.

**The "yes too fast" beat.** The hike *invitation* (off-page, on the post-kiss walk home — unrendered in May I Choose) lands here as the light, near-forgotten thing. Rendered as a **brushed-past stumble** in the telling — *I said yes so fast it was — anyway. Yeah* — NO self-diagnosis (cut: any "the thing where I check" naming). The reader catches the swerve; Vee hands over nothing. **Primary placement of the too-fast retrospect; the Hiking Rainstorm gets a thin echo only.**

**State-troopers bit = who-is-he, dual register.** *What's his name, so I know what to tell the state troopers when you go missing.* First read: affectionate roommate banter about a hiking date. Second read: the only time anyone asks the real question, and Vee answers with a **road number** — Pace + off 614 + the trail, fast and glad, *counted off like a dossier, like plenty.* She doesn't experience it as a deflection; she hands over her whole (thin) inventory as closeness and can't feel that it's thin.

**Rich-question (folded into the 614 exchange).** *Rich people hike. Poor people walk. Is he rich?* Vee **doesn't know** — hasn't seen the house yet; it goes into "the pile of things she'd get to find out, which was the best pile there was." Plants the money question; **resolved at the first house visit as modest middle-class for this part of Virginia.** The aphorism is **spent fresh here**; at the hike it returns as Vee's *internalized frame* (Cassie's line carried in), not as new material — so the hike's class-thread treats it as callback.

**Close warm.** No *Oh, Vee.* The troopers/rich bit lands affectionate; Cassie returns to the chair, feet up off the floor where they never reach, highlighter recapped, the small *mm.* "Borrow good ones." The only cold note is literal — the window's thin line of air over both of them; Vee lies warm and does not feel it.

**"Real shoes"** migrated into Vee's relay of what Pace said (not Cassie's closing line) — plants the hike's class material (real-shoes / rich-people-hike) as a Pace instruction.
