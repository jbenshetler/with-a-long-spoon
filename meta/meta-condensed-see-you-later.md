# Condensed — See You Later

*SCENE (DIPTYCH) · Draft complete · first class meeting after the collision*

## Summary

A two-panel scene collapsing former scenes 4, 5, and 6. Panel one (the lecture) renders the first Randi/Vee proximity; panel two (Chi Latte) renders the first one-on-one, same day, straight after class. Cassie's warning is delivered live in the seam between the two panels.

## Key Beats — Panel One (The Lecture)

Vee's POV. Vee got there early with Cassie, deciding not to watch the door; she met Randi the day before (the engineered collision in {{The Pointing Game}}, less than a day earlier) and has already worn a groove running *"we should sit together"* both ways. Randi enters from the back, sidesteps in lit-up, drops into the aisle seat so Vee is *between* her and Cassie, squeezes Vee's forearm, lands one specific affirmation (*the right pen — I love that*).

**The name-bit fires on Cassie** (first contact): Randi's practiced *"not Randy, my parents weren't trying to be funny"* routine, built to be answered with the other person's own name-bit — and Cassie declines it, returns one flat word, *"Cassie,"* leaving the routine's open door unwalked-through. Vee smooths the half-second hitch (*"Cassie says about four words a day"*) and reads the flatness as dry humor.

Lecture (residuals) goes to drone; Randi's proximity is the better thing at her elbow and Vee lets the math she's good at pass like scenery.

**Buried open/close framing carries the latent-variable plant:** the professor's end-of-class preview — *next time, latent variables, the factors you can't measure directly that turn out to be driving the whole thing from underneath* — washes past Vee, who transcribes the term and connects it to nothing.

**The hinge:** Randi names *Chi Latte* aloud and invites both girls; Cassie demurs (lab at noon, ball cap, shorts — the venue is plainly not hers; the exclusion is engineered and invisible, carried only by the fluency of Randi's choice). Vee says yes, working to fold the *yes* down to a cooler size. Cassie's parting line — flat, plain, the register-wrong word — *"Goodbye, Vee"*; Vee re-normalizes it (*"It's coffee, weirdo, I'll see you at home"*); Cassie lets it stand and goes.

## Key Beats — Panel Two (Chi Latte)

**The sign:** read as **Χ ΛΑΤΕ** (Greek *Chi* first, the latte-pun resolving a beat behind the alphabet because Randi has already said it aloud) — a threshold Vee has wanted and never felt invited to. Yellow-and-white room, coffee-and-chocolate smell, spoons ringing against cups stirring up the sweetness.

Vee scans the room in the pointing-game's grading grammar, now run by *her* eye: the matched leggings-and-quarter-zip cluster, the two near-bare girls at the bar (*floozies*, the word arriving in her mother's voice) — and under the appraisal a beat of looking that runs past appraisal's business (the latent-attraction-to-women plant), and the accurate, faintly self-pleased *I have more fabric on and I'd still wear it better*, shut down by the inherited *slut* voice; the cardigan stays. Then she looks at Randi and the calculus goes quiet — Randi isn't *in* the room's competition, she's what it's a competition toward; Vee looks up at her with flat aspirational want (rank, not appetite — Randi is filed above the floor she measures herself against).

The high table by the window (the *display* posture; Vee finally gets to sit up there). Then **the content-evaporation beat:** Vee hangs on every word and afterward cannot reconstruct one — she kept the *feeling* of being chosen, not the transcript; best hour in months, blank on what was said.

Parting on the sidewalk: a hug, the cold-citrus smell, a hand flat between her shoulder blades, **"Love you, girl. See you later"** — the *see you later* as scheduled-fact that leaves Vee spinning. Close on Vee warm and amnesiac the whole walk home; Cassie's *goodbye* already smoothed flat and forgotten.
