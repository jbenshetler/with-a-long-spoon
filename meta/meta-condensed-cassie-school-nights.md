# Condensed — Cassie — School Nights

*VIGNETTE · Unwritten · ~Oct 23 (between Dress Pickup and the CW Dance) · Vee POV · short, ~⅓–½ page*

The overnights that thicken the rhythm happen **off-page**; this is their only trace — the **greedy early phase** of the relationship, when they can't yet keep to weekends (it settles into the *new ordinary* by early November — guideline, not guarantee). Vee relays the accumulating nights to Cassie in a warm, romantic register — the proud/sweet version — while the bodily shame stays walled off (it goes to Randi). The relay does what dramatizing the overnights would not: it keeps the off-page rhythm visible without spending scenes on it, *and* it shows the confidante-sort operating in miniature. It is also the one natural place Vee says, in words, what makes Pace different. Dorm room, Vawter Hall; the cold-window detail and shared-suite life already canon.

### Beat one — the warm telling
Vee comes in from another night out, lit up, and gives Cassie the sweet ledger: they keep ending up at his place. The version that asserts the *new ordinary* and her own happiness in it — agency and romance, the register Cassie can hold. Nothing edged; the wall around the bodily response is kind, not cold (the narrowing of what you share with someone who can't follow you there). This is the same sort that began in **Cassie — After** — the romance offered, the shame withheld — now running smooth.

### Beat two — what Cassie already knew
Cassie's dry, affectionate read: *"I know. You didn't come home on school nights."* The friend who tracks the beloved's absence without judgment. Warm on first read — she's glad for her. On reread it's **Pace's feeling overflowing the weekend plan** (weeknights were never Randi's to lose) and it is *Cassie*, the one who notices Vee receding from her own life, who does the counting. The Cassandra seeing clearly, here only warmly.

### Beat three — why this one
Cassie's *why are you so gone over this one?* is the open door, and the only natural occasion for the comparison. Vee answers in **deeds, not thesis** — the men before were *fine*; Pace is not fine, he is so much more. The love she carries gets named here, the spoken companion to the version she feels without words. Keep it specific and embodied (what he does, how he is), never an abstract account of why he's better. *(Before drafting: canon-check Vee's prior sexual/relationship history so "the others were fine" rests on something established.)*

### The double register (canonical)
First read is pure warmth, no signal — a roommate clocking her friend's happiness. The cold lives only in the pattern: that the weeknights are going to Pace, that Cassie is the one keeping count, that this is the last warm beat before the SATC track takes over the heat (CW-Dance Debrief onward). **Cassandra device never named, never explained, never commented on — including by Cassie.** Cut any line that makes Cassie *suspicious* rather than simply *seeing*.

### The count (guard)
"Two more times" must reconcile with **Dress Pickup (Oct 17), already a drafted overnight** since the last Cassie telling. Prefer **rhythm phrasing** over a hard tally — Vee conveys frequency, Cassie's *"school nights"* does the counting — so there's no ledger to square. If a number is kept, scope it to the casual gap-nights (one in Oct 13–16, one in Oct 20–22) and let Cassie's line carry it.

### What it installs
For the arc: the accreted overnight rhythm that makes the new ordinary land as *earned*, not sudden — without spending a scene on it. For the relationship: the one place Vee articulates why Pace is different (the others *fine*, Pace much more), the spoken companion to the love she feels without words. For the track: Cassie alive as the warmth/agency channel in the Oct 12 → early-Nov gap, and the sort (romance to Cassie, appetite to Randi) shown rather than asserted. For the pattern: Pace's feeling overflowing the weekend plan and Vee's recession from her own life, planted quietly under a warm line.
