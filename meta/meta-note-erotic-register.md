## 1. The register, in one line

**Direct and lyrical: anatomically precise, never euphemistic, never clinical, never pornographic — "clinical accuracy with emotional weight" (`meta-rules.md`), felt from the inside of the touching, not filmed.**

The prose does not look away from the body, and it does not look at it *provocatively* either. It names the response first and the cognition second, and it trusts precise sensation over imagery. When it reaches, it reaches through sensation, gesture, and material consequence — not through medical nouns and not through romance-genre evasion.

The four modes to stay out of:
- **Euphemism / romance evasion** — "making love," "she welcomed him," "he entered her garden," "her flower." Never.
- **Clinical / medical** — "penetrated," "ejaculated," "vagina," "labia," "aroused." Never (see lexicon for the exception `areola`, used *once* in inventory mode).
- **Pornographic** — technique lists, choreography for its own sake, anatomy narrated to titillate rather than to be *felt*.
- **Coy fade-out** — cutting away at the threshold. The book stays in the room. What it withholds, it withholds by *displacement* (see §3), not by closing the door.

## 2. Explicitness: baseline and ceiling

**The current level is the baseline, not the ceiling.** The reference scenes define the normal working range; peak scenes (the threesome above all) may go *more* graphic, but escalation is earned the same way heat is — one riser at a time, for a structural reason, never as an arms race.

The four references are themselves a range, and the range is instructive:

- **`{{On Her Floor}}` — lowest anatomical.** Arousal entirely by implication; no genitals named at all; the whole explicit charge displaced onto a single object (the soaked green thong she must peel off, alone). Proof that a scene can run at maximum *erotic* pressure with near-zero anatomical vocabulary.
- **`{{The Bench}}` — moderate.** Digital penetration, edging, the standing survey. Anatomy named when active ("clit," "cleft," "gloss"); Pace's own erection noted once, late, and deprioritized.
- **`{{Vee on the Bench}}` — explicit but oblique.** Full penetrative sex and multiple orgasms, the drip sequence — graphic, but her genitals rendered through circumlocution ("the point of her," "her gripping sex," "the soaked open mouth of her"). Explicit without a single crude word.
- **`{{Nothing Underneath}}` — the current ceiling.** Masturbation, oral, standing penetration, mutual climax rendered in full. The most anatomically frank drafted scene ("clit," "channel," "drive up into her," "spilled into her," "milking"). Even here, *his* genitals are never named and "fuck" appears exactly once.

**Rule of thumb:** match the scene's *kind* (§6), not the last scene's *heat number*. A tender scene is not a failure for being less graphic than `{{Nothing Underneath}}`; a shame scene earns its explicitness through the indignity, not the inventory.

**When escalating past the current ceiling** (climactic scenes): keep the lexicon rules (§3) — precision does the work, not new vulgarity. More explicit means *more of the body admitted and felt*, not *coarser words imported*.

## 3. The lexicon

Concrete, extracted from the reference set. These are strong defaults, not a cage — but a departure should be a decision.

### Female anatomy — *named, with an oblique-first preference*

Clitoris / vulva / opening, in rough order of preference:
- **`the point of her`** — and its variants: *the swollen point of her, the bright pressed point of her, the small stiff point of her.* The dominant oblique term (`{{Vee on the Bench}}`). Precise, tactile, never coy.
- **`clit`** — the one plain word that is allowed. Use *sparingly* and usually in private/early beats (mirror, the longing before contact). It is the register's floor, not its default.
- **`her sex`** — euphemistic but unapologetic; fine at speed.
- **`her center` / `wet center` / `the center of her`** — the region.
- **`the cleft`** — the visual/static view (esp. Pace's gaze); *`the coral lips`* is the one endorsed anatomical metaphor; *`the lips` / `outer lips`* acceptable.
- **`the soaked open mouth of her`** — the opening, at penetration.
- **`her gripping sex` / `her channel`** — the interior; rare, literary, reserved for the deep beats. "Channel" once per scene at most.

### Male anatomy — *never named*

This is a hard, consistent asymmetry across every scene: **his genitals are never given a noun.** No *cock, penis, dick.* Rendered entirely through:
- pronoun + action: *took him out; found her and drove up into her; seated on him; how hard he was; hard and ready.*
- state, once: *he was hard / still hard.*

Her body is named with increasing directness; his is withheld to the last. Preserve the asymmetry — it is part of the POV architecture (the reader lives in her body, watches his).

### Fluids — *tactile, adjectival, never nominalized-clinical*

- **`the wet`** (noun: *gathered the wet; her own wet*), **`slick`** (adj + noun: *the slick of her; slick and warm*), **`wetness`**, **`gloss`** (the visual/tactile sheen, esp. Pace's gaze).
- Metaphor sparingly: *a dark bloom of herself* (the coat stain), *the sea in it* (scent).
- **His climax:** *spilled into her; let go.* His semen is never named. Never "ejaculated," never "cum/come" as a noun.
- Avoid: "juices," "wetness" used clinically, anything that reads like a product label.

### Breasts / buttocks — *plain and direct*

- **`breasts`, `nipples`** — standard, never euphemized, never softened. State described plainly: *drawn up hard, insistent pink.* (`areola` appears exactly once, in Pace's cool inventory survey — that clinical note is a deliberate one-off, not license.)
- **`ass`** — the vernacular default in action (Pace's hands, the strike placement). **`bottom` / `backside`** — the gentler register, for tenderer beats. *`the roundness of her`* for the reflective gaze.

### Verbs — *labor-, craft-, and motion-adjacent*

- **Building/touch:** *work* (the dominant verb — *he worked her, unhurried*), *run up* (*ran her up the long slope*), *stroke, circle* (clitoral), *roll* (nipples), *press, slide, find, handle, knead.*
- **Penetration:** *drive* (`{{Nothing Underneath}}`), *breach* (`{{Vee on the Bench}}`), *fill / seated / take him up into.* **`thrust` is absent — keep it absent.**
- **Orgasm (intransitive):** *came, went over, went off, broke over her, came apart.* The involuntary interior cluster: *clenching and pulling and milking; clenched down on.*
- **`fuck`:** used exactly *once* in the whole drafted corpus (`{{Nothing Underneath}}`, unremarkably, at the graceless reunion peak). Not forbidden — but it is a once-a-book word. If you reach for it, that had better be why.
- **Spanking has its own cluster:** monosyllabic Anglo-Saxon — *hit, struck, came down, fell, landed, laid down* — always paired with the aftercare verb *soothed.* (Sexual touch gets the slower, varied verbs above; the strike gets the blunt ones. The contrast is the point.)

### Two non-verbal channels that carry the explicit

- **Sound.** The body's acoustics do explicit work no noun does: *a small wet sound her own body made against his hand; the wet sound of her still in the air; Pat. Pat.* (the drip landing on bare wood). When you want frankness without a crude word, reach for the sound.
- **Scent.** Named by *character and effect*, never clinically: *the sea in it; her own scent rising thick and warm and animal; musky and undeniable.*

## 4. Metaphor vs. literal

**Literal and tactile during the action; metaphor in the approach and the reflection.** Roughly 70/30 metaphor-to-literal in the slow kiss/build beats; tips hard toward literal in penetrative action and climax. The literal grounds the metaphor — without the concrete (the taste of salt and lime, the position of a hand, the temperature under a palm) the metaphors would float.

Metaphors that work here are **sensory and tactile**, not conceptual: arousal as an *electric wire* / *bright wire to the low place*; heat *letting down through her* like liquid/gravity; scent that *turned in her like a key*; the *long slope* / *gathering wave* of the climb; *fire*, *flood*. The one *structural* metaphor the book allows itself is the collapse of the self — *the whole careful architecture of her went the way a dropped plate goes* — and it is reserved for the moment of breaking.

The emotional metaphors do their heaviest lifting in the *gap* before contact (*he looked at her like a starving man, and like a man just told he is forgiven*), which frees the sex itself to stay literal.

## 5. POV interiority during sex

Governed in full by the craft docs; the sex-specific shape:

**Close third on Vee (the erotic/confidante scenes) — double consciousness.** The "watching part" of her narrates her own exposure with her mother's eyes; its gradual loosening and final *drop* **is** the orgasm (*the watching part, with nothing left to track, let go, all the way, and dropped*). Cognition stays fully present but **never forensic** (body before mind — `meta-craft-vivienne.md`): she states what she knows as known, and reports what her body does from the *outside* (the "announcement" idiom). Under pressure her voice **regresses down** — smaller words, younger syntax — never sharper.

**Pace's POV (used to render Randi's body, arm the irony) — observer mode.** His interiority is the *reading of her body*, not the naming of his own sensation; his own arousal is deprioritized and surfaces once, late (*he was still hard… he held it the way you hold a fact, and set it down*). No filing, no cataloguing — when something reaches him, render it embodied (his hand already on her before his mind finishes the observation). Analysis *brackets* the sensation; it does not narrate through it.

**The threesome** is rendered *inside Vee's blindfold* — see `meta-note-threesome-reveal.md`.

## 6. Sentence rhythm

The syntax enacts the arc. This is one of the most portable tools in the set:

- **Approach / escalation** — long, cumulative, *and*-chained sentences; semicolons and em-dashes holding clauses in suspension; the em-dash pivot that names the thing she was trying not to name and continues past it. The accumulation *is* the pressure building without release.
- **Climax / the break** — the compression: short declaratives, then fragments, sometimes a single word on its own line (*Already. Filled. And the surface broke. Pat. Pat.*). The rhythm slows or shatters at the peak — it does **not** accelerate.
- **Aftercare / rest** — sentences lengthen again, syntax relaxes into trailing clauses (*she could feel him still in her, going nowhere, her body loose and joined around him*).

Denial beats run clipped (*"No," she said. Too quickly.*); the deflating truth-line is often the *shortest* sentence in the scene (*it went hard and fast and graceless, which was the truth of them tonight*).

## 7. Shame — the engine (rendering notes)

The *why* lives in `meta-arch-bible.md` and `meta-craft-vivienne.md` (Most Defended Nerve; the freshness rule). The *how it lands on the page*:

- **The involuntary tell is the wound:** wet, hard nipples, flush — the body announcing its wanting *without her consent*. Hierarchy: withheld/deepest = the wet spot; extractable/surface = the nipples.
- **Cringe and heat arrive as one thing**, simultaneous and indistinguishable — *the cringe and the heat one thing arriving together, so that she could not have said where the wanting-to-die ended and the wanting began.* Not shame *then* arousal; fused.
- **The mother's voice / the watching part** — free-indirect, italic, unattributed: *floozie… a girl who gets like that.* The shame in its most compressed form. Render at experience level; she is *subject to* the voice, never performing or reporting it.
- **Freshness is mandatory.** What she's ashamed of is constant (the involuntary announcement); the *occasion* must be new every time. The through-line of indignities is itself a staircase: spanking-proof → the secretions → watched exposure → complicity → tasting herself → the blindfold. **Never re-light the last indignity at the same heat.**
- **The complicity escalation** — the deepest turn: from *her body betrays her* to *she is helping it* (*her hands white on the grips; no one had tied them there… there was no rope in the world she could have laid the blame on*). When available, this is a full riser up from the plain involuntary tell.
- **Denial-as-proof** — Pace (and Randi, in her register) names the denial and turns it into evidence (*"That 'no' keeps getting deeper." "All the way down on 'no.'"*). The denial refrain is an erotic device, not a brake.

**How shame relaxes in tender/relief scenes.** In reunion/relief mode (`{{Nothing Underneath}}`), the shame mechanism is *invoked and then inverted*: the same "body telling on her" formula fires, but her *agency* converts the affect — *thrilled her past anything like shame, because she had done this.* The shame doesn't vanish; the thrill *exceeds its ceiling.* Tender scenes may let this conversion happen; shame scenes must not — there the shame stays load-bearing and unresolved. See `meta-note-tender-relief.md` for the full restful-restraint sub-register (how shame relaxes; oral / penetration / male-finish micro-mechanics).

## 8. Vary the heat by *kind* (not just intensity)

An intensity-only arms race is unwinnable and burns the tonal contrast the dark ending needs. The five kinds, with a drafted touchstone for each:

| Kind | What it feels like | Touchstone |
|---|---|---|
| **Psychologically intense** | shame as engine; the watching-self dismantled; denial-as-proof | `{{Vee on the Bench}}`; `{{The Bench}}` |
| **Tender / relief / reconnection** | shame invoked then inverted by her agency; graceless, honest, warm | `{{Famished}}` (first night); `{{We Find Out}}`; `{{Nothing Underneath}}` (couch) — see `meta-note-tender-relief.md` |
| **Playful** | teasing as a direct line to arousal; banter; delight | Pace's register — see `meta-craft-pace.md` |
| **Languid** | unhurried, savoring, heat from patience | the slow-build sections of `{{Vee on the Bench}}` |
| **Restful-restraint** | stillness, being held/kept, heat without motion | `meta-note-tender-relief.md` |

The confidante track (Randi/Vee, e.g. `{{On Her Floor}}`) runs its own arc — implication and displacement over anatomy, warm on both reads, **no sinister signal ever** (`meta-note-taste-thread.md`; `meta-craft-randi.md`).

## 9. Quick pre-draft checklist

Before drafting a sex scene, in addition to the standard lore prep:
1. **Which kind?** (§8) — decide before the first sentence; don't default to intense.
2. **Whose POV shape?** (§5) — Vee's double-consciousness or Pace's observer-mode.
3. **What's the *new* indignity?** (§7) — the shame occasion must not repeat the last one.
4. **Does a thread want a seed?** — taste thread for any Pace/Vee sex (`meta-note-taste-thread.md`); most scenes seed nothing — *decide*, don't plant by reflex.
5. **Explicitness = the kind, not the last scene's number.** (§2)
6. **Lexicon discipline** (§3): oblique-first for her, never-name for him, tactile fluids, no clinical/euphemistic/pornographic drift.
7. **Let the syntax carry the arc** (§6): long to build, short to break, long to rest.
8. **Run `na.py style`** on the draft and check the reserved words (undone/undid; seen-vs-chosen).
