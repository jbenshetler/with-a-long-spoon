He sits, helps via her econ teacher's frame — turn the problem into one you already know how to do. She is circling a hard integral; he writes the horrible closed-form from memory only to wave it away (*don't — nobody should carry that around*), then draws a small right-angle cross and reminds her of Euler's formula, which she already knows but had filed in its own chapter as if it weren't *for* anything. The right-angles/Euler figure (two things at right angles making a third that looks like neither; the circle she's trapped in resolved) hums against the book's engine and against *may I call you* later in the scene — kept fully underground, never pointed. *"May I call you?"* — the *may* from a register her mother would approve. He takes her number, gives Randi the same courteous nod he gives anyone, and walks out.
