# Notes — Sheri — First Ride

*Pre-draft notes from workshop. The scene is unwritten; this captures its thesis, the voice work, the broach mechanism, and the craft guards. Companion to the chronology entry for Sheri — First Ride — this is the craft thinking behind that entry.*

---

## Position & job

- **Sat Oct 26, morning.** Sits in the gap between the CW-Dance blowup (Thu Oct 24) and the reconciliation that evening (the First Swat / The Setup opens with Vee arriving Saturday evening to apologize). Pace is **raw and unresolved** — he doesn't yet know the redhead thing survives. That unresolved state is the engine: it's *why* he tells Sheri more than he means to.
- **The one outside-the-house bond shown.** Its job is to render who Pace is when the chivalric grammar simply *works* — his register on the outside of his own architecture, with the one person who costs him nothing.
- First rendered instance of a friendship that has existed throughout. Brief, warm.
- **POV: Pace, close third — locked.** It's the only frame in which both the mirrored-appetite read and the load-bearing silence work: inside his head the scheme simply never surfaces across the whole ride, conspicuous on reread. Also the POV the book uses to pay the visual debt early.

## Pace's state here — calibrate the affect (NOT love)

- **Not in love. Yet.** He is *highly attracted to her combination of attributes* — pitch every leaked beat to that, never to romance. Overshooting into lovesick makes him a simpler man and spends a card the book needs later.
- **The unique resonance** (home: arch-pace / relationship docs; restated here only to calibrate): Vee *meets* him where Randi only operates — she sees the made things (the furniture), is pulled into his math books, sees deeper than Randi. And the kink is engaged: his appetite is getting a woman to *willingly* transgress her own boundaries, and Vee reads as heavily caged, bucking to be loose, needing help — the ideal canvas.
- **So "more than he bargained for" = the doubling:** the subject Randi pitched turns out to both meet him *and* be the perfect canvas for the appetite. *That* unsettles the clean scheme — not love. The crack the silence exposes is *"she's become someone who sees me,"* not *"I love her."*
- **What leaks to Sheri:** the charged, animated attention of a man more *caught* by this one than a disposable subject warrants — not devotion. Sheri, reading her friend, clocks that he's into this one *differently.*

## Form is load-bearing: bicycles, side by side

- **Bicycles, genuinely side by side, no eye contact.** This is the only social format that costs Pace nothing neurologically (the other is the dance floor). The form is not flavor — it *causes* the leak: he overshares precisely because he isn't being looked at across a table.
- Cycling is already their established shared format (the bacon-cheeseburgers-after-cycling friendship). Do **not** make this his motorcycle / Sheri on the back — that breaks the side-by-side rule and is the wrong intimacy.
- Render the friendship through the motion. Avoid the face-to-face confessional scene that would cost Pace neurologically.

## The broach mechanism

- **Sheri saw the blowup — and it must be clearly implied on the page, not left vague.** She's a CW regular: blends in, home turf. Thursday night she watched the redhead blow up at Pace and watched him leave alone; because Pace is her friend, she kept an eye on Vee the rest of the night. Render the presence through a **concrete report** she volunteers — e.g. *"After you left Thursday, I kept an eye on her. All she did was sit by herself glaring at the dance floor, nursing a drink. She left alone."* The line places Sheri at the scene, renders her loyalty as *protective intelligence* (she posted up on the woman who fought with him), and is built entirely of reassurance — Vee was miserable, danced with no one, **left alone** (pointed, given the blowup was about Pace "getting girls home safe": the redhead is not what she accused him of, and she's gone on *him*). Consistent with the drafted CW Dance (Pace leaves alone; Vee stays nursing one drink watching the floor; goes home alone).
- **Dark-on-reread layer:** Sheri's loyalty is *reassuring Pace toward Vee* — nudging him deeper into the attachment that becomes his reckoning. Her fierce care greases the trap. Light on first read (a friend cheering him up); cold only in the pattern. Do not point at it.
- **She asks first about what happened with the redhead** — checking on her friend, who she assumes got burned.
- She names the body plainly: the redhead, *some great curves.* This is her idiom — working/country, plain surface, the body named without shame. **Impossible in Cassie's mouth** (Cassie is outside the appearance economy; she'd never volunteer an appraisal). The frank, unashamed naming of a woman's body is also, resonantly, the un-shamed version of the exact thing the novel makes Vee suffer over — do not point at this; let it sit.
- When she learns they're actually together — and sees how gone he is — she asks **"What happened to the pale sorority chick?"** Randi is canon-pale, sorority junior (Standards Officer); the line is hers exactly.
- **Keep *how* Sheri knows about Randi offstage** (TBD or never disclosed). Pace and Randi are not a public couple — sleeping together, unnamed, the sorority disapproving — so Sheri can't have read them as boyfriend/girlfriend in public. The line works as a *swap-out* (there was a pale sorority girl; now there's a redhead), not as someone tracking a relationship. Don't explain the sourcing. This is consistent with her canon "one Randi encounter — *she's a lot*."

## Pace's honesty — non-denial, not confession

The beat is **"Pace doesn't lie to Sheri."** Render it as:

- **Non-denial, not disclosure.** He doesn't say Randi's over; he doesn't pretend the redhead is simple. He gives a **true-but-partial** answer to the door Sheri opens — confirms the overlap, falsifies nothing — and simply does not volunteer the frame that connects the two women (the scheme with Randi). He never explains.
- **"Tells more than he intends" = affect, not plot.** What leaks is how *caught* he is by Vee — charged, animated attention, not devotion (he is not in love; see "Pace's state here"). Sheri reads her friend (not the system) and clocks that he's into this one differently. That is the whole of the overshare — it stays inside **never name, never explain.**

## The load-bearing omission (the reread tell)

The single most important beat, and it's a *silence*, not a line.

- **Sheri's "complicated" is only about the obvious thing:** you're sleeping with two women at once, that's going to blow up. Plain bar-stool common sense. She does **not** know about the scheme; she is aiming at the *visible* complication and is blind to the real one. (Reinforces not-a-Cassandra: she warns about the small fire while the big one sits unspoken.)
- **Pace does not tell her about the scheme** — the threesome he cooked up with Randi. The reason matters: **not fear of judgment.** Sheri wouldn't judge the threesome, and fiercely loyal, probably wouldn't judge the scheme either. So the omission can't be read as hiding-from-disapproval. The only answer left, on reread, is that **Pace can no longer say it out loud himself** — Vee has become more than he bargained for (the subject the plan was built around turns out to both meet him and be the perfect canvas for the appetite — see "Pace's state here"; this is *not* love), and the plan has become a thing he can't look at directly. The silence is the first cost of the reckoning, paid before he knows he's paying it.
- **The patent rhyme is the anchor that makes the silence legible.** Pace is the man who goes *voluble* about his clever constructions — the patent monologue, pure childish wonder at having made a useful solution, given to Sheri on a bike ride (canon). The scheme is exactly that kind of clever object to the old Pace; he'd normally narrate it to her with delight. On *this* ride he doesn't give the monologue. **The one construction he can't show Sheri.** The reader who remembers the patent feels the shape of the silence.
- **Rhymes against Cassie** by inversion: Cassie declines to walk through the open door a routine is built to receive (clarity); here Pace declines the open door Sheri holds for him (the first flinch of conscience).

### Render guards for the omission

- **Invisible first read.** No visible dodge — no changing the subject, no marked hesitation, no "he didn't want to get into it." That telegraphs and makes Pace read guilty/calculating, killing both the lightness and his realness. First-read cover must be total: a private guy being a little close about a messy situation. Nobody narrates their sex logistics on a bike ride.
- **No narrated doubt.** Body before mind: Pace does not agonize on the page, does not think *I'm not sure about this anymore.* At most a small friction he can't name and rides past. The reader assembles "he has questions" from the **omission + the leaked affect**, never from Pace announcing unease. The text does not say it.
- The scene's argument is a **juxtaposition the reader completes on reread:** he's gone on her / he built a plan to use her / he couldn't say the plan to the one person safe to hear it. Set the three side by side; explain none of them.

## The device that earns the page: Sheri warns Pace

- **Cassie warns Vee; Sheri warns Pace.** Pace's specific deficit is that he misses meaning *encoded* under deniability — Randi runs entirely on that. Sheri is the one person in the book who speaks in **plain, uncoded surface.** She hands him the warning in the one register he *can* parse — *"If that's not already complicated, it will be"* — and he still can't act on it, because the appetite is already engaged.
- That is his reckoning hole (deceiving someone into consent) handed to him in words he's equipped to hear, and refused anyway. On-thesis; do not gloss it.

## Voice guards (Sheri vs. Cassie — must not converge)

- **Sheri:** working/country idiom, plain surface, names bodies without shame; warmth lives in her **fierceness, not pleasantness**; her humor punctures Pace's intellectualism specifically; she is not dazzled by his competence and has no investment in his masculine performance.
- **Cassie:** bone-dry, *withholding* — few words, exact; an eyebrow held back to see if you catch the line; outside the appearance economy entirely.
- **Sheri reads her friend; Cassie reads the system.** Sheri's "if that's not complicated, it will be" is **NOT** structural insight into the configuration — it's plain relational common sense plus her single radar-ping on a conditional person (Randi = loves on terms, the exact shape of Sheri's family wound). **She is not a second Cassandra.** Protect the asymmetry: Cassie structural/sustained, Sheri a single ping from a different sensor.

## Introducing Sheri cold (her first appearance — establish through behavior)

This is the reader's first meeting with Sheri. None of her canon (lesbian, fierce loyalty, not-a-Cassandra) can be *referenced* — it must be **performed** in the few strokes a brief vignette allows. The strokes stack: one appraisal line carries orientation *and* voice; one protective offer plus Pace's deflection carries loyalty *and* tenderness *and* the first turn of the omission. Do not let it sprawl into a scene that establishes Sheri; she is rendered through effect.

**Lesbian — render as appetite, not envy.** The crude failure mode (*"I'm jealous you get all the hot chicks"*) frames her look at Vee as rivalry-with-Pace — straight-woman sizing-up inside the appearance economy she is supposed to be *outside* of. Fix: put the desire where a straight woman's wouldn't be — **appreciation with zero comparison, appetite with no stake in being looked at herself.**
- The appraisal carries *relish, not measurement.* Same words read opposite by engine: cataloguing = threat-assessment; enjoying = desire. Lean the description toward liking-to-look, not ranking.
- **Pace's POV does the quiet confirming:** he recognizes his own appetite mirrored in her (*she looked at the redhead the way he did*) and registers it as utterly ordinary from Sheri. Tells the reader she wants women, no label — and (dark on reread) puts two people who want Vee on the same ride.
- **Move the humor onto Pace, off the envy:** the joke punctures *him* (a woman like that wasted on a man who'd rather explain a proof) — her canonical register (deflates his intellectualism, not dazzled), and it signals she'd appreciate the woman herself with no rivalry in it.
- Resist a second stroke (a girl Sheri's seeing, a glance at another woman) — one beat too many; the appraisal + Pace's mirror is enough and cleaner.

**Loyalty — protective intelligence, reported back as comfort (primary render; legible cold).** The concrete CW-Dance report (above) *is* the loyalty beat: she didn't idly notice the redhead, she **posted up and monitored** the woman who fought with her friend, and every fact she volunteers is chosen to make Pace feel better (Vee miserable, danced with no one, left alone). Care rendered as behavior — surveillance-as-devotion — unmistakable even at first meeting. "Warmth lives in the fierceness, not the pleasantness" — rendered, not asserted. A secondary flash is available if wanted (a dry, standing offer to *dislike the redhead on his say-so, no questions* — adopting his enemy sight-unseen), but watch economy; the report alone may carry it.

**Pace deflects her off Vee — and the deflection interlocks with the omission.** He calls Sheri's fierceness back off the redhead. Critically: **not by taking false blame.** The blowup genuinely wasn't his fault (the CW Dance establishes Vee was wrong), and Pace is accurate; he won't lie to claim fault. He deflects with affectionate humor that protects Vee while staying light: *"No. She couldn't take your heat."* It works on Sheri's **fighter self-image** (martial-arts backstory) — she hears *you'd win / you're too much for her* and is flattered, so Pace never has to forbid her or explain. **No backstory on the page** (no heights, no weights, no martial arts named); the size mismatch is *shown*, not stated — Sheri's smallness rendered on a child-sized frame as she pulls ahead on a mid-ride climb, just before the line, with Vee's height planted earlier in the book. Ironic for the reader who can see it; the text never points. NOT false blame (the blowup wasn't his fault; Pace is accurate and won't claim it). The move does three jobs at once: establishes Sheri's protectiveness, shows Pace's genuine tenderness toward Vee (defends her unprompted), and turns the omission's first gear — he shields Vee from his own friend's loyalty, reflex-protecting the very person he's scheming to use.

**Drafted:** `scenes/sheri-first-ride.md`. Structure is talk → climb → talk: the flat carries the CW report, the chair/math-books gush, and *"Listen to you"*; a climb then breaks the talk and plants tiny-Sheri (child-sized frame, the small engine of her, the bleached ponytail) while Pace marvels; the recovered flat carries the deflection (*"She couldn't take your heat"*), the Randi door (*"That's not finished"*), and the warning (*"If that's not already complicated, it will be"*), then they ride on with the warning unanswered. The climb-before-the-joke is load-bearing — it *shows* Sheri's smallness so the line lands with no backstory on the page.

## Craft watch

- **Earn the dark by being light.** The whole exchange must read as ordinary friend-skepticism on first pass — warm, funny, true. Only the reader, who knows the machine, hears the understatement in *"it will be."* If you can feel the scene signaling doom, cut the signal.
- **Brief.** Secondary character, rendered through effect and function, not standalone interiority. Don't over-feed Sheri's inner life.
- **Pace's virtues are real, not cynical.** His ease with Sheri, his not-lying, the friendship itself — render genuinely warm first.

If a more settled Pace is wanted, the ride would need to move past the First Swat.
