# Notes — Sheri — First Ride

*Companion to the drafted scene (`scenes/sheri-first-ride.md`) and its condensed (`meta-condensed-sheri-first-ride.md`). Holds the craft intent that is deliberately **not** on the page — the subtext, calibrations, and guards a continuity pass or a later Sheri scene will need. For what happens on the page, read the condensed; for surface beats, the scene.*

---

## Why the scene is shaped this way

- **POV: Pace, close third — locked.** The only frame in which the load-bearing silence works: inside his head the scheme simply never surfaces across the whole ride, conspicuous on reread. Also the POV the book uses to pay the visual debt early. (Governs the later Sheri vignettes too.)
- **The unresolved-state choice.** The Oct 26 morning slot sits *between* the CW-Dance blowup (Thu) and that evening's reconciliation. Pace is raw and doesn't yet know the redhead thing survives — that's *why* he overshares. (Decided; recorded so a later edit doesn't "settle" him here.)
- **Bicycles, side by side, no eye contact.** The form *causes* the leak — he overshares because he isn't being looked at across a table. Not an accommodation; face-to-face doesn't tax him, this is just how he and Sheri are. Not his motorcycle / Sheri on the back — wrong intimacy, breaks the format. Same discipline for Cheeseburgers.

## Pace's state here — calibrate the affect (NOT love)

- **Not in love. Yet.** He is *highly attracted to her combination of attributes*; pitch every leaked beat to that, never to romance. Overshooting into lovesick makes him a simpler man and spends a card the book needs later.
- **The resonance** (home: arch-pace / relationship docs; here only to calibrate): Vee *meets* him where Randi only operates — sees the made things, pulled into his math books — and the kink is engaged (his appetite is getting a woman to *willingly* transgress her own caging; Vee reads as the ideal canvas). **"More than he bargained for" = the doubling:** the subject Randi pitched turns out to both meet him *and* be the perfect canvas. That unsettles the clean scheme — not love. The crack the silence exposes is *"she's become someone who sees me,"* not *"I love her."*
- **What leaks to Sheri:** charged, animated attention, not devotion. She reads her friend and clocks he's into this one *differently.*

## The load-bearing omission (the reread tell) — the most important thing here, and it's a silence

- **Pace never mentions the scheme** (the threesome with Randi). Not from fear of judgment — Sheri wouldn't judge the threesome, and fiercely loyal, probably not the scheme either. So the only answer left, on reread, is that **he can no longer say it out loud himself.** The silence is the first cost of the reckoning, paid before he knows he's paying it.
- **The patent rhyme makes the silence legible.** Pace goes *voluble* about his clever constructions (the patent monologue, given to Sheri on a bike ride — canon). The scheme is exactly that kind of clever object; he'd normally narrate it with delight. Here he doesn't. **The one construction he can't show Sheri.**
- **Rhymes against Cassie** by inversion: Cassie declines to walk through an open door (clarity); Pace declines the open door Sheri holds for him (the first flinch of conscience).
- **Render guards:** *invisible first read* — no visible dodge, no marked hesitation; first-read cover is a private guy being a little close about a messy situation. *No narrated doubt* — body before mind; the reader assembles "he has questions" from the omission + the leaked affect, never from Pace announcing unease. The argument is a juxtaposition completed on reread (caught by her / built a plan to use her / couldn't say it to the one person safe to hear it); explain none of it.

## The device that earns the page: Sheri warns Pace

- **Cassie warns Vee; Sheri warns Pace.** Pace's deficit is missing meaning *encoded* under deniability — Randi runs on that. Sheri is the one person who speaks in **plain, uncoded surface**, and hands him the warning in the one register he *can* parse (*"If that's not already complicated, it will be"*) — and he still can't act on it, the appetite already engaged. His reckoning hole (deceiving someone into consent) handed to him in words he's equipped to hear, and refused. On-thesis; do not gloss.
- **Her "complicated" is only the obvious thing** (two women at once). She does not know about the scheme; she aims at the *visible* fire while the big one sits unspoken. This is the not-a-Cassandra guard in action — plain relational sense plus one radar-ping on a conditional person (Randi), **not** structural insight. (Sheri reads her friend; Cassie reads the system. Keep them from converging — full character/voice in `meta-arch-sheri`.)

## Dark-on-reread layer (do not point at it)

Sheri's loyalty is *reassuring Pace toward Vee* — the CW report (Vee miserable, danced with no one, left alone) nudges him deeper into the attachment that becomes his reckoning. Her fierce care greases the trap. Light on first read (a friend cheering him up); cold only in the pattern.

## Continuity hook — resolved

The size-mismatch joke (*"She couldn't take your heat"*) shows Sheri tiny but states no figures; it needs only that the reader already knows Vee is **tall and lushly built**. Met in **the pointing game** (Vee "tall, built on a lavish scale"; sharpened at the collision — sleek narrow Randi against the redhead's lush curves and the half-head of height on her), which precedes this ride. The **Vee-vs-Pace near-parity is deliberately NOT planted early** — Vee carries him as taller than he is, and *The First Swat* spends that misperception as the heel-inversion surprise; planting it sooner would dull the payoff. (Epistemic home: arch-vivienne; guard: meta-condensed-the-first-swat.)

## Craft watch

- **Earn the dark by being light** — reads as ordinary, warm, funny friend-talk first; only the reader who knows the machine hears the understatement. Cut any doom signal.
- **Brief** — Sheri is rendered through effect, not interiority; don't over-feed her.
- **Pace's virtues are real, not cynical** — his ease with Sheri and his not-lying are genuinely warm first.
