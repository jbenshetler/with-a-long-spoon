# Condensed — The CW Dance

*SCENE · Unwritten · Thursday evening · close third on Vee*

## Position

Follows whatever social scene precedes it; feeds directly into the CW-Dance Debrief (the next morning, Randi's single). The scene that makes the debrief necessary. Vee's first time with Pace outside his house, outside her campus — a public social context she didn't engineer. The overnight converts the fight from hot embarrassment to cold, clear *I have to fix this.*

---

## The Lesson (Before the Dance Hall)

Pace texts Tuesday for Thursday — specific date, casual register, no ambiguity about what this is. He teaches her to two-step on a concrete pad somewhere on campus: a boom box, not a phone. He came prepared. The lesson is intimate before it's anything else.

**The frame conflict.** Vee objects to the restriction of the dance frame — her arms, her movement, the way the lead governs her. Pace is unyielding. He says it once: *Don't think, follow the lead.* She complies reluctantly. The payoff is immediate and physical: when she stops fighting the frame, the dance takes her. Body first — the sensation of it working — then the small internal admission that he was right. Do not narrate the admission. Render the body arriving there.

**The plant.** This line, said once here and never repeated, returns at the threesome when Vee asks not to know who the second woman is until it's over. *Don't think, just feel the lead.* The reader's body carries the lesson forward; the text does not announce the callback.

---

## The Dance Hall

The lesson was private and tutorial; the hall is loud, warm, crowded with people who know what they're doing. Arrival changes the register. Vee shows up knowing something about herself she didn't know before the concrete pad.

**The dancing.** Pace is an excellent lead. What the lesson installed works in the hall in a way it couldn't on the concrete pad — the music, the crowd, the momentum behind it. Vee is good because he's good; she knows this about herself now and doesn't entirely mind knowing it. Other women notice them on the floor. This is a high before the turn.

---

## Kelsey

Pace goes to get drinks. Kelsey approaches — a regular at the hall, friendly, no visible agenda. She delivers: the getting-girls-home-safely thing, the he's-not-like-that thing, the accurate and casually-offered corrective to any concern Vee might have about what Pace gets up to. The line is delivered the way you'd tell a stranger their shoe is untied.

**Kelsey's tell.** One phrase, one detail after the warm delivery — a glance toward where Pace went, or a fraction of warmth in her voice that's too personal for a stranger. The register shifts from *friendly acquaintance* to *woman who has feelings about this man.* She knew what those words would do to another woman and said them anyway. She leaves before it becomes a scene.

**What Vee reads.** Vee reads Kelsey correctly. The narration does not confirm the read. Vee knows; the reader knows; nobody says it. The damage is done in one sentence.

---

## The Fight

The jealousy fires before she can stop it. Pace comes back. The specific content of the fight is less important than its shape: Vee accuses him of the thing he pointedly doesn't do, performs the proprietary jealousy she'd have mocked in another woman, and he goes. He says *Vivienne, this isn't—* and doesn't finish it. He leaves.

**What makes it bad.** Not that she was angry. That she was wrong, visibly wrong, and he knew it, and she performed exactly the thing she would have mocked in someone else. The hot embarrassment is not about the fight. It's about being the woman who did that.

---

## The Bartender

Bar closing. An older woman — fond of Pace, matter-of-fact in the register of someone who has watched this room for years and keeps her own counsel. She offers the correction with no editorializing. A factual error, corrected.

**The inversion.** The women Pace gets home safely are not in competition with Vee — they're the ones looking at Vee's seat and wishing. She came to the dance afraid she might be losing standing; the bartender tells her she's been demonstrating it. She was not only wrong about Pace; she was wrong about her own position entirely. The correction lands complete: no ambiguity, nothing to reinterpret on the ride home.

---

## Getting Home

**The cab.** Pace was going to drive her. He's gone. She sits at the bar at closing time and calls a cab — pays a stranger to do the thing Pace was going to do anyway. The transaction is small and uncomfortable in a specific way she doesn't examine. She doesn't talk to the driver. She pays.

The "gets them home safely" hook turns: the thing Pace provides for free, she purchases, alone, at 2am, because she sent him away.

---

## The Overnight

**Home.** She cleans her face. She puts on Pace's shirt — not a decision, just the body doing what it does. The shirt is too large. She gets into bed.

**Can't sleep.** The overnight is sensation, not analysis. A specific image that keeps returning — not a thought but something physical from the dancing (the hand in the small of her back; the specific moment the frame stopped being a restriction and became something else). The wrongness of the room. She doesn't rehearse what she'll say. She doesn't plan. Planning is morning.

**Morning.** The shift happens off the page. She's wearing his shirt at 3am and the humiliation is hot and circular. Next paragraph: it's light, and the hot has cooled into cold and clear. *I have to fix this.* The night is the wrongness; morning is the decision.

---

## What This Plants

- **"Don't think, follow the lead"** — planted here (lesson), paid at the threesome. Said once. Not repeated.
- **The cab** — she sent away the man who would have gotten her home safely and paid for the alternative. One texture in Social Price #1.
- **The shirt** — private to the overnight. The body reaching for him while the mind is in the wrong place.
- **The debrief** — Vee arrives knowing she was wrong; this scene establishes exactly what she was wrong about and why the correction landed complete. No ambiguity to process. Only the plan for repair.
