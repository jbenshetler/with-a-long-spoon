# Notes — Rock

*Companion to the planned scene and its condensed (`meta-condensed-rock.md`). Holds the craft intent deliberately not on the page. For the surface beats, read the condensed.*

---

## What this scene is for

It plants **Peter** — the first time the true name touches the page — so the failed reach at First Weekend Back reads as a **regression** (she had the name, reaches for it on the cold step, and it won't travel), not a first appearance. The whole Pace/Peter load ("if Vee uses Peter it should land") needs this plant under it.

## The porch as the second liminal space

The porch is where truth surfaces **calm and unforced** — the structural opposite of the SATC restaurants, where Randi *extracts.* It does this twice: here, his name (his truth); in December's Porch Scene, Vee's displaced *I-love-you* (hers). Same room, opposite people, the tonal inverse of how Randi gets her truths. Render the calm; don't stage the contrast.

## How Peter surfaces — not extracted

In the flow of the ordinary "who are you" talk (born where, how many siblings — rendered only enough to host the name). It comes out because he's calm here, not because she digs. **Received, not confessed.** He gives it with a faint **set-aside** — *Peter* as someone he put down, not someone he hides; wistful, not defensive. The self under the name stays mostly withheld (never explain — the reader feels there's a whole self there without being told).

## Accepted decisions / surface materials

- **Draft status.** Drafted to `scenes/rock.md`; this note preserves the agreed decisions and intended beat logic for revision.
- **Opening physical facts.** Cold late-October night; Vee in Pace's robe, Pace in boxers; both under his quilt on the glassed-in porch couch. They drink **herbal tea** (not coffee, not green tea) while the den fireplace burns behind them; keep the fire behind the scene so the porch remains the glass/night/stars room.
- **Drink decision.** Herbal tea is the Rock drink. Wine was considered and rejected because it pulls toward meal/taste/seduction; coffee belongs to December's Porch Scene; green tea remains a general Pace-house prop but is not this scene's drink.
- **Food into family.** The talk starts from how different Pace's cooking is from Vee's childhood food: Ohio, generous conservative portions, casserole/gravy/potato/meat food that fed her but did not surprise her. Her mother's wedding-present spice rack: pepper, bay leaf, and cinnamon were the only jars she remembers being replaced; many paper seals stayed intact. This quietly mirrors Pace's sexual/courtship challenge without naming the parallel.
- **Vee family facts.** Vee is from Ohio. Her parents grew up in the same city and met in church. Her mother is a nurse and defaults to **Vivienne**, carrying propriety/formality and strict boundaries. Her father is an airplane mechanic for a major commercial airline, working on jet engines; he calls her **Vivienne** only when she is in trouble, otherwise **Vee**. Vee has one older brother, three years ahead, in the military and unmarried; there is no animosity, but they are not close.
- **Pace family facts.** His mother is a stay-at-home mother; his father works in a chemical plant. Do not define here whether Pace has siblings. His answers should be flat/factual and emotionally dry beside Vee's warmer family material.
- **Pace education facts.** He is twenty-two / 22–23 in this window, a math PhD candidate, and started college at fifteen after taking the SAT for a summer program and scoring into a scholarship path. Let Vee ask "How?" rather than front-loading credential exposition.
- **Pace school wound, light touch.** The seventh-grade long-division anecdote is available here: as backstory, he had taught himself algebra/trig because he wanted to plot fractals on the computer, but on the page keep it to a graphics program. His math teacher, not an anonymous school, made him move his desk into the hall each day during math class to do long-division worksheets because they did not know what to do with him. Vee should see the cruelty plainly without turning the scene maudlin; pivot to college as the place where math became joy.
- **Vee's response to the school wound.** She should name the cruelty without apology/therapy-speak: "They took the thing you loved and made it punishment." Pace receives it with contained precision, then the scene turns toward college as joy.
- **Math beauty.** Use the compressed examples, then Newton: a horn you can fill but never paint; a coastline/river boundary that gets longer the more carefully you measure it; laws of motion that make a thrown ball, leaves turning, and stars moving part of the same world. Vee can say she had never thought of it that way. Pace's point is that mathematics is the poetry/language of the universe — him seeing beauty others miss and trying to share a precious thing with someone he cares about, like showing her his favorite painting or song. He is immersed and carried by the beauty, but still with her.
- **Powerlifting language.** Keep the explanation concrete and bodily, not cosmic/abstract. Accepted phrasing: “When I step up to a squat bar with enough weight on it to bend the one-inch steel like dry spaghetti, there can be nothing in my mind but that moment. No future. No past. Only this lift, this heartbeat. And that is freeing.” The point is getting out of his head into the present moment.
- **Attention recognition.** Vee should connect the math/lifting presence to how Pace listens and touches her: he is entirely there, not planning the next clever thing, not wandering inside himself. Keep this low-heat and embodied, not thesis-y.
- **Scar deferral.** Include the small Step-1 scar deferral. With Pace in boxers under the quilt, Vee's hand can find the old hip/femur scar; she asks "This?"; he covers her hand and says "Not tonight." He does not move her away. She accepts the boundary. The spring Scar scene pays it off.

## The name permitted here — the riser this builds

She tries *Peter* softly and he **lets it sit.** The point: *Peter* is the name allowed **in the calm of the porch** — which is exactly what makes the spring stoop unbearable. At First Weekend Back she reaches for the porch-name in the wrong weather, on the cold step, and it has no purchase. The name didn't fail because she'd never used it; it failed because she'd only ever been *allowed* it here. (See `meta-note-first-weekend-back.md` → the "Peter" reach.)

## The resonance — bank it, never state it

The porch faces the **rock** of the mountains; **Peter means rock/foundation.** The true name surfaces in the one room that looks onto bedrock. Pure resonance — never put it on the page, never have anyone notice it.
