# Companion Notes — The Fitting

Two pieces that hang off the fitting scene: the plant it pays off, and the scene that should follow it.

---

## 1. The No-Tag Plant (goes EARLIER — wherever he first gives her the good shirt)

The fitting pays off a discovery — *no tag in it* — but the payoff only works if the absence was planted before, lightly, with no weight on it. The plant must read as a throwaway when she hits it; the reader (and Vee) should not file it as significant. It just sits there until the fitting detonates it.

Place it in the scene where he first gives her the good shirt (an early gift, before the fitting). Keep it to a beat — she notices, is briefly puzzled, supplies a wrong-category explanation, drops it. Her *wrong* guesses are the point: they're the same working-class wrong-category reflex as canned peaches and frozen chicken, and they make the real answer (he made it) land harder later.

**Draft beat to drop in:**

> Later, alone, she went looking for the label — to know what it was, where it was from, whether she could find another in a color she'd actually wear. She turned the collar back. Ran her thumb down the inside seam where the tag should be. Nothing. No little folded rectangle, no brand, no size, no wash symbols, not even the ghost of one cut out. She turned it fully inside out and found only the seams, which were better than the seams on anything she owned, flat and even and somehow *finished* in a way that nagged at her without resolving into a thought. Counterfeit, maybe — a knockoff good enough to skip the fake label. Or a sample, the kind that fell off a truck. She landed on nothing, because the only answer that fit the quality was one that hadn't occurred to her and wouldn't for weeks, and she put the shirt on and forgot the question the way you forget a question whose answer is standing somewhere you haven't thought to look.

Notes:
- The closing clause deliberately rhymes with the fitting's opening ("the way you forget a question whose answer is standing right in front of you"). The two echo each other across the gap — the warm house and the no-tag shirt are the same shape of un-asked question. Keep both, or vary one if the repetition reads as too neat.
- Don't let her guess "he made it." The whole value-jump depends on her never considering it until the fitting forces it.
- "Better than the seams on anything she owned" quietly seeds his competence so the reveal feels earned, not magical.

---

## 2. Scene Idea — Shoe Shopping with Randi (SATC, place RIGHT AFTER the fitting)

**Status:** Scene idea / to develop.

**One-line:** Randi insists on helping Vee find shoes for the new dress — and in doing so learns about the dress, and what it cost (nothing, in money; everything, in attention) to come by.

### What it does structurally

- **Feeds Randi the dress, and the jealousy.** This is the cleanest way for Randi to find out about the russet silk. And the datum that detonates her isn't the dress — it's *he made it.* That's the cruelest possible information for Randi, and it lands on the exact nerve the jar-realization already exposed: **tenderness/attention/effort is the load-bearing wall of what Vee feels, and it's the one thing Randi cannot manufacture.** Randi buys signifiers; Pace made the thing with his hands, in a shade he chose for Vee's coloring. Randi can match neither the gesture nor its meaning. Her jealousy here is not "he has a girlfriend" — it's "he gave her a kind of attention I have no way to give," and she covers it instantly and reaches for the one domain where she still outranks: taste, money, the shopping floor.
- **Establishes shopping-together as a format**, with Randi excelling, which sets up the later summer-dress dressing-room format-break (the money-as-power scene). This is the low-stakes warm-up: same activity, same power Randi wields (taste + money + the confident eye), but here it reads as generous friendship. On reread it's the first turn of the screw the dressing-room scene tightens.
- **Confidante invades the sacred object.** The dress was the most private, tender Pace-thing — a thing just between her and Vee. Bringing Randi in to accessorize it is Vee doing what she always does (tells Randi everything, the natural confider), but it should carry a faint cost the reader feels and Vee doesn't: Randi now has her hands on the dress, metaphorically, choosing what goes *with* it, inserting herself into the one space that was Pace's. Don't state the cost; let it sit under the warmth.

### Character levers

- **Vee's shoe philosophy is established and usable:** block heels she can actually walk in, the spindly version reads as trying-too-hard against her self-image, she tells herself she chose the sensible one for style. Randi pushes her *off* the sensible choice. This is a tiny version of the dressing-room trap — Randi steering Vee toward a self-image she didn't choose — but warm, deniable, "trust me, with that dress?"
- **Money tells, lightly.** Vee reads the prices; the shoes Randi gravitates to cost more than the dress would have if Vee had bought it. Vee's "money lightly worn" — she finds the good-enough pair at the place she can afford, restyles, has the eye if not the budget. Whether Randi offers to buy (and Vee's independence-pride refusing) is probably *too soon* — save the money-as-power detonation for the dressing room. Here Randi might just *want* to and not, or buy something small (a clutch, a pair of earrings) that Vee can't fully refuse, planting the dynamic without firing it.
- **The dress description, filtered through Randi's reaction.** We don't re-describe the dress; we watch Randi receive it. Her face managing itself. The beat where Vee says *he made it* and Randi goes still in the way she covers immediately (the jar-telling register — Randi off her instrument for a wordless beat = the tell the reader tracks). Then the recovery into shopping-expert mode, brighter than before, taking control of the outing.

### Physical-staircase opportunity (optional)

A shoe fitting is hands-on — Randi's hands on Vee's ankle, calf, the back of her heel; kneeling to do the strap (an inversion-rhyme with Pace kneeling at the fitting, which the reader can feel without it being stated — two people on their knees in front of Vee in adjacent scenes, one making something for her, one selling her a version of herself). The goodbye kiss can land here. But per the SATC rules, **don't force a physical beat into every scene** — if the dress/jealousy work is carrying the scene, a physical-staircase step may be spectacle creep. Decide based on where the last physical step landed.

### The retelling fold

This scene can double as the container for Vee retelling the fitting — but be careful what she can and can't tell. She'd tell the *dress* easily (funny, warm, "he MADE it, can you believe it"). The **secretions beat / the wet spot / him craning up to her face** — that's the thing she can't tell cleanly, the way bondage and tasting-herself resist clean telling. If she tries to tell the tender hinge and her voice changes, that's a bathroom-trip candidate, and Randi's question on return reopens it. So: the dress is the bright surface she hands over; the thing underneath (being seen at her most ashamed and not flinched from) is what leaks out sideways and gives Randi the real material. Same mechanism as always — Vee brings the funny version, the true version escapes her, Randi files it.

### Caution

Keep it warm and fun on first read — Randi genuinely good at this, the outing genuinely a pleasure, the friendship genuinely working. The cold is only in the pattern (Randi acquiring the dress as information, reasserting control after being out-gifted by a man she can't compete with on tenderness) and is invisible from inside the scene. Earn the dark by being light.
