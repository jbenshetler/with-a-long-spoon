## What it is

The **spring mirror-class**, the fall stats room's successor. Anthropology is almost too on-the-nose in the best way, so keep it buried exactly like the stats mirror.

## The binding (why it's this class, with these people)

Fall stats was **accidental** — Vee, Randi, and Cassie happened into one room. Spring anthro is **chosen**: Vee and Randi sign up together, on purpose. The accidental → chosen escalation is the point — Vee electing more Randi is the sealed room beginning at the level of her schedule.

Vee's schedule is freer, Randi's too. So **divergent majors, not a snub,** peel the truth-teller off Vee and pair Vee with the operator three afternoons a week — plus study sessions and group work. On reread: the schedule itself separated Cassie from Vee and handed Vee to Randi, and no one did a single wrong thing (the book's engine — *"it was all there,"* no legible villain).

Cassie stays the Cassandra **from the dorm**, watching the drift she can't stop — which sharpens the late *"you saw it and I didn't."*
- **Telegraph guard.** Never narrate "Vee was drifting from Cassie." On the page it's just: Vee and Randi have anthro this term; Cassie has clinical-track science. Warm on first read (friends taking a fun class); the contraction visible only in the pattern.

## The subjects (pick one per scene, plant, never gloss)

Vee reads the theory without seeing she's the case. The concepts that rhyme hardest with this plot:

- **Lévi-Strauss — the exchange of women.** Women circulated between men to bind them; Vee as the object trafficked toward the threesome. The devastating one.
- **Douglas — purity and danger / taboo.** The forbidden structuring desire; dirt as matter-out-of-place. Straight into the shame engine.
- **Mauss — the gift.** The gift that obligates, that must be reciprocated; Randi's "friendship" as the gift with a hook, the reciprocity trap. (Sahlins refines it: a *generalized-reciprocity* mask over *negative* reciprocity — taking without equivalent return.)
- **Van Gennep / Turner — liminality, rites of passage.** The initiand stripped of status in the threshold phase. Her whole year; the blindfold is the liminal veil.
- **Participant observation — the observer who is never neutral.** The ethnographer changes what she studies by joining it; Randi *participates* in Vee's life while steering it, Vee the unwitting informant. (Optional companion: "going native" — losing oneself in the culture one entered — for Vee absorbed into Pace's sealed world.)
