## What it is

The **spring mirror-class**, the fall stats room's successor. Same craft device — a course whose subject silently diagrams the plot, transcribed by Vee and connected to nothing (subtle, never explained; observation before cognition). The classroom vehicle turns over with the semester; the mirror *function* is carried all year. Where stats gave the **aggregate that lies** (Simpson's paradox — the whole reverses the parts, the deception forming), anthropology gives **exchange, taboo, ritual** — the consummation. Anthropology is almost too on-the-nose in the best way, so keep it buried exactly like the stats mirror.

## The binding (why it's this class, with these people)

Fall stats was **accidental** — Vee, Randi, and Cassie happened into one room. Spring anthro is **chosen**: Vee and Randi sign up together, on purpose. The accidental → chosen escalation is the point — Vee electing more Randi is the sealed room beginning at the level of her schedule.

- **Vee suggests it.** She floats and embraces the shared class; Randi simply accepts, her calculation withheld — Randi stays clean, Vee delivers herself (she keeps unknowingly authoring her own trap).
- **No Cassie — and blamelessly.** Cassie is pre-nursing; her sciences-locked track (bio, chem, A&P) had her clear her one humanities elective early — **she already did it, with the oddball Dr. Wardy** — so she has no reason to be in the spring anthro section. (In `all-the-time.md` Vee reports it in one offhand line: *"already did hers, with that oddball Wardy."*) So **divergent tracks, not a snub** — the schedule itself peels the truth-teller off Vee and pairs Vee with the operator three afternoons a week, plus study sessions and group work. On reread: the schedule separated Cassie from Vee and handed Vee to Randi, and no one did a single wrong thing (the book's engine — *"it was all there,"* no legible villain).
- **The cost, and why it's fine.** We lose an anthro-room group-project Cassandra beat (as `outlier.md` gave in stats). That device already fired once, memorably; repeating it would dull it. Cassie stays the Cassandra **from the dorm**, watching the drift she can't stop — which sharpens the late *"you saw it and I didn't."*
- **Telegraph guard.** Never narrate "Vee was drifting from Cassie." On the page it's just: Vee and Randi have anthro this term; Cassie has clinical-track science. Warm on first read (friends taking a fun class); the contraction visible only in the pattern.

## The subjects (pick one per scene, plant, never gloss)

Vee reads the theory without seeing she's the case. The concepts that rhyme hardest with this plot:

- **Lévi-Strauss — the exchange of women.** Women circulated between men to bind them; Vee as the object trafficked toward the threesome. The devastating one.
- **Douglas — purity and danger / taboo.** The forbidden structuring desire; dirt as matter-out-of-place. Straight into the shame engine.
- **Mauss — the gift.** The gift that obligates, that must be reciprocated; Randi's "friendship" as the gift with a hook, the reciprocity trap. (Sahlins refines it: a *generalized-reciprocity* mask over *negative* reciprocity — taking without equivalent return.)
- **Van Gennep / Turner — liminality, rites of passage.** The initiand stripped of status in the threshold phase. Her whole year; the blindfold is the liminal veil.
- **Participant observation — the observer who is never neutral.** The ethnographer changes what she studies by joining it; Randi *participates* in Vee's life while steering it, Vee the unwitting informant. (Optional companion: "going native" — losing oneself in the culture one entered — for Vee absorbed into Pace's sealed world.)
