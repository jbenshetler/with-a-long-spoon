# Note — Water Wings (Vee's body in the world; the desirability anchor)

*Scene · Architecture complete, prose not drafted · early fall, just after The Long Way (before May I Choose) · Vee POV · Vee + Cassie · companion to `meta-condensed-water-wings.md` · anchors the desirability thread (`meta-note-desirability-thread.md`)*

## What this scene is

A warm Vee-and-Cassie afternoon at the university pool that does three jobs at once, none of them announced: it establishes a **non-mother source** for Vee's sense that her body is "too much"; it plants the **involuntary, objective anchor** of the desirability thread (third parties want her while she's doing nothing to invite it); and it primes the Fitting (a few weeks on), where Pace looks and *doesn't* leer — the payoff to the wound planted here.

Pace is not in it, and barely in her head — she's only just met him. Randi isn't in it either. This is the one early scene with **no erotic-plot stake in the room** — pure neutral third parties plus the one friend who has no agenda — which is exactly why it can prove, objectively, that Vee is desirable.

## Why the pool (the involuntary anchor)

Vee's desirability elsewhere is attested mainly by Pace and Randi — the two who want to sleep with her, so **compromised witnesses**. This scene supplies the neutral proof: strangers react to her body *while she is trying to disappear inside it*. Unmanaged desirability is the objective kind — she can't be accused of fishing — and it's the opposite of Randi's curated version (the un-molded against the molded). Keep every reaction **involuntary and ambient** (see guards); the moment anyone crosses to her it reads creepy and the warmth dies. Full thread coordination: `meta-note-desirability-thread.md`.

## The deck / water structure

The scene argues its theme with its geography.

- **The deck** (locker room, the walk out): the world's verdict. Baggy clothes gone, the body unhideable in a one-piece; the whistles and the holler; her practiced armor. Here the body is "too much," public property, a problem to manage.
- **The water:** not the opposite of the gaze (the gaze is right there on the deck) but something older and prior — below.

## The water = first love, and the exile (render as resonance, never exposition)

Vee loved the water before she had a body — before "too much" was a category. Then the body came in and **exiled her from synchronized swimming**, a looked-at sport whose competitive ideal her build couldn't use (canon: "burdened out of competition"). So the same abundance that draws the leers on the deck is what *cost her the thing she loved* — one body, punished coming and going: too womanly for the sport to keep, too much for the world to leave alone.

But the **water itself survived the exile.** She lost the sport; she didn't lose the water. So the fade-out is a **homecoming** to the first love, from before any of it — with a thread of grief for the synchro her body took. This is *not* "escape from the gaze" (that would make it reactive, subordinate to the leers) and *not* a display of prowess (redundant with the trophies, and it would steal the spring **Deep End**'s expert-inversion). She cuts no impressive stroke. She just sinks into the old love. Keep the exile a **felt undercurrent**, never explained — body before mind.

## The five beats

1. **Cassie grumbles into the required suit** — the warm entry, and the clean contrast: Cassie indifferent to exposure (body-for-use; nobody looks and she wouldn't care), Vee unable to escape it.
2. **The body is "too much" — the world's verdict.** At the mirror / on the deck: the "stripper's body if she let the word be said," hidden since fourteen; the wish to be **sleek like Randi**, rendered as wanting to *disappear* (sleek = unremarkable, manageable), never as ranking herself below Randi (canon: she doesn't buy Randi's grading). Source the "too much" to **the world and the exile**, not the mother.
3. **The catcalls are the proof; Cassie is the solidarity — she does *not* appraise Vee.** Vee walks the gauntlet leered-at and *annoyed*, not insecure — eyeroll, a long blink with half-pursed mouth, practiced armor; **she does not feel unattractive here, she feels leered-at.** The objective desirability is carried by the involuntary third-party want itself (the men reacting), not by anyone telling her she's desirable. Cassie's line — *"Tossers. They will never know the love of a woman."* — is a **dismissal of the men, kept low on purpose**: she's a habitual bleacher-shouter (rec league) who *chooses* not to make a scene Vee would like even less, so the restraint is the gift. Vee laughs (small joke — but the game face breaks and fifty yards of held breath go out on it) and feels Cassie on her side. **NB (resolved):** the desirability thread's female *seal* has been **moved off Cassie** — she reads asexual (no desiring standpoint, so her word is a great-aunt's, not a seal) and here only *dismisses* the tossers. The seal is reassigned to a **straight, worldly dorm friend (Kayla)** in a later dorm beat (`meta-note-desirability-thread.md`); Water Wings anchors the thread on the **ambient leers alone**, Cassie supplying solidarity, not verdict.
   - **The holler — line options (crude/admiring, annoying not creepy).** A faceless bit performed for his friends, landing *near* her, not *at* her — she's the punchline, the laugh is what stings and also what removes the threat. Candidates: **"You won't need floaties with those!"** (or *"She's got her own flotation devices"*) — the pool-native flotation gag, groan not menace, evokes the title's buoyancy half from the crowd; the plainer **"She's stacked."**; **"Legs for days!"** — the corny timeless idiom, safe-by-cornyness; and **"Lifeguard, I'm drowning over here!"** — the whole-body swoon bit, pool-logic, admires the effect without naming a part. Not all get used.
   - **Guard — the crowd never says "water wings."** Keep the holler an adjacent flotation cousin (*floaties / flotation devices*), never the title phrase itself. If the crude guy owns "water wings," he colonizes the title and the tender half (buoyancy-as-grace, the wings, the homecoming) gets retroactively contaminated into the leer. The two meanings must never touch on the page: crude flotation from the deck, angelic lightness in the water, and the *reader* (never the prose) fuses them in the title.
4. **She sinks into the water** — the first love, still hers, the grief under it. One held, solitary beat; let it breathe.
5. **Cassie splashes her out of it; Vee splashes back, laughing** — the water as *play* (the child-joy that predates even the sport, the one thing the body couldn't take), alive and shared. Fade on two friends being idiots in the water, the body-as-problem left on the deck.

## Guards

- **Leers stay ambient, never an approach.** Whistles and looks from across the water — the world's noise, at a distance, armored against. Nobody crosses the deck to her (that reads creepy and turns her deflection defensive; the specific *bid* lives at Jitterbug, a venue built for being approached).
- **"Too much" = the world, not the mother.** The mother owns the prim register elsewhere; this scene exists for the *other* source.
- **The water is homecoming — not escape, not prowess.** No stroke-display; no "no one's looking here." The old love, the grief, the shared play.
- **The splash is Cassie-care as Cassie** — she clocks her friend drift somewhere sad and, rather than ask, splashes her in the face. Wordless (or one dry word). Friendship-level perception only — no Cassandra, no reading the synchro grief.
- **Keep it real, not saccharine** — dumb, specific, a hard splash and a shriek-laugh. **The play *routes* the grief, it doesn't coexist with it** — you cannot grieve and fight your best friend for a mouthful of pool at once; the splashing wins. The sadness relocates to the *reader* (who just watched the held underwater beat); Vee's face is pure idiot-joy. Undertow lives only in **resonance** (e.g. "back before any of it"), never in stated melancholy on her face. Ending unshadowed is deliberate: this is the Cassie-friendship high-water mark, and pre-shadowing it would spend the coming loss early.
- **Pace stays out** (barely met; the water is the in-scene meaning-giver, Pace the downstream one). POV close third on Vee.
