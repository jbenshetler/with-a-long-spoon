# Vivienne — Craft Reference

*On-page writing rules for Vee. For structural architecture, see `meta-arch-vivienne.md`.*
*Open this file before writing any Vee scene.*
*To verify a completed draft, run `meta-rules.md`.*

---

## POV and Reader Identification

The reader inhabits her. Her interior should be felt *with* her, not observed from outside. Architecture is honored; moment-to-moment emotional weather is discovered in scene. Never let a scene place Vee at a distance the reader can feel safe from — her hunger is the one most reflective readers carry; the recognition is part of what the novel installs.

---

## Voice and Register

**Baseline:** warm, quick, fluent, genuinely smart (more academically verbal than Randi), funny in a self-aware deflecting way.

**Under pressure, her voice regresses down — not up.** Smaller voice, simpler words, shorter sentences, younger syntax. NOT sophisticated vocabulary or academic precision (that is Pace's register, never Vee's defense). The regression is the master tell: plainest/smallest speech = most threatened; most articulate = safest.

**Her signature deflection:** preemptive self-exposure as a joke — naming the embarrassing thing first to control it (e.g. joking about her own hard nipples before anyone else can).

**Per relationship:**
- *With Cassie:* unguarded-bawdy; deflection relaxed; the truest register.
- *With Randi:* warmth-pressure regresses her; the curated script collapses; the true voice is *taken* by pressure.
- *With Pace:* the deflection fires reflexively as a vulnerability-flag but meets *may I* not attack, so it fails to be necessary. The reader learns his safety through the deflection's failure. True voice is *drawn out* by safety. Regression fires hard early and recedes with trust; never fully gone.

**The mother in dialogue:** audible ONLY as prim displaced judgment of other women's sexual laxity (*floozy* and kin — anachronistic, inherited). The self-directed verdict stays interior. Never have Vee reach up for armor under threat; the wound strips fluency, it does not summon it.

## Behavioral Signatures

- Body responds before mind permits. Render response, then cognition — never cognition first.
- **Tell:** hand to collarbone when something surprises her emotionally.
- Goes still and inward when processing something real.
- When she meets something real she has no toolkit for, she makes a joke.
- Extends grace before it's earned. (This same quality stops her from becoming a stalker; drives toward his neighborhood once, turns back.)
- **The snide/prudish commentary** about "girls like that" — her cruelest judgments are about exactly what she secretly wants. Used sparingly. Gone by novel's end — not announced, just absent.
- Primary coping mechanism for things that don't fit: a cultural explanation (*"sorority thing"*) that files the feeling without examining it. She notices she's doing it; the writing-off is active, not passive.

## The Most Defended Nerve — Involuntary Sexual Signals

Vee's shame concentrates on the involuntary tells — wetness, hard nipples, flush, the arousal that announces itself without her consent. These betray the respectable self with no deciding-them-away.

**Hierarchy:** withheld/deepest = the wet spot; extractable/surface = the nipples.

Every key scene presses this nerve:
- The deflection supplies a non-sexual alibi for an involuntary signal
- The secretions reframe lands because Pace meets the most-defended involuntary signal with gladness
- Randi's extraction works by stripping the alibi and leaving the involuntary reaction standing as witnessed appetite

When any scene touches Vee's involuntary responses, it is touching the live wire. Calibrate the regression, the deflection, and the shame-arousal loop accordingly.

## The Arousal–Humiliation Loop

Central. Render it whenever Vee's body is in play. **Pace defuses the humiliation step; Randi amplifies it.** Both produce intense arousal because both engage her core mechanism — in opposite directions. She does not perceive the coordination. *(Key instances: `the-fitting.md` for the secretions reframe; `brunch-fitting-randi.md` for Randi's extraction of that same material.)*

## The Cutting Voice — How to Render It

The voice cuts her; she does not narrate it as something she is running. Render at the experience level: *it cuts her, it predicts condemnation, it produces shame on demand when her body responds.* Let the reader feel it as something Vee is subject to, not something she is performing. The architectural truth (she is running it) sits below the experience-level rendering.

---

## Bravery on the Page

Render brave moments as the appetite reaching for the not-fine — beautiful, real, brave in the conventional sense. **First read: virtue. Second read: appetite-in-motion.** Do not signal the second register explicitly; it must emerge from the architecture operating.

## The Randi Attraction

Conscious and growing from early in the novel. Render it as part of Vee's interior from the start. The kiss does not reveal feelings she hadn't known about. The kiss reveals architecture — the fact of collaboration — not feeling. **The catastrophe is epistemic, not emotional.**

## The SATC Scenes

High-intensity BDSM rendered as lunch conversation. Randi is dominant and sadistic, fed by jealousy and the six-channel week. The conversational edging is real edging. The bathroom breaks are part of the scene. Render them with appropriate weight — they are not just friendship scenes with some heat; they are the S operating on the M in the only register available outside the bedroom.

## Physical Description — Reminders

**The Art Nouveau archetype — not a prison, a grammar.** The organizing visual logic of Vee's self-presentation is Art Nouveau: tall, lush, uncontained, vital natural energy constantly pushing against social rules she's conflicted about. Think Klimt, Mucha — gold leaf, florals, sinuous curves, abundance that cannot be disciplined into geometry. The archetype names what was already in the prose; use it to calibrate new scenes.

- *Lush*, not voluptuous. Abundant, ripe, alive — command and abundance in the same frame.
- Tall, full breasts, deep waist, hips with real width. Moves like an athlete under the abundance — low center, real balance. Synchronized swimmer's control, burdened out of competition by a build the competitive ideal can't use.
- Shoulder-length hair worn loose, warm dark red, the highlights sparking to copper only in direct sun. The wind device: the same wind that finds almost nothing to move on Randi's bound ponytail lifts Vee's hair the whole time, playing with it.
- Snub nose tipped up, chestnut freckles across nose and tops of cheeks. These features — the snub nose, the freckles, the uncurated hair — are the anti-curation made flesh. They cannot be bought or disciplined. Randi's grammar finds nothing in her surface to grade.
- Dress: soft drapey things, denim jacket, canvas bag slung careless, scuffed boots. The surface offers the sorority grammar nothing. Not anti-fashion — indifferent to the grammar. She wears what she likes.
- Jewelry: something she saved up for or was gifted — recognizable quality without a box. A Tiffany pendant is right: not a matched set, not a signature system, just the one good thing she has. It stays on during the fitting; Pace doesn't comment on it.

**Scent — no signature perfume.** The Art Nouveau register wants her to announce herself — *she wants people to smell her coming* — but the cutting voice files strong sweet florals under her mother's verdict (*floozy*, that register), and Vee has been filing them there too. The perfume she wants is not the perfume she wears. What she actually smells like: clean skin, whatever she used that morning (something drugstore, something that fades by afternoon), the specific smell of her hair discovered only at close range. Pace knows her smell; he cannot name it as a product.

She may own something warm and sweet she almost never wears — a bottle gifted or found on sale, sitting on her dresser. She puts it on twice a year when she feels bold. The mother's voice fires. She usually takes it off. After the year, maybe she stops taking it off. This is author-knowledge, not prose material.

**Keep scent as a discovered detail, not a fixed announcement.** It arrives because she is close, not because she prepared it. This contrast with Randi — who arrives pre-constructed, Eau des Sens already on — is sharper than any floral signature would be. Reserve scent for specific scenes where closeness earns it; the field is most useful open.

## Social World

Vee and Cassie are **unaffiliated** — not in any sorority, never attending as sisters, never inside that social world. This never wavers. The status gap with Randi is cross-world and load-bearing: for the flattery-on-surface/targeting-on-reread structure; for the money choreography (Vee always pays her own way, stretching for venues she can't afford); for the Cassie/Randi disclosure-sort being a world-sort as well as a competence-sort.

## What the Prose Must Hold — The Three Commitments

**1. The goods of the year are real, in real time.** The sex, the becoming, the friendship, the bravery — rendered at full force as the goods they were. Do not let the prose pre-emptively contaminate them with the future reveal. Vee was not naive to value them. *(The fitting as template for this: `the-fitting.md`.)* When the reader reaches the reveal, the contamination is retrospective only. If the prose tilts toward *Vee should have refused the year in advance,* the argument collapses.

**2. The no is the equipment working, not damage.** She walks out ascendant and hungry. The no is part of the gains. If the prose tilts toward the no reading as trauma or ingratitude, the argument collapses.

**3. There is no free lunch.** Do not let the prose suggest Vee will find a healthy relationship that surpasses what she had with Pace. The fine man will not engage her shame as fuel. The real friend will not be the substrate of the intensity Randi was. The hunger is permanent. Her future is honest about this.

---

## The Parallel-and-Opposite Structure — The Discipline

The chiasmus between Vee's transformation and Randi's is real, architectural, and **must remain invisible in the prose.** Vee cannot see what is happening in Randi. Do not have Vee notice the structure. Do not have the narrator surface it. Do not let any character articulate it. The reader feels the structure from outside two interiors that cannot see each other at this depth. If the structure surfaces in dialogue or narrator-asserted observation, it flips from earned-and-true to designed-and-imposed. This is the most important single discipline for protecting the architecture against literary risk.

---

## The Cracker-and-Meal Image

The writer's tool for understanding Vee's interior across the year. **Does not appear in the prose** — with one exception: Vee speaks the figure once, whole, in displacement as her *I love you* at the porch scene. *(→ `porch-scene.md`.)* That is the sole prose appearance. Everywhere else it lives only in the architecture.

---

## The Closing Image

Small, undecorated, in motion. Contains the whole without announcing any of it: the gains, the no, the hunger, the ascent, the knowledge. She is walking forward into something. The specific image is a craft decision. The architectural commitment: it carries all of this, announces none of it.
