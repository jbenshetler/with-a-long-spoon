# Condensed — Water Wings

*SCENE · Architecture complete, prose not drafted · early fall, just after {{The Long Way}} (before {{May I Choose}}) · Vee POV · Vee + Cassie*

A warm Vee-and-Cassie pool afternoon doing three quiet jobs: a **non-mother source** for the body being "too much"; the **involuntary anchor** of the desirability thread (neutral third parties want her while she's doing nothing to invite it — the objective proof Pace and Randi, as compromised witnesses, can't give); and a **setup for the Fitting** (the leer-wound Pace's non-leering gaze later dissolves). **Deck vs. water:** on the deck the body is unhideable in a one-piece, the world's "too much" — whistles, a holler, her practiced armor; the wish to be "sleek like Randi" rendered as wanting to *disappear*, never as ranking herself lower. In the water: not escape-from-gaze (the gaze is on the deck) but **homecoming** — she loved the water before she had a body, and the same abundance that draws the leers is what **exiled her from synchro** (the competitive ideal her build couldn't use); she lost the sport, not the water, so the fade carries the first love and a thread of grief. Five beats: Cassie grumbles into the required suit (indifferent to exposure — the clean contrast) → the body as the world's "too much" → Cassie dismisses the leers — solidarity, not a seal (the female seal reassigned to a later Kayla dorm beat) → she sinks into the first love, the grief under it (a held beat) → **Cassie splashes her out of it, Vee splashes back laughing** — the water as *play*, alive and shared, fade on the body-problem left on the deck. Guards: leers **ambient, never an approach** (a crossing reads creepy; the specific bid lives at {{Jitterbug}}); "too much" = the world / the exile, not the mother; the water is homecoming, **not** escape and **not** prowess (protects the spring Deep End); the exile stays resonance, never exposition; the splash is Cassie-care-as-horseplay, wordless; Pace out.
