## Guardrails

- **Vee's vantage only, in the order her body meets it.** Drop the opening's material — Pace's design-pride and the construction exposition. Her scene is shorter than the opening and is all perception-in-sequence, not a furniture catalog. (The disguised table *as her half-noticed glance* is fine — that's her vantage; what's dropped is the opening's Pace-POV account of building it.)
- **No narrator analysis.** The text never explains the apparatus or names the humiliation. Reader supplies the design; prose supplies the body. (Don't telegraph; don't editorialize.)
- **The reveal (detailed in Opening):** Pace lifts the wooden top off while she watches — she sees a man uncovering a strange table; the reader sees the bench return (the ritual that closed the opening, "tomorrow he'd put the top back on").
- **The game is honest; the plan is not.** Vee is in on the *game* (the mutual play), never the *configuration*. The bedroom transparency must not bleed into any awareness of the larger arrangement.
