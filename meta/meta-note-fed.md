## Where it sits

The Randi/Vee beat that immediately follows {{Famished}} (within a few days). Verbal-staircase rung #4. The physical goodbye-kiss staircase debuted in {{How It's Done}} (the surprise); this scene is its **2nd** occurrence — the recognition rung, one step past surprise: Vee half-expects it and meets it, and the debut's witness-scan does *not* fire (rendered behaviorally only, never as Vee thinking the kiss is "different now"). ## Setting — the sun porch, and the mimosa

A bistro with a glassed sun porch, off-campus. Deliberate inversion of the venue progression: {{How It's Done}} ran inside the velvet alcove (enclosed, three-sided, dark, conspiratorial); this scene — the warmest rung, carrying the cruelest operation — happens in full daylight. The dark is invisible from inside the warm, and an open, bright porch refuses the dread an enclosed booth would telegraph. The light also does craft work: it makes Vee's flushes (the sheet-flush, the color-flush) legible, which is the natural cover for Randi reading the tell *off her face* — see the hard guard on the color facet.

**Mimosas install here as the recurring off-campus brunch image** ("at least one"), varied scene to scene going forward. Vee orders one — mimicry of Randi in a register she can see and approve of (acceptable copying that belongs to the same machine as the verbal copying she can't see). The money beat rides on it without a word of bank-balance interior: she runs the established right-side-of-the-menu-first gesture (how-its-done.md), and where the Rombauer in {{How It's Done}} was a wall (soup and water instead), the mimosa is the price point she *clears* — the cheap glamour, buying in at her level. Pride, not pinch. Same body-move, opposite outcome; marks how far in she now is. Guard: **Vee pays her own way** — no question of Randi treating, which keeps the dressing-room dress-offer the first deployment of Randi's money against Vee. The planted mimosa-taste-on-the-goodbye-kiss cousin (meta-plan-satc-tracks.md) is *available* once the motif exists but is **not** played here; this kiss is continuation, no new riser.

## The inversion engine

Randi's openers always run thematic prep that Vee's own story then hits. Here the resonance is an inversion on two axes:

- **Anticipation vs. body.** Randi's opener is *anticipation > body*: rose-colored glasses, the romanced memory, the dinner up front, and then the body shows up a disappointment. Vee's First Night is the mirror — *body > anticipation*: she'd been sleeping in the flannel proxy ("a week-old version, stored and approximate") and the real thing exceeded it ("She'd thought she knew it. She'd been wrong in the best possible way," famished.md). Randi got the wanting and lost the thing; Vee got the thing and it beat the wanting. That mirror is what arms the conversion: Randi can land "you got the rare thing" because her own story just showed how seldom it happens.
- **The anti-Pace.** "Nervous and in such a rush" is the photographic negative of "meticulous and unhurried." The cold engine: Randi gets Pace's report, then collects Vee's version — the one thing Pace can't give her, Vee's interior. "Tell me everything" is intelligence work wearing warmth.

**Dinner-sequence is the hinge that links the opener to the catch.** Randi's man gave her dinner *ahead of time* (front-loaded romance the body then disappointed). Vee's sheet detail reveals dinner came *after* (the sated afterglow the body had already earned). So Randi's "at least I got dinner ahead of time" plants the very axis — *when was dinner?* — that the sheet-catch then springs. The opener arms the trap. Build it so it's there on reread; neither of them says it.

## Fabricated men (behind-curtain truth)

Randi's brunch-story men are invented or composite — chimeras of men she's known plus sorority gossip. This is a **writer's truth** governing how freely her stories may vary; the **text keeps "real or imagined, NEVER resolved"** (meta-plan-satc-tracks.md) for Vee and the reader. The page never settles it.

Register guard: her stories stay in the loud/rueful anecdote key and **never drift toward Pace's fingerprint** (tenderness, edging-and-watching-her-face, attentive restraint, consent-architecture). The rueful-ex story is safe by construction because "nervous and in a rush" is the negative of Pace — but keep any *good* part of it out of his key. The ex stays a disappointment; never accidentally tender.

## Beat spine

1. Randi's opener — the rueful fabricated ex (warms the room, sets candor-as-currency, runs the inversion prep).
2. Handoff to Vee → Vee **raves**: the Cassie-clean version (the dinner whole), topped with the sheet flourish.
3. The sheet flourish springs the catch.
4. The premeditation surfaces in facets — toes, the color, the good panties — caught *warm*, not interrogated.
5. Randi gives the platform → Vee describes how good it was, in Pace's true fingerprint (the line Randi takes clean).
6. Randi steers → **Vee speaks her own seal** (calibrated; see below).
7. Goodbye kiss out.

## Randi's opener (candidate lines, author)

Ran into an old boyfriend on campus. "I thought, he's so good-looking, why did we break up?" Went on a date, then slept with him. "The boy tries, but he's nervous and in such a rush! At least I got dinner ahead of time and anticipation through some rose colored glasses." Handoff: "That smile you're failing to hide tells me your date went better than mine. Give me something juicy, gorgeous, so I can live vicariously through you."

"Live vicariously through you" is the sharpest double-register line: first read, a glamorous friend egging on the younger one, self-deprecating, clean; reread, she has zero need to live vicariously — she gets it direct, and doubled. Keep it; it isn't a wink because the surface meaning is complete and warm.

## Vee's clean version + the sheet flourish

Vee gives the same version she gave Cassie — the dinner whole: the apron, garlic and pepper blooming, the patient complete attention, the fra diavolo, the Chianti opened before she came (toenails.md, where she "lifted it out clean as she went, the way a ladle takes soup from the pot and the surface closes without a hole" and Cassie let it stand). She tops it with the flourish she's proudest of, the thing she thinks seals "most romantic":

> "and after I sat in the kitchen wrapped only in a bedsheet watching him cook me dinner."

She volunteers it believing it's safe and cozy. It smuggles in everything she lifted out for Cassie: the nakedness, the sex, and the *sequence* (dinner came after). She hands Randi the architecture inside the detail she was proudest of.

**{{Toenails}} rhyme and inversion.** With Cassie the dinner is the safe, whole thing she *can* give, and Cassie lets it close the door ("He cooked." "A man made you dinner." "Mm."). Randi uses the dinner's own logic to open it. Same detail, opposite confidante: one lets it stand, one walks through it.

This also fires the no-management-gap engine exactly: narrating a present-tense bodily fact (*I sat there in only a sheet*) to another woman in daylight re-enters the self-watching she didn't have in the moment. The flush isn't Randi shaming her — it's Vee hearing her own image land as *floozie in a man's sheet* the second it's out of her mouth.

## The catch (candidate line, author)

Replaces the bullshit-look. Truer to Randi's voice — she names things rather than withholds (cf. the wordless "cold... hmm" in {{How It's Done}}, a device used and now varied).

> "Ooo, that's sexy. I love the image. But I noticed that you skipped the part explaining how you ended up wearing only his bedsheet."

"Ooo, that's sexy, I love the image" does the earn-the-light work first (warm, fun, complicit); then the catch points at the *gap* without naming the content. The silence after still does the filling.

## Premeditation facets — caught warm, not interrogated

The shame escalates here from First Night's involuntary register ("she felt her nipples before he did," her mother's *floozie*) to **premeditation**: she consented in advance and dressed for the verdict. That is the fresh indignity, a genuine new riser, not a re-light. The facets are three faces of that one thing, deepening — and each uses a *different mechanism* so the run doesn't read as one repeating gesture: **catch → self-betrayal → name**. The catch (toes) is light — Randi spotting what Vee half-hid. The self-betrayal (color) is the deep interior exposure, gravity fully on Vee as she convicts herself; "you're lucky" warms it back down. The naming (panties) then externalizes the premeditation into words plain enough for Vee to *own* — which is what hands the run into the conversion and the seal. Keep all three; varying the mechanism, not cutting, is what keeps it from reading as point-by-point prying.

- **The sheet** → the sex happened, and the sequence (dinner after).
- **Toes (Randi's line: "You did those Friday. For him.").** *Mechanism: catch* — quick and light. Randi asks whether Vee did anything to get ready for him; Vee volunteers her nails, then, because she can't not, her toes. Randi catches what the toes mean: you paint your fingers for the world, your toes for a man who'll have your feet bare in his hands. It exposes that she anticipated being undressed. (First-Night grooming is toes — toenails frosted plum under ballet flats, famished.md.) Keep the logic unspoken; Vee volunteering "And my toes," Randi's "fingers for the room" / "toes for a man" catch, "You did those Friday. For him.", and Vee's flush carry it. Never explain *why* toes mean what they mean.
- **The color (Vee names it easily, then can't say *why* — Randi reads it off her).** *Mechanism: self-betrayal* — Randi catches nothing; Vee convicts herself. Asked the color, Vee answers plainly (*"Frosted plum."*); it's the follow-up — *why did you pick it?* — that ambushes her. She picked the frosted plum because it woke the plum hiding under the deep red of the silk Pace held to her face in the sun porch; she "noticed, then put the connection away, then came back to it twice before the polish dried" (famished.md), and re-notices *why* mid-question, in front of Randi. She has the answer and can't give it; it comes up in her face instead. Randi: *"That face isn't about a color, is it."* Names that it's loaded without naming *what*; the content stays unspoken (the silk connection is Vee's and the reader's, not Randi's), Vee's flush carrying the rest, the reader half a step ahead.
  - **Hard guard:** Randi reads "it means something" **off Vee's face, never off any knowledge of the silk.** She's sleeping with Pace; if the line lands as Randi *recognizing the color*, it leaks the connection and the deception cracks. Pin it to Vee's tell, not to the fact.
- **The good panties (Randi's line).** *Mechanism: name* — the one real verbal escalation, where Randi says the premeditation out loud, and the last facet because naming is what makes it ownable — it hands straight into the seal. Vee bought new ones for the night and moved the green satin to the top of the drawer that morning, "told herself none of this meant anything in particular" (famished.md). Randi lands it as a *campaign*: *"You didn't buy those hoping he'd be a gentleman. You bought them hoping he'd give you no reason to keep them on."* Warm/teasing on the surface; on reread, taking inventory of premeditated desire.

**What Vee keeps:** she does **not** tell Randi about the panties trade ("Trade.", famished.md). That stays Vee's.

## The rave — Pace's fingerprint, taken clean

Randi gives the platform ("okay — but how was he?") and lets Vee talk herself from mortification into pride.

> patient, gentle, hungry for me in a way that made me feel unique — not just for tonight

- Faithful to First Night ("meticulous and unhurried," "would you be bothered if I was too hard?", the hunger she could feel landing). Texture it as the same night. It is also the precise inversion of the opener's nervous, self-focused ex.
- **Hardest discipline beat in the scene.** "Hungry for me in a way that made me feel unique" is the envy-of-capacity trigger; the water-glass tell would fire here for anyone less controlled. Per Flag #13 it does **not** fire — Randi is operating, hands easy. She takes the one line built to wound her (Vee describing *Randi's own lover's gift* as if it were Vee's alone, "not just for tonight," when it is precisely *not* unique) completely clean. The restraint is the characterization.
- "Made me feel unique, not just for tonight" is a quiet **plant for the reveal**. Leave it as resonance; do not press it.

## The conversion — drawn from Vee, calibrated down

Randi does not pronounce a thesis. She asks the question whose only answer is the proud thing, then confirms ("there it is"), and **Vee speaks her own seal**. Why: no telegraph (a Randi thesis-line signals the author); it sticks (a reframe Vee thinks she reached herself she will defend); and it is the verbal-track version of Pace's method — Pace gets Vee's *body* to author its response, Randi gets Vee's *mind* to author its permission.

**Altitude (calibration — the load-bearing constraint).** Scope the seal to the **social** shame of this one night.

- **Drafted seal:** *"I wanted it. And I took it. For once."* Mirrors Randi's reframe ("not a thing that happened to you, a thing you did") — Vee adopts the agency frame. "And I took it" is the ceiling: agency/ownership, brushing the forbidden sovereign-self-possession line; **"For once" is what caps it** — this night as the exception, not a method learned or a permanent new self. Randi's confirm is *"Good,"* with a hand laid over Vee's (approval-as-reward; the reward mechanism in one syllable, not a pronounced thesis).
- **Too high (cut):** any insight that her shame is *fuel*; any sense she's learned a *method*; sovereign self-possession; and — critically — any lucidity that Randi is operating.
- **Leave the deep shame live.** Don't surface the transgressive/body layer to convert it. Vee walks out lighter about being *a woman who wanted a man*, the body-shame entirely untouched and waiting. Durable, physiological ownership of shame-as-fuel is the later staircase (it first fires in retelling at {{The Practice Room}}), not this rung. The cruelty stays invisible-in-the-pattern, never loaded into this scene.

## Pacing guard

The facets in a row (sheet → toes → color → panties) can tip into *Randi systematically prying*, which reads sinister and kills the warmth and the arousal. Two things hold it: the varied mechanism (catch → self-betrayal → name, the deepest gravity landing mid-run on Vee convicting herself and the whole beat resolving into Vee's own seal, not Randi's verdict), and the release built in — **Vee wants to tell** ("the wanting to give it, finally, to set it down on the table," how-its-done.md). Play it as Vee tumbling out her joy and Randi delightedly receiving and occasionally catching, not Randi extracting point by point. Fallback if it still feels like an interrogation in draft: fold toes + color into a single grooming beat.
