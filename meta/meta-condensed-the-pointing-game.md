# Condensed — The Pointing Game

*SCENE (DIPTYCH) · Draft complete · `the-pointing-game.md` · day after the Walk of Shame*

## Summary

A two-panel scene, Pace POV throughout, one continuous afternoon. Panel one (bed): post-coital, he draws out her fantasy and finds the live wire. Panel two (campus walk): the same act in daylight, reconnaissance wearing the costume of a game. The plan's origin and its first execution rendered as a single dramatic movement.

## Key Beats — Panel One (Bed)

Post-coital, his mouth still on her taste. He kisses her up into the warm disorder; she tastes herself on him without flinch. He pitches the question lightly, into the open afterglow: *"Would you like to taste another woman?"* — and her body answers before any part of her can intervene. She comes again, hard, almost at once, with him barely inside her — far too fast for anything he is doing to account for it. *Her body told him in bed that she wanted a woman.* He recognizes a door he is built not to leave closed.

He works a third orgasm out of her slowly, deliberately — pleasure as both true gift and bridge, closing the gap between what her body wants and what her mind has not yet agreed to want.

After. The room cools, the talk goes small and ordinary, and he asks again in daylight register: *"Do you actually want to taste another woman?"* He watches her understand it; he sees the flinch — the *sound of the catching*. Then the recovery: the warm lower register, the trailed finger, the kissed jaw — *"Only in a threesome, with you,"* all silk, the appetite handed to him and herself made its generous instrument. He sees the move and lets her have the frame, because the frame she has built to hide in is the exact frame he needs to proceed: he will find a woman for *theirs*, not hers.

*"Okay,"* he says, easy, and means several things by it. He kisses her forehead, gets up, says *"Let's go for a walk on campus."* She is already up, already reaching for the bright easy version of herself, game for the unnamed thing. He does not tell her what the walk is for.

## Key Beats — Panel Two (Campus Walk)

Cool late-September afternoon; brick walk, colonnade, one sugar maple burned orange in a field of things not yet decided. Randi bound up clean — black ponytail, the one allowed scrunchie, *she had decided that morning exactly how much of her the day would be permitted to touch, and bound the rest.*

**The game runs as reconnaissance.** He points; she judges. Rotating typology — the petite sorority girl in Greek pastels (*too expected*); the matched pair from the rec center (*"those two go everywhere together"*); the closed pre-law camel coat (*"she'd be a project"*); the small inward girl on the library steps with the green beanie (*maybe* — and he kisses her hard there, mouth-to-mouth without wiping his face, to teach her the *maybe* costs); the striking running-the-room girl with the blowout (*"God, no"* — the wrongness instructive). He is reading her grain. He never points first; the choosing is hers; she learns the rule inside a sentence.

**The hinge:** somewhere past the athletic center the coil sets in — a follow's faint forward lean, weighting before the lead calls the step. She is shopping. The frame has dropped; only the *later-pretending* still has her.

**At the dining-hall doors,** Vee comes out *the way weather arrives* — tall, lush, copper hair the wind keeps lifting, laughing with her whole body, throat bare to the sky, *laughing like a person who had genuinely forgotten, for the length of the laugh, that she could be seen.* Pace points. Pace is reading **Randi** — Randi is the instrument, the girl is only the note — and he sees it land. The loose afternoon walk arrested mid-step, the dropped half-stride, the eyes that for the first time all afternoon **do not look away.**

*"Yes,"* she says. The word arrives alone, with nothing around it.

Then the recovery — the warmth coming up over the bareness, the kitten finding the floor: *"Uh — yeah. Yeah. I like her."* The bare *Yes* re-filed as a girlfriend's offer. He has read her right all afternoon.

**The execution.** He sends her with a chin-tip — *Go.* He posts up against a light pole and watches. She drifts, adjusting nothing nameable, line bending toward line by degrees so gradual it looks like chance — and the geometry arranged across thirty feet of lawn arrives all at once and the shoulders meet. The collision is so clean **it is the redhead who apologizes.** Randi's hand on the girl's arm, a half-beat longer than the apology required; laughter; names; two minutes of standing on the lawn.

Randi comes back glowing and failing to hide it. *"Her name is Vee. And we've got a stats class together."* **Her feet do a small happy thing on the brick, a half-second of happy footwork she didn't decide to do, there and gone** — body out ahead of composure again, the third time that afternoon (after the dropped stride and the bare *yes*). Pace lets her see the pride in it. *"Let's go feed you."*

## What the Diptych Argues

- **The two panels are the same act in two registers.** Bed: the appetite found and named-then-renamed. Walk: the same appetite operationalized in daylight, under a game's costume. The reframe in bed (*"only in a threesome, with you"*) is what makes the walk possible — he is not finding her a woman for *her* transgression; he is finding a woman for *theirs*. The diptych is the apparatus assembling itself.
- **The bare Yes is the worked example of 🔒 the Need Engine** (`meta-arch-randi.md`): need surfacing before the box arrives, the box arriving in the recovery (*"I like her"* = need re-filed as want).
- **Pace's reading is sincere and instructive AND blind in his signature way.** First-read he is clocking a special moment; reread, he is misattributing the cause (it is need surfacing in Randi, not Vee being ungradeable; he sees the *behavior* accurately and *misreads its source*). Protected — see `meta-note-the-pointing-game-pace-misread.md`.
- **Vee's appetite is already operating before she knows toward what.** The collision so precisely executed that *the redhead apologizes*, and her feet do the small happy thing — her bravery as the appetite's executable, panel one of the reader's running ledger.
