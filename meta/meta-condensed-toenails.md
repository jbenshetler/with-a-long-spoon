# Condensed — Toenails

*SCENE · Unwritten · morning after · Cassandra track · short, kept small*

Vee comes home late morning, bare-faced, hair like she rolled out of bed — no overnight bag (she couldn't admit the plan that far) and no brush at Pace's house for her long curls — wearing the smile she can't pull off with a crowbar. Cassie is at the little fridge over a bowl of Coco Puffs; her face goes worried for a quarter-second, then she reads her friend and mirrors the glow, glad for her. Last night runs in Vee on a continuous, unstoppable loop, and she **knows she is editing it for Cassie and frames the edit as a kindness** — sex isn't Cassie's thing. The kindness is real enough to be usable; the floozie-shame underneath it is the actual reason. Never connect the two on the page.

Cassie hands her the conspiratorial card — **"Was he worth painting your toenails?"** — already knowing the answer, because she clocked the winter toenails (in the cold they're for no one but him; the naked-intent tell), the same accurate eye that reads everyone, deployed warm. The card grazes the *intent* — the anticipation axis Cassie can hold — and cannot reach the *response*. Vee takes it and gives the **Hallmark Channel edit**: how they met (trimmed of how fast it went to sex), how sweet he is, that he's a good kisser, the dinner he cooked (chicken fra diavolo) — fade to black. Everything that implies the body is cut, down to the sheet she sat wrapped in watching him cook. PG narration over an NC-17 loop; the gap between the two ratings is the **first withheld thing** — a part of this she has no one to tell.

Then the withholding escalates from words to flesh. Vee grabs her towel and starts to undress for the shared-suite shower (she strips to bra and panties in the room first) and **stops mid-act** — she gave her panties to Pace, there is nothing under the dress, and the absence is the undeniable proof of the night she just edited out. Cassie, **oblivious** — no reason to imagine the gift — reads the fumble as lovestruck scatterbrain: something about Vee needing to get her brain back from her boyfriend. The bookend lands: Cassie reads her **perfectly** at the top (toenails) and **not at all** at the bottom (panties); she sees the intent she's allowed to see and misses the consequence she isn't. The panties do triple duty — the gift (each thought of the absence spikes the loop), the proof, the obstacle. Cassie finishes the cereal and leaves for practice (the sport calendar quietly doing its work — the friend who'd keep her safe, reliably elsewhere), and Vee is finally **alone with it**: the night, the loop, the absence, the glow, and no one to tell — far too happy to feel that as a loss. The reader feels it. The vacancy this opens is **exactly what Randi fills at First-Night Debrief**; the un-tellability that protects the intimacy becomes the confidante track's first real feed. Photographic negative of Vee Tells Cassie — Hike.

**Guards.** Warm and funny on first read; the cold visible only on reread. Cassie's quarter-second of worry never attaches to a thought (Cassandra discipline). At the close, keep Vee's interiority on the shower logistics only — never let her or the narration draw the rhyme between hiding the panties and editing the story; resonance only. Kept small: a short scene, not a staircase step.
