# Condensed — The Shirt — Re-Scenting (The New Ordinary)

*SCENE · Unwritten · ~mid–late November, a Friday night a couple of weeks after the First Swat · Pace/Vee · Vee POV*

A full scene doing two jobs at once: it *demonstrates* the settled domestic rhythm (the new ordinary) and stages the shirt re-scenting beat inside it. It also lays down, on purpose, the **ordinary baseline of how the couple's sex is narrated**, so the later taste-thread changes read as change and not as signal.

## Why they've been apart

Several nights without seeing each other. The over-staying caught up with Vee — too many short nights away from her dorm, schoolwork slipping — and she reluctantly said she needed the time. Pace didn't push (the deference is genuinely held, not a tactic; [[pace-good-qualities-not-cynical]]). This pull-back is the mechanism by which the **greedy phase banks into the new ordinary**: weekends his, weeknights hers. Still running through The Top Drawer and Cassie — School Nights, it settles here; a shape, not a guarantee.

## Shape

- **Arrival.** The Corolla barely stopped; she grabs the overnight bag, lighter now that the top drawer holds a few of her things — *her space* — and smiles at the thought. Warm greeting at the door.
- **The bed.** Straight back, reunion-hungry — several nights' appetite. This is the **ordinary-baseline sex**: bed, not the bench (the bench is occasion). Narrated with enough specific, particular detail that the later taste-thread shifts in sex scenes show as departures from an established norm. (Author is carrying much of this specificity.)
- **The pizza (taste-seed).** After, the kitchen. Pace cooks in hyperfocus, eyes on the work not on her face: breadmaker dough; ~30 min cooking down roma tomatoes, onion, garlic, wine and a little cream into a smooth paste he spreads over the dough; buffalo mozzarella grated on top (*"from buffalo, the European kind"*); slivered yellow onion, sliced button mushroom; a pinch of cornmeal on the oven stone, the pizza launched with a wooden peel; ~15 min. He drinks a **Nebbiolo** sparingly — the work has him; Vee drinks more freely and watches. Then she marvels at the flavor: she can *taste the components* — yeast in the dough, acid and sweetness from the tomato, heat from the pepper, crunchy onion and yielding mushroom — **the difference between cotton candy and sliced strawberries.**
- **The shirt.** Unpacking, she hands him the hike shirt, washed and folded. He's puzzled, a little saddened (reads it as her returning it for good). *"This is still my shirt. You don't get to keep it."* — playful, possessive, a defense of something precious — then the small abject admission, not quite meeting his eye: *"I had to wash it. It doesn't smell like you anymore. I need you to wear it and give it back to me."* He takes it without making her explain twice; his face splits with a warm smile. She'll have it back, re-scented, at the end of the weekend.

## The taste-seed register (load-bearing)

Minor key, fully subordinated to the domestic warmth — first read, she's just fed and loved. It's the **food-safe practice run at connoisseurship**: the faculty of attending to components, discriminating one taste from another, which Whiskey and Wine and First Taste later turn on her own body. **Vee does the discriminating; Pace only names the cheese** — if he taught her to taste it would read as cultivating her palate on purpose (which it secretly is) and telegraph. Points at nothing; the echo lands on reread. Governed by [[meta-note-taste-thread]] (the thread hitchhikes, never drives).

## The shirt beat — direction (canon)

Vee owns the shirt (the first-night *"Trade"* — panties for the shirt). She is **not** undoing the trade: she's lending it back to be re-scented. The need is hers; the abjection is hers; "this is still my shirt" is literally true *and* playful *and* a defense of something precious, with the admission tucked right behind it. Pace's "puzzled → saddened → warm smile" is the receiving arc — moved, and (untipped) registering how deep in him she sleeps. **Real-and-instrumental, never resolved** ([[pace-good-qualities-not-cynical]]). Do not invert to Pace-as-owner; that erases the Trade and edges into the marking-villain tell.

## The geometry (felt, never named)

Weekends his, weeknights hers, the shirt bridging the nights apart. Sleeping in his shirt is evidence of the nights she's *not* there — which is exactly what defines the new ordinary: the rhythm has a shape now, and the shirt is how she carries him across its gaps.

## The sibling-rhyme (never explained)

Both object-tokens of care in one frame: **her things live in his drawer; his shirt lives on her skin.** The drawer token is what the spring strips; the shirt token is what she'll be left holding. Resonance only — no line of prose may notice it.

## Mirror-time (settled ritual, texture only)

If the bathroom comes on camera: hair up at his sink, a scrunchie off the form in her top drawer, a returned one dropped loose — the never-rethread habit visible as nothing but domestic texture (origin: We Find Out — the form; The Top Drawer — the drawer). Here the grammar is already ordinary, which is the point.

## Pays off / plants

Pays off: the washed-out shirt at the close of the CW Dance (where the lost smell surfaces); the first-night trade. Establishes: the regular overnight habit and the new-ordinary rhythm; the **sex baseline** the taste-thread later departs from.
