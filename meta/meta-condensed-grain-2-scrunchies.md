# Condensed — Grain #2 — Worn in Plain Sight

*Vignette-thread · Architecture complete, prose not drafted · recurring, March onward · seen only in Vee-POV scenes*

Randi wears Vee's scrunchies around Vee — and Vee is **thrilled**: the glamour queen is mimicking *her* style. And not just the style — *her color*: the deep green that isn't in the local stores.

## The object design (canonical)

**Usual design, unusual color** — each property does one job. The common style keeps the object deniable (it's just a scrunchie; they're everywhere). The uncommon green carries both the flattery and the flicker: it's what makes the sighting *recognizable* without making it *evidence*. The rarity tier is calibrated — online-but-not-local, never unique. Custom or one-batch would be a fingerprint and convert the sighting into proof; the design forbids proof in either direction.

## Mechanism — evidence in the open, wearing a perfect disguise

The sighting is fully conscious and completely misread. Vee's reading is flattery — and it has a characterological engine, planted in the fall ({{Sorority}}): **when Randi sees something she wants, she buys it.** *She saw it on me, she wanted it, she hunted it down — of course she did, she's Randi.* This is the **loving-Randi cover**, twin to grain #3's flicker of the loving-Pace story: every corrupted datum in the campaign gets explained by love. The evidence isn't suppressed; it's metabolized as intimacy — which actively protects Randi at grain #3, because "Randi wears scrunchies like mine" is already filed under *friendship* and can't combine with the form arithmetic into "Randi has *my* scrunchies."

## Shape

- **First instance — spoken.** Vee delighted (*"did I actually convert you?"*); Randi's double-register reply: *"You've ruined me. I used to have standards."* Warm, frank, fun on first read; on reread she is being complimented on wearing the trespass to its owner's face, and accepting the compliment. No word changes.
- **Recurrences — unremarked.** Set dressing riding scenes that do other work; no camera linger, no authorial wink. The prose says "a scrunchie" or "the green" on the incidental recurrences, but the **full descriptor fires at the load-bearing beats** — the christening ({{We Find Out}}), the recognizing instance here, and the reveal — routed through Vee's **notice-and-dismiss** so she reads it and overrides it (see [[meta-plan-scrunchie-gaslight]]); the reread does the rest.
- **Late variation — post-Restoration.** One sighting where the delight takes a half-second longer to arrive. The only trace that her instrument is still running underneath. Theme-and-variations inside the grain.

## Guardrails (anti-telegraph)

1. No rarity-talk and no provenance dialogue, ever — Vee's delight is about being copied, not about sourcing; if the scene audits supply chains, it's prosecuting.
2. The buys-what-she-wants trait must be planted in the fall as pure characterization, long before it's needed as cover.
3. Space the sightings away from drawer beats — Randi's first wearing never sits adjacent to the First Flicker or any mirror-time beat; proximity invites the combination the design forbids.
4. It looks *good* on her — green flatters Randi's black hair, and her curated discipline would never wear what doesn't work. The wearing stays in character; Vee's "it looks amazing on you" stays honest.

## Reader irony

The reader carries the First Flicker and the named green, so the jolt is available (*wait — are those—?*) while Vee beams — but it stays unprovable: the color is buyable, and Randi acquires. The warmth of the scenes seduces the reader into half-buying the flattery too; the *is-Vee-paranoid* question extends to the reader: *am I?*

## The reciprocity cut

Everything in this friendship flows Randi→Vee — the shoes, the borrowed witty lines, the steering. The scrunchies appear to flow Vee→Randi: the only mutuality Vee ever perceives in the friendship, the moment she believes she gives Randi something too — style *and* color, her whole signature adopted. On reread, the only thing that ever flowed from Vee to Randi was taken from her drawer.
