# Condensed — The Drawer, First Visit (off-page event)

*EVENT · NOT A SCENE · never rendered · ~mid-to-late February, a weekday afternoon · no POV*

What happens (in the docs only, never on the page): Randi takes ~6 scrunchies from the form in **Vee's top drawer** (~38 → ~32) during one of her weekday-afternoon visits. She keeps them.

## Trespass, not theft

The top drawer is gifted territory — the one space in that house that is Vee's own, founded at The Top Drawer and never touched by Pace's hand since. Randi doesn't take tokens from Pace's bathroom; she reaches into Vee's space. On reread the violation isn't of provision but of territory, and what she strips is the artifact of Vee being met with grace — the form in her color, the gift that answered a need she never voiced. The space he gave Vee so she'd be at home becomes the instrument of her self-doubt.

## Why it is never rendered

The reader holds **means** (weekday access) and **motive** (Flat Date; Spring Inversion) but never the **act** — *is Vee being paranoid?* is the design, and it runs on the reader as much as on Vee. When the First Flicker arrives, the reader's model of Randi-the-strategist supplies the dismissal: petty scrunchie-taking is illegible as strategy, serves no plan-purpose, risks the threesome — so the reader files Vee's accurate perception under "probably miscounting," using the same logic Vee does. Readers who do suspect get slow horror; readers who don't are implicated on reread: they dismissed her correct reading too. Keeping the act off-page also keeps Randi's double register unbroken all the way to the reveal — her interior stays withheld, and no scene ever shows her nakedly cold.

## Motive chain (reread-only; never marked in text)

**Flat Date → the Jar → the taking.** The Flat Date proves she feels nothing outside the configuration — both flames or no fire. Her aliveness runs on two channels, and both are real: submission (being seen by Pace — it reaches her fully, and it is intolerable; the exposure costs her what the bench cost) and sadism (operating on Vee — alive, controlled, deniable). The Jar shows her Vee thriving openly on the same exposure Randi can only endure and return to — same feeling, opposite tolerance. The envy is of capacity: not capacity to feel (she feels it) but capacity to *hold* it — to be seen and grow rather than seen and stripped. The water-glass nerve at full amperage; the taking follows as the plan escaping her — a compulsive leak, not strategy. The fuse is visible only on reread.

## Hard constraints

- **Sequencing:** Spring Inversion (the reader's last interior access to Randi before the campaign) must already be on the page; the overnight habit, the form, and the drawer's grammar must be reader-established since October–November (The Scrunchies; The Top Drawer; Re-Scenting).
- **No staged opportunity beats.** Never plant a deniable "she excused herself" in any Randi–Pace scene — that is the author signaling. The strongest version marks nothing; weekday access alone is the opportunity. In Pace-POV scenes the camera rides Pace and honestly inherits his blindness ("he'd see scrunchies and scrunchies").
- **POV key:** the bathroom/drawer is on-camera only in Vee's POV (`meta-plan-pace-house.md`; chronology flag 15).
