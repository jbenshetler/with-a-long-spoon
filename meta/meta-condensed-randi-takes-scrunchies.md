# Condensed — The Drawer, First Visit (off-page event)

*EVENT · NOT A SCENE · never rendered · ~mid-to-late February, a weekday afternoon · no POV*

What happens (in the docs only, never on the page): Randi takes ~6 scrunchies from the cardboard form in Pace's bathroom drawer (~38 → ~32) during one of her weekday-afternoon visits. She keeps them.

## Why it is never rendered

The reader holds **means** (weekday access) and **motive** (Flat Date; Spring Inversion) but never the **act** — *is Vee being paranoid?* is the design, and it runs on the reader as much as on Vee. When the First Flicker arrives, the reader's model of Randi-the-strategist supplies the dismissal: petty scrunchie theft is illegible as strategy, serves no plan-purpose, risks the threesome — so the reader files Vee's accurate perception under "probably miscounting," using the same logic Vee does. Readers who do suspect get slow horror; readers who don't are implicated on reread: they dismissed her correct reading too. Keeping the act off-page also keeps Randi's double register unbroken all the way to the reveal — her interior stays withheld, and no scene ever shows her nakedly cold.

## Motive chain (reread-only; never marked in text)

**Flat Date → the Jar → the taking.** The Flat Date proves her want has no substitute; the Jar hands her the unbearable datum (Vee has exactly the thing, and it's the *tenderness* — the one thing Randi cannot extract from him and just proved she can't get elsewhere); the taking follows as the plan escaping her — a compulsive leak, not strategy. The fuse is visible only on reread.

## Hard constraints

- **Sequencing:** Spring Inversion (the reader's last interior access to Randi before the campaign) must already be on the page; the overnight habit and the form must be reader-established since early November (Re-Scenting; the mirror-time thread).
- **No staged opportunity beats.** Never plant a deniable "she excused herself" in any Randi–Pace scene — that is the author signaling. The strongest version marks nothing; weekday access alone is the opportunity. In Pace-POV scenes the camera rides Pace and honestly inherits his blindness ("he'd see scrunchies and scrunchies").
- **POV key:** Pace's bathroom/drawer is on-camera only in Vee's POV (`meta-plan-pace-house.md`; chronology flag 15).
