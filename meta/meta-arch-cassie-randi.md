## The Shape in One Line

Cassie sees Randi clearly; Randi does not see that Cassie sees. Cassie reads Randi for *operation*; Randi files Cassie as **a thing in the way of what she wants** — and it is hard to be irritated with a thing, particularly one **so easily disposed of.** The mutual underestimation runs opposite directions and neither is ever named.

## CASSIE'S SIDE (on record; see `meta-arch-cassie.md` for the full constitution)

- **Instant antipathy needs no explanation.** Established as a given — and *asymmetric*: Cassie's principled dislike, met by Randi's **pure dismissal** (no rivalry, no threat felt; see below). Not symmetric enmity.
- **Does not twin Randi's silhouette** — the aesthetic non-overlap (brown, unmanaged, used-not-watched vs. Randi's sleek controlled black-on-black). Permanent managed-vs-unmanaged contrast; never staged.
- **Sees Randi clearly because she has no investment in Randi being what she presents as.** Cassie is outside the appearance economy — no skin in being-looked-at — so Randi's warmth-instrument finds nothing to grip, and Cassie reads the *operation* instead of receiving the *beam.*
- **Her warnings are Randi-shaped** (conscious); the meet-cute no-attraction line (great-aunt look) attaches to the staging / Randi, not to Pace's character. She registers structure, not character.

## RANDI'S SIDE (the recovered material — was blank in the Randi docs; THIS is the piece that got lost)

**Cassie is a thing in the way of what Randi wants.** It is hard to be irritated with a thing, particularly one so easily disposed of. Randi does not experience Cassie as a rival or a threat — feeling something about Cassie would require granting her the status of a person who could matter, and Randi does not. The non-affect is the move. **Temperature: pure dismissal — Cassie barely registers — not because Randi holds herself above Cassie, but because Cassie isn't a player in the exchange Randi is running.** The flat name-bit return doesn't even curdle into a "huh"; a closed door you're already walking past costs nothing.

**Critically, Randi does NOT clock Cassie as Cassandra.** Randi does not register that Cassie has read her. She files Cassie as the dull, un-charmable, removable roommate — not as the one set of clear eyes on the operation. **This underestimation is exactly what leaves the one true witness un-managed and therefore still seeing.** (Mirror of Cassie's own blindness to the Pace-engine: each underrates the other. Chiasmus-class — felt from outside only, never named by anyone.)

### Why Cassie is "easily disposed of" — TWO LAYERS, running together

**Layer 1 — the ambient gravity (Vee's appetite).** *Randi has what Vee wants; Vee has what Randi wants.* Vee wants Randi's glamour, ascent, the world she was priced out of — Randi is what Vee reaches *toward.* Randi wants what Vee has — the aliveness, the bravery, the experience of being a real friend to someone who treats her as substance rather than position. Each is the other's hunger-object. **Cassie has neither pole of that exchange:** she is not glamorous (nothing Vee aspires to) and she is not the wound's instrument (nothing Randi needs). She is the beloved-but-not-*new* peer, representing what Vee already has. So when the two confidantes pull on Vee's disclosures, it was never a fair contest — aspiration outweighs the familiar — and Vee routes the real material toward the thing she's climbing toward. Cassie is sidelined by Vee's own longing, costing Randi nothing. *(Vee would never say it this way and never sees it. The sort feels like the most natural thing in the world.)*

**Layer 2 — the deliberate engineering (Randi's method).** Randi ALSO makes intentional choices to cut Cassie out. **Planned and fully intentional.** She does not bar Cassie or speak against her — that would show, would cost her with Vee, would make her the villain. Instead she selects *conditions* under which Cassie removes herself, and stays warm and inclusive throughout. This is the warmth-instrument applied to logistics — the same architecture as the SATC extraction (supply the conditions, let the target's own nature do the rest) and the off-campus brunch venue (engineer the ground so witnesses can't price what they see).

**The two layers point the same direction and reinforce each other — which is why the engineering is invisible: the deliberate cut looks identical to the natural drift.**

### The two worked examples (the proof — both must be remembered)

1. **The Greek coffee shop (Chi Latte).** In `see-you-later.md` Randi steers Vee to the Greek-coded coffee shop on Draper — **a place Cassie would never go.** Surface: just where Randi likes to go (glamorous, aspirational, Randi's taste). Underneath: ground selected to be Cassie-incompatible. The exclusion is built into the *choice of venue.*

2. **The dress-code lunch.** Randi invites them **both** — looks generous, looks inclusive — to a lunch with a **dress code**, knowing **Cassie doesn't dress.** The invitation is the alibi; the dress code is the instrument; Cassie excludes *herself* by being who she is; Randi's hands stay clean and she gets to have extended the invitation. The social-perimeter version of what the velvet alcove is for disclosure: a room built so the inconvenient party can't be in it, presented as a nice place to eat. **Status: conceptual, not yet placed — likely becomes an SATC brunch. See `meta-todo-doc-updates.md` item 5 (placement pending; no live chronology flag).**

## DISCIPLINES (how this renders on the page)

1. **Venues read as Randi's TASTE, never as tactic.** The Greek coffee shop and the dress-code lunch must read, on first pass, as the glamorous places a girl like Randi naturally goes. The exclusion is **reread-only.** If the prose flags a venue as a tactic, Randi flips from warm-operator to obvious-schemer and the whole "power wears warmth" architecture breaks. (Craft rule — `meta-craft-randi.md`, The Speech Engine: Randi's power wears warmth, not coolness.)

2. **Vee never articulates any of it.** Vee loves Cassie; that is real and unthreatened. Vee does not perceive the sort as a betrayal, does not see the venue-engineering, would never say "Cassie isn't new" or "Randi is a gateway." The whole mechanism is sub-articulate — the most natural sorting imaginable. (Rule 393: Vee does harm by being a good friend, unable to see it.)

3. **Cassie's safety and her marginalization are the SAME fact.** She stays clear and un-corrupted *because* she is outside the exchange; she gets left behind *because* she is outside the exchange. Do not render one without the other being structurally true. The friend who'd have kept Vee safe is the friend the configuration removes — and *"You saw it and I didn't"* is the novel confirming that the thing disposed of without a thought was the only thing that could have saved Vee.

4. **They barely interact.** This is not a relationship with scenes of its own; it is two endpoints whose few contacts (the diptych, the meet-cute) generate all the dynamic-content. Do not manufacture Cassie–Randi confrontations. The power is in how little needs to happen.
