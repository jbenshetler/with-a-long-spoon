# Condensed — Shoe Shopping with Randi

*SCENE · **Draft complete:** `scenes/shoe-shopping-randi.md` · ~Sat Oct 19 · Vee POV · diptych · SATC*

## Summary

The second showing-to-Randi scene — a warm two-panel diptych: the finished gown's first unveiling to Randi (Vee's dorm room), then the boutique fitting. Structured as the contrast to Vee showing Cassie in Stats — Simpson's Paradox (Cassie watches the *person*; Randi runs *appraisal-as-interest*). **Played straight:** the dark is **reread-only**, carried by Randi's appraisal and the deniable physical installation — **not** by any planted secret. Establishes shopping-together as a format Randi excels at.

## Form

Diptych, close-third Vee, panels split by `---`, **match-cut on the dress** (worn → bag-zip → hook). Opens in medias res (outing arranged at Stats — *"How's Saturday"*).

## Panel I — Vee's room (the unveiling)

- **First time Randi sees the FINISHED gown** (she knew Pace was making it; never saw it done).
- Vee puts it *on* for Randi (the wanting overrides the sane hold-it-up-on-a-hanger). Cassie absent, her bed made tight — the **Cassandra bite lives in the furniture** (Vee gives Randi the first sight in the room she shares with Cassie, who only ever got the telling). Resonance only, never named.
- Randi's four beats: *"Oh, baby. Turn for me — slow. … God, Vee."* → *"Is it real silk?"* + the **silk-verification touch** (two fingers up the bias seam to the bare shoulder — deniable, the signal) + *"…it is."* (appraisal/verdict) → *"He didn't make this **for** you. He made it **to** you."* (the possession-preposition) → *"…the whole room is going to stop. Every one of those girls in the one dress she owns that has to do for everything…"* (**Randi's own snobbery — she does NOT mimic Vee**; that protects the singular scrunchie-mimicry later).
- Ends on the garment-bag zip.

## Panel II — the boutique (the fitting + the buys)

- Dress on its hook (color reference); the **inversion-rhyme literalized** — Pace's made gown hangs while Randi kneels at Vee's feet.
- **Price dismay** (Vee's TJMaxx world); pride bars the honest exit; she drifts to the cheaper-but-wrong "sensible" shoe (**cowardice, not taste**) and transcends it.
- **Randi steers by taste, never money:** *"A dress your **lover** made you? That doesn't go on those. It goes on these."* (*lover* upgrades Pace in Vee's chest; hands Vee the laundering frame; dark-irony — Randi naming her own lover).
- Randi displaces the clerk and kneels to fit — the **toned-burgundy toes** (dialed back to defer to the gown), the single **"Mm"** + thumb (the First-Night-Debrief seal, now skin-to-skin), Vee's **wobble** (body before mind; converted register, not raw shame).
- **The laundering:** aesthetic rightness overrides price — the shoes are *what the dress was owed*, the want relocated to the gown's needs (the rationalization engine, never named).
- **Shared register, same counter, opposite weight:** Vee reaches past her debit for the **credit card** — the most she's ever charged, no plan to repay, face calm (prudence-armor cracks); Randi drops a careless **multiple** without a glance and **wears hers out** (the 10× knife, rhymes the wine-glass).
- **Exit warm:** Randi's dry *"You can put it in the trunk… it's not going to spoil"* lands the genuine, unperformed laugh; Vee carries her box like a relic.
