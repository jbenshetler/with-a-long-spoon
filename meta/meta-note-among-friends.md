# Note — Among Friends (Pace enters Vee's world)

*Scene · Architecture complete, prose not drafted · ~late January, the first weekend of spring semester or a weeknight soon after · just after First Weekend Back, around {{Forbidden Kiss}} / {{The Reach}} · Vee POV · companion to meta-condensed-among-friends.md · title provisional (alt: "Without Being Asked")*

## What this scene is now

The **first of the paired world-openings.** This scene brings Pace into *Vee's* world — a gathering of her friends at a bar or restaurant; {{Another Round}} (~late January) then **reciprocates**, bringing Vee into *Pace's* (his people, Sheri). It is **Volume Two's warm opener** ("the bloom before the souring"), promoted out of what was the bomber-jacket coda tucked at the end of First Weekend Back. First Weekend Back proper now ends on the reconciliation; this scene carries the public echo.

Its engine is **Pace's deed-answer to the *{{Under the Rug}}* visibility wound.** In *{{Under the Rug}}* (~mid-December) Vee asked, in the dark, *"Is this what we are now? … will we ever be seen in public again?"* — and the Chili's disaster left it unanswered (his sensory overload misread as rejection of *being seen with her*). Pace metabolizes that over the break, and this is his response, given in the only grammar he has — **deed, not word.** He comes into her world and lets himself be seen *with* her, among her people, without her having to ask. The wound answered by an act, never a declaration (the vow holds all year).

**The double answer, and the trap inside it.** He answers only one axis — *he'll be seen with me in public* — while the enclosure of his *own* life stays sealed (she still can't get into his world; the gym proves the outside dangerous at `The Burn`; his people wait for {{Another Round}}). So the answer **soothes the wound and deepens the cage at once**: warm and real on the surface, and on reread the beginning of the lesson the book runs on — *his room is where I'm real; the world out there doesn't quite hold us.* He gives her the public claim and keeps the private word; she takes the deed for the thing she actually asked for. Nothing named.

## The engine — being seen (deed, not word)

- **The visibility wound answered in deed.** The whole point of the scene is Pace *present*, *claimed*, *among her friends* — the public "yes" to the question he couldn't answer at the Chili's. Render the answer as the *fact of his being there*, never as a line about it. He does not say "I want to be seen with you"; he simply comes, unasked, and works the room as hers.
- **Vee's proprietary claim, unearned.** She gets to present him to her friends as *hers* — the proprietary feeling the First Weekend Back note names (she "hasn't earned the right to" it, because he still won't name her). The public status stands in for the private word. Continues the clothing-as-proxy staircase (the shirt-scenting, the Top Drawer) one rung further: now it's not his shirt on her skin, it's *him*, in his own jacket, standing in her world as the claim she can't otherwise make.
- **The asymmetry is the dark.** Only her world opens. Keep the sealed-room engine intact: his life stays withheld. Don't let this scene read as the walls coming down — it's one gift on one axis, and the reader should feel (never be told) that the other walls hold.

## Vee's interior arc (the felt throughline)

This is Vee's POV, and the warmth has to be *lived from inside her*, not asserted — it is the ground the whole back half stands on. **The scene digs Vee in deep.** The relationship has to become, here, something the reader falls for as hard as she does, because her spring/summer escalation (the anesthetic erotic, the road to the threesome) must read as *believable and even justified* — the deep, genuine happiness is what makes the later choices land as choices, not folly. Earn it here or the back half floats.

**The arc: nervous → floatingly happy.**
- **Entry — nerves.** She walks in braced: will her two worlds *mix*? Will Pace be all right among her people, will they like him, will she have to manage the seams (Chili's under this, unnamed)? A knot carried in.
- **The dissolve.** It goes *right*, and the knot melts by degrees: Pace is warm and easy with her friends (the charm, real), and — the part that undoes her — **attentive to her** throughout, and then the public claim (*"Vee's boyfriend"*). Each lands as relief tipping into joy until she is **floating** — her man among her people, claimed, and hers. The scene's weather is this floating; it's where she ends.

**The one twinge (a single grace note, then gone).** Watching Pace make *Randi* laugh, a flicker of jealousy crosses her — and she reads it, correctly to herself, as *not about Randi* (her safe, best friend). What it actually is: the resurfaced **Christmas-break fear that Pace and Sheri were together**, the one she'd confided to Randi in `my-friend-randi`. Him being funny and warm with a woman pings the old insecurity; she talks herself down and the floating reasserts. **On reread:** the radar fired at the right *category* (Pace + another woman) and the wrong *target* (absent, imagined Sheri) while the real one laughs with her hand in Vee's — and Vee had handed that exact fear to *that exact woman*. The everything-inward training (flag 3) overrides the ping before it can form a question. It also quietly builds the **Sheri-as-absorber** slot (flag 21, the spring found-hair): Vee's insecurity keeps installing *Sheri* as the woman-in-his-world, a ready wrong story that always points away from Randi.
- **Guards:** body-first — the twinge is *felt*, misfiled, gone; never a narrated deduction. The Sheri-fear is a **glancing memory, not re-explained** (resonance, not exposition). The reassurance is *genuine* — she isn't consciously suppressing suspicion; she truly reads it as nothing. **One** twinge only. Consider **spacing it from the introduction/claim** — a later, across-the-room moment — so the Randi-cluster isn't overloaded.

## The room (populate it, or the charm has nothing to work on)

- **The guest list:** 2–3 of Vee's **dorm** friends (unaffiliated — Vawter Hall floor, maybe one non-stats classmate) + **Cassie** + **Randi** + **Pace**. The dorm friends must be **seeded before this scene** (flag 22): individuate 2–3, keep the rest ambient, draw them from Vee's own world, never the sorority (Randi's turf — Brooke is the sole individuated sorority actor) and never honor society. Stats class stays the one room that holds Randi + Vee + Cassie. **Kayla** — Vee's Vawter-floor neighbor two doors down, a warm chatterbox — is the first individuated dorm friend, seeded in `Nothing Underneath` (the hall-and-laundry beat, where she clocks Vee's going-out shoes and guesses Pace); she reappears in the room here.
- **The named few (kept light):** **Kayla** (breezy chatterbox, Vawter floor, seeded in `Nothing Underneath`) and **Meg** (a warm dorm friend, coupled — her boyfriend among the ambient couples) carry the individuated warmth. **Theo is scenery** — a guy from Vee's sophomore English who once asked her out and took *let's be friends* in stride, present and easy; his one job is a **light desirability touch** (a man wanted her, she turned it kindly and kept him — she's no ugly-duckling), a single glancing beat, never a foregrounded thread (the desirability thread proper runs across other vignettes). Vawter is **girls-only**, so Theo is a separate pocket. Everyone else is ambient couples-and-friends wallpaper — room-fullness that thins later, and among whom Pace blends (one more reason his room-temperature charm reads as normal). **The girls have all heard so much about Pace** — a room predisposed to adore the mythologized boyfriend (his charm showcase; warm on first read, and on reread they're meeting the man orchestrating toward the threesome, his collaborator smiling beside him).
- **Vee assembles the unlikely mix.** Dorm sophomores + a sorority junior (Randi) + Pace is a combination only Vee would convene — a quiet render of her **aliveness** (Vivienne = alive; the connective warmth canon gives her "in abundance while Randi has to buy hers"). It is also the *mechanism*: her own connecting nature is what unknowingly seats her boyfriend and her orchestrator in one room. She builds the collision, exactly as she keeps (unknowingly) authoring her own trap.
- **The dorm friends carry the aliveness that later thins.** Seed the fullness here (warm, silly, ordinary richness); the sealed room erases it across spring/summer, so *"the world contracts until nothing outside it exists"* becomes something the reader *watches happen* rather than gets told. Don't overbuild them into a subplot — texture, not a cast.

## Pace working the room (the charm is canon, the cost is canon)

**A core purpose of the scene, not texture.** Show Pace *charming and socially capable* — the reader (and Vee) must fall for him here, because the back half depends on it. His easy competence with her people is what dissolves her nerves and earns the floating; give it real room.

- **His charm mechanism (canon, meta-arch-bible.md):** "he sets up the jokes deftly and lets other people land the laugh lines. He makes himself useful and lets others take the win." Genuine, warm, present, deferring — the *same faculty*, un-aimed, that he later aims at Vee's calculus and the choreography, here unweaponized. He reads the room and makes people feel seen. This is the "fall for Pace again" material — the reader (and Vee) watch him be effortlessly, generously good with her people.
- **Venue guard — do NOT re-run the Chili's failure by accident.** Canon: Pace passes socially **in short, structured doses at a cost** (the CW bar works — defined role, mapped interactions), and *{{Under the Rug}}* established that a loud, chaotic room *breaks* his apparatus. So the setting must be a **format he can pass in.** **The handoff:** *Pace* proposes the outing itself (the deed — he wants to be out among her people); Vee, **thrilled and nervous** and quietly set on not repeating Chili's, **asks him to pick the place** — *you know the area* — the deniable cover for her real reason, which neither of them names. He lands a **roadhouse with food** (over a campus bar: under-21 Kayla stays with the group, so the *full* room holds). Two unspoken accommodations in one exchange: his initiating and choosing the ground he can hold (rendered as thoughtfulness, never control), and her deferring the choice because she learned from the wreck — neither naming why (the un-named temperament; POV protects it — we only ever see that he chose a roadhouse and it works). *Why this venue and not Chili's* is answered in silence. It keeps his charm real (he shines instead of straining) **and** it is *another deed* — the man who couldn't explain the Chili's overload now quietly lands the room where the public night succeeds. If you write a loud blowout he works effortlessly, you've quietly cancelled the temperament; if you must use a taxing room, let the *cost* show.

## Randi present — the concealment (the writing challenge)

This is the most dangerous stretch to write, because it puts the two concealed collaborators in a room with the mark. The rules that make it safe:

- **They perform "met once," not "strangers."** Vee *witnessed* the coffee-shop meet-cute, so they can't pretend never to have met. They hold exactly the meet-cute line — acquaintances who met that once, nothing more. This is both the only consistent play and the **lowest-cost** one for the reveal: Vee already banked "they met once," so a second low-key co-presence adds no new "acquaintance" she has to un-know later. Don't let them build a *friendship* on the page — that's what would soften the eventual "they were lovers all along."
- **Concealment is UNIFORMITY, not distance.** For a room-worker, going conspicuously *cool* on one person is the tell — a charming man who charms everyone except his girlfriend's friend flags that friend. So Pace charms Randi at **room temperature**, the same warmth he gives the whole room. What stays absent (invisibly) is **desire**, not warmth — the same needle as the meet-cute's "great-aunt" look, now with the charm on. The lovers hide in plain sight at ambient temperature; on reread, the fact that he worked Randi *exactly like the others* is the perfect crime.
- **Cassie does NOT re-clock the no-attraction.** She made the "great-aunt" observation at the meet-cute (continuity flag 5); a *second* clock draws the line in ink and tips "it was all there" into "I knew." She can be present and warm; she just doesn't narrate the not-attraction again.
- **The loaded undercurrent (optional, reader-side only):** this is the one social scene where **Pace is the operator and Randi is the audience** — she watches him work a room the way she works Vee alone (dyad → group is also a register-shift from her private brunches, a minor dilution of that exclusivity, worth clocking). If Randi's jealousy/assessment leaks, it leaks only as a wordless body tell (the containers rule, flag 15 — her interior never opens in company); on first read she is simply a supportive friend enjoying the boyfriend. Invisible on first read or cut it.

## The introduction — "Vee's boyfriend" (one line, both women's submission)

When Pace meets the group he introduces himself not as *Pace* but as **"Vee's boyfriend"** — the register the room hears, landing sharpest in one piece of staging. Because as far as Vee knows, Pace and Randi **only exchanged names once** at the coffee shop last fall, she does the most natural, alive thing: she **takes Randi by the hand and pulls her over to *properly* meet him** — her two favorite people, barely introduced. And she **under-claims** — *"Randi, this is Pace"* — because she can't quite presume the word, given how unnamed they still are. Pace **finishes the sentence she didn't dare**, warm, to Randi's face: ***"Vee's boyfriend."*** In that one line, delivered to his own concealed lover as though to a stranger, three things fire at once: the **public claim** (Vee owned, out loud), the **met-once performance** (flag 23), and the **cut** — naming Vee his, to Randi. And **Randi's hand closes hard around Vee's** — the wounded party's body leaking into the one channel it has — while Vee, glowing, reads the grip as *Randi so happy for me*. The squeeze is the tell; the misread is the cover. It does **triple work**, all of it surface-warm on first read:

- **The label as claim-substitute (Vee).** He won't say *I love you* all year (the vow holds), and he'd **never been anywhere that needed a name** — so this is a small threshold he crosses, a word new in his mouth, not a practiced play (non-cynical; keep the flicker of first-time-ness). It's the deed-answer's verbal apex *without* editorializing the wound — a self-introduction that happens to claim her, never *"I want to be seen with you."* And it **soothes without closing** (like the cabin key): she takes the public label for the private word, and the real word still never comes — the cage deepens under the warmth. Rendered body-first in her POV: the public claiming lands on her submission (owned, out loud, witnessed by her confidante) — something low and tight, never analyzed, the masochism never named.
- **The same line cuts Randi (canon-true).** Randi is Pace's lover (reader knows from the Bench), and her masochism's standing content is **her own debasement relative to Vee** — she arranged for the man she sleeps with to spend himself on this girl (*sloppy seconds*), a cut she authored and takes each time it runs (`meta-arch-randi.md`). The public claim **sharpens that rung**: not just that he services Vee but that he *names her his, out loud,* while Randi stands unclaimed, performing *met-once*. Vee gets the word; Randi gets concealment. Masochistic cut and charge at once (arousal climbs on the humiliation), self-inflicted, deniable.
- **The reader's live dread.** No reread needed — the reader *knows* Randi and Pace are lovers, so the line knifes in real time (the book's engine: watching Vee walk into the dark voluntarily).

**Rendering (load-bearing).** Randi's side is real in the fiction but **never on the page as interior** — Vee POV plus the containers rule (flag 15) seal her. Handle it like `outlier.md`: the cut lands **now, in company, as surface + body**, never explained from inside her. Here the body-leak is the **hard squeeze**, and it can be *noticeable* precisely because Vee's misread cloaks it — the deniability lives in the **interpretation** (*happy for me*), not in faintness. That is the whole safety: a grip Vee feels and warmly mis-files, never a flinch or a cool that would read as *wrong*. This is a **rung in Randi's ongoing masochism (a sharpening), not a new aperture** — her interior stays sealed here; the apertures remain the Bench and {{Fairytale}} mirrors.

**Guards.** Pace's line is **not aimed** — his plain self-introduction to the room, never territorial (the instant it reads as marking against the guy who once asked Vee out, he's a jealous boyfriend and cheapens). It must **not read as "he finally named what they are"** — a social label, a station, not the destination; the naming wound stays open. It is the scene's **single** real-AND-instrumental claim: Vee owned in one warm word, love-not-declared and claim-made at once.

## Continuity guards

- **Earn the dark by being light.** A genuinely warm, fun night — a girlfriend delighted that her boyfriend charmed her friends. Cut any signal that Randi is sinister or that she and Pace know each other beyond the meet-cute. Suspicion kills the warmth (and the arousal the book runs on).
- **Never name, never explain.** The temperament (the venue choice), the threesome pre-seed, the cage deepening — all resonance, never stated. Body before mind; the reader infers.
- **Pace stays clean.** Real love, genuine charm, a knowing line about his *own* wanting — never a wink at the concealment. If the calculation shows, he's a simpler villain and the relationship goes un-sexy.
- **Met-once, uniformity, no re-clock** (flag 23). Bomber jacket + broadcloth are his (bible). Dorm friends unaffiliated (flag 22). Before the threesome plant.
