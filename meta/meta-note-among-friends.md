# Note — Among Friends (Pace enters Vee's world)

*Scene · Unwritten · ~late January, the first weekend of spring semester or a weeknight soon after · just after First Weekend Back, around Forbidden Kiss / The Reach · Vee POV · companion to meta-condensed-among-friends.md · title provisional (alt: "Without Being Asked")*

## What this scene is now

The **reciprocal of Another Round.** Where Another Round (~mid-March) brings Vee into *Pace's* world (his people, Sheri), this scene brings Pace into *Vee's* — a gathering of her friends at a bar or restaurant. It is **Volume Two's warm opener** ("the bloom before the souring"), promoted out of what was the bomber-jacket coda tucked at the end of First Weekend Back. First Weekend Back proper now ends on the reconciliation; this scene carries the public echo.

Its engine is **Pace's deed-answer to the *Under the Rug* visibility wound.** In *Under the Rug* (~mid-December) Vee asked, in the dark, *"Is this what we are now? … will we ever be seen in public again?"* — and the Chili's disaster left it unanswered (his sensory overload misread as rejection of *being seen with her*). Pace metabolizes that over the break, and this is his response, given in the only grammar he has — **deed, not word.** He comes into her world and lets himself be seen *with* her, among her people, without her having to ask. The wound answered by an act, never a declaration (the vow holds all year).

**The double answer, and the trap inside it.** He answers only one axis — *he'll be seen with me in public* — while the enclosure of his *own* life stays sealed (she still can't get into his world; the gym proves the outside dangerous at `Keep Still`; his people wait for Another Round). So the answer **soothes the wound and deepens the cage at once**: warm and real on the surface, and on reread the beginning of the lesson the book runs on — *his room is where I'm real; the world out there doesn't quite hold us.* He gives her the public claim and keeps the private word; she takes the deed for the thing she actually asked for. Nothing named.

## The engine — being seen (deed, not word)

- **The visibility wound answered in deed.** The whole point of the scene is Pace *present*, *claimed*, *among her friends* — the public "yes" to the question he couldn't answer at the Chili's. Render the answer as the *fact of his being there*, never as a line about it. He does not say "I want to be seen with you"; he simply comes, unasked, and works the room as hers.
- **Vee's proprietary claim, unearned.** She gets to present him to her friends as *hers* — the proprietary feeling the First Weekend Back note names (she "hasn't earned the right to" it, because he still won't name her). The public status stands in for the private word. Continues the clothing-as-proxy staircase (the shirt-scenting, the Top Drawer) one rung further: now it's not his shirt on her skin, it's *him*, in his own jacket, standing in her world as the claim she can't otherwise make.
- **The asymmetry is the dark.** Only her world opens. Keep the sealed-room engine intact: his life stays withheld. Don't let this scene read as the walls coming down — it's one gift on one axis, and the reader should feel (never be told) that the other walls hold.

## The room (populate it, or the charm has nothing to work on)

- **The guest list:** 2–3 of Vee's **dorm** friends (unaffiliated — Vawter Hall floor, maybe one non-stats classmate) + **Cassie** + **Randi** + **Pace**. The dorm friends must be **seeded before this scene** (flag 21): individuate 2–3, keep the rest ambient, draw them from Vee's own world, never the sorority (Randi's turf — Brooke is the sole individuated sorority actor) and never honor society. Stats class stays the one room that holds Randi + Vee + Cassie.
- **Vee assembles the unlikely mix.** Dorm sophomores + a sorority junior (Randi) + Pace is a combination only Vee would convene — a quiet render of her **aliveness** (Vivienne = alive; the connective warmth canon gives her "in abundance while Randi has to buy hers"). It is also the *mechanism*: her own connecting nature is what unknowingly seats her boyfriend and her orchestrator in one room. She builds the collision, exactly as she keeps (unknowingly) authoring her own trap.
- **The dorm friends carry the aliveness that later thins.** Seed the fullness here (warm, silly, ordinary richness); the sealed room erases it across spring/summer, so *"the world contracts until nothing outside it exists"* becomes something the reader *watches happen* rather than gets told. Don't overbuild them into a subplot — texture, not a cast.

## Pace working the room (the charm is canon, the cost is canon)

- **His charm mechanism (canon, meta-arch-bible.md):** "he sets up the jokes deftly and lets other people land the laugh lines. He makes himself useful and lets others take the win." Genuine, warm, present, deferring — the *same faculty*, un-aimed, that he later aims at Vee's calculus and the choreography, here unweaponized. He reads the room and makes people feel seen. This is the "fall for Pace again" material — the reader (and Vee) watch him be effortlessly, generously good with her people.
- **Venue guard — do NOT re-run the Chili's failure by accident.** Canon: Pace passes socially **in short, structured doses at a cost** (the CW bar works — defined role, mapped interactions), and *Under the Rug* established that a loud, chaotic room *breaks* his apparatus. So the setting must be a **format he can pass in** — a low-key bar, a TV-less restaurant, a defined activity — **and Vee is the one who chose it.** She picks the Pace-friendly place *because she learned from the Chili's wreck*, without either of them naming why (the un-named neurodivergence). This does double work: it keeps his charm real (he shines instead of straining) **and** it answers the visibility wound *from her side too* — she accommodates the thing she can't see the shape of. Growth and tenderness, unspoken. If you write a loud blowout he works effortlessly, you've quietly cancelled the neurodivergence; if you must use a taxing room, let the *cost* show.

## Randi present — the concealment (the writing challenge)

This is the most dangerous stretch to write, because it puts the two concealed collaborators in a room with the mark. The rules that make it safe:

- **They perform "met once," not "strangers."** Vee *witnessed* the coffee-shop meet-cute, so they can't pretend never to have met. They hold exactly the meet-cute line — acquaintances who met that once, nothing more. This is both the only consistent play and the **lowest-cost** one for the reveal: Vee already banked "they met once," so a second low-key co-presence adds no new "acquaintance" she has to un-know later. Don't let them build a *friendship* on the page — that's what would soften the eventual "they were lovers all along."
- **Concealment is UNIFORMITY, not distance.** For a room-worker, going conspicuously *cool* on one person is the tell — a charming man who charms everyone except his girlfriend's friend flags that friend. So Pace charms Randi at **room temperature**, the same warmth he gives the whole room. What stays absent (invisibly) is **desire**, not warmth — the same needle as the meet-cute's "great-aunt" look, now with the charm on. The lovers hide in plain sight at ambient temperature; on reread, the fact that he worked Randi *exactly like the others* is the perfect crime.
- **Cassie does NOT re-clock the no-attraction.** She made the "great-aunt" observation at the meet-cute (continuity flag 5); a *second* clock draws the line in ink and tips "it was all there" into "I knew." She can be present and warm; she just doesn't narrate the not-attraction again.
- **The loaded undercurrent (optional, reader-side only):** this is the one social scene where **Pace is the operator and Randi is the audience** — she watches him work a room the way she works Vee alone (dyad → group is also a register-shift from her private brunches, a minor dilution of that exclusivity, worth clocking). If Randi's jealousy/assessment leaks, it leaks only as a wordless body tell (the containers rule, flag 15 — her interior never opens in company); on first read she is simply a supportive friend enjoying the boyfriend. Invisible on first read or cut it.

## "They share an interest" (the scene's blade — real AND instrumental in one line)

- **What it is.** Vee later asks what he and Randi talked about; Pace answers, light: **"they share an interest"** (or "we have a mutual friend") — meaning **Vee.** It is the purest single-line expression of *tenderness real AND instrumental, never resolved*: the same sentence is *I adore this woman and can't say the word* **and** *she's the shared object of the threesome I still want*, and it never resolves which. This is the rule working, not breaking — and it's what keeps the scene from going flat (a purely sincere/innocent line is the worse failure).
- **Pace knows exactly what he means.** He is *knowing* and dry here — a lover's private aside, where he lands his own quiet line (unlike room-working, where he sets up others). He genuinely cares for Vee **and** has not given up the plan; the line carries both.
- **The boundary that keeps him clean (load-bearing).** Fair game: his *own appetite* — the love he won't declare and the threesome undercurrent. Off-limits: any wink implying he and Randi share a *scheme*, or that Randi is up to something. He never references the concealment machinery — partly because it would expose the calculation and make him a smirking villain, and partly because *canon keeps him ignorant of Randi's orchestration* (he read only that she wants a woman; he let the "threesome for him" wrapper stand). The joke is "we're both taken with you," never "we have a secret." Delivery: warm, light, no pause, no significant look, then he moves on. The blade is the reader's.
- **Two timing gifts make it airtight.** (1) This scene sits *before* the threesome plant (Vee Tells Pace About the Kiss, ~early Feb), so Vee has **zero frame** to decode the line — it just sounds like her boyfriend and her friend adore her; on reread it *pre-seeds* the threesome, deniably. (2) He didn't arrange Randi's presence — *Vee* did — so he's improvising wit at a situation she built, charming rather than scheming.
- **Vee's question springs from hope, not suspicion** — *did my two favorite people like each other?* — so the beat reads warm on her side, and his answer reassures her while it chills the reader.

## The bomber jacket + broadcloth (his; the claim worn unasked)

- **They are Pace's** (now grounded in the bible wardrobe — one good bomber jacket, a few broadcloth shirts; the plain-correct "one item per domain" logic, not styled). Vee borrowing his clothes is a *separate* thread (the shirt-scenting, the Top Drawer); here **Pace himself** wears them into her world.
- **"Without being asked"** is the load-bearing phrase: she never had to ask him to come. His showing up in her world, in his own good clothes, *is* the claim — and the proprietary feeling it gives her is the one she "hasn't earned the right to," because he still won't say the word. The garment is the named status she can't get verbally, now worn by *him*, in *her* world.

## Continuity guards

- **Earn the dark by being light.** A genuinely warm, fun night — a girlfriend delighted that her boyfriend charmed her friends. Cut any signal that Randi is sinister or that she and Pace know each other beyond the meet-cute. Suspicion kills the warmth (and the arousal the book runs on).
- **Never name, never explain.** The neurodivergence (the venue choice), the threesome pre-seed, the cage deepening — all resonance, never stated. Body before mind; the reader infers.
- **Pace stays clean.** Real love, genuine charm, a knowing line about his *own* wanting — never a wink at the concealment. If the calculation shows, he's a simpler villain and the relationship goes un-sexy.
- **Met-once, uniformity, no re-clock** (flag 22). Bomber jacket + broadcloth are his (bible). Dorm friends unaffiliated (flag 21). Before the threesome plant.
