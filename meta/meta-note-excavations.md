# Excavations — Companion Note

**Slug:** `excavations` · **Slot:** ~late July / early August · after {{The Peaches}} · **the last Pace/Vee two-hander before the threesome** · **POV:** Vee · **Status:** planned

The past-lovers conversation the book owes the reader, fused with the first-love telling (backstory canon + the telling's register: `meta-note-first-love.md`). Staged late so it arrives as a *symptom*, not backstory: Vee digs into Pace's romantic strata while she is literally digging for Dr. Marsh, and the probing is the never-stated tell that she has begun to worry — maybe not consciously. The quiet cousin of her displaced-fight move: she can't ask *what is happening to us*, so she asks *who came before me*. The open {{The Peaches}} wound (no word, no monogamy) is the fuel under the asking.

## Title

Surface: the ordinary academic term — a field season is "summer excavations." Charge: how many trowels are in the ground at once — Vee sifting Pace's strata; his courteous reciprocal trench into hers; and (rhyming outward, never stated) the dig Randi runs on Vee every week. Plural on purpose: singular would spotlight the one dig that matters and telegraph. Reread charge deepens after the ending: she went digging out of dread and unearthed the thing that made her fall hardest, days before the blindfold.

## The container (summer fieldwork)

Both women are in Blacksburg for Dr. Marsh's summer fieldwork — **archaeological excavation under the anthro department, both women diggers at separate sites** (long days, weekends scarce; both dirty, both sunburned, with sites to compare — `meta-plan-summer.md`). The dig is this scene's mirror made literal: what was buried comes up, gets brushed off, gets catalogued.

## Movement one — his reciprocal trench (light)

- **Savvy, not oblivious:** Pace knows ordinary boyfriends are expected to ask, so he asks — courteous, genuinely interested, good follow-ups. Flawless form.
- **Her answer lands nowhere.** No flicker, no filed grievance, no cooled half-day. He receives her romantic history like a story about a trip she took before they met.
- **She tests:** puts a little shine on an ex in the telling, deploys a name, watches. The provocation sails through him; she's left holding it.
- **What she wanted:** him a little insecure. In her grammar, jealousy is proof of value — men guard what they prize. He expresses possession through attention, provision, and the scene-register; she's listening for the ordinary tell (the flinch at an ex's name) and it never comes. Deeds-vs-words running in a new channel.
- **Why he has none (layered, never stated):** temperamental first — present-moment man, no audit channel; the past isn't real to him the way the room is. And beneath it, passive and structural: nothing could approach Vee without eventually reaching him through the confidante channel, because Vee tells Randi everything. He never watches anyone; the configuration simply has no blind approach. *One restrained line of design, reread-only — never rendered as knowledge he's using.*
- **The unsayable grievance:** "my boyfriend is too secure" sounds insane spoken aloud — it can't discharge as a fight, so it files under the unnameable and feeds the reservoir. **SATC hook (do not foreclose):** if a version of it ever reaches Randi ("he didn't even *blink*"), she holds the real answer and can play it either direction, warm on first read either way. Not designed here; the hook stays open.
- **Density flag:** the merged scene carries a lot; at drafting this movement may compress to a beat rather than a full movement. Keep the failed provocation and the nothing-where-the-flicker-should-be; everything else is negotiable.

## Movement two — her dig (the pattern)

- **Motive (never stated, possibly not conscious):** the summer is destabilizing and the Peaches wound is open; her system registers it; the worry surfaces in the only register available — archaeology instead of the present tense. The reader feels the worry entirely through what she chooses to ask.
- **He answers everything she asks, plainly and truly, at pattern level.** Long women, few of them, no roster, no names (his history carries the same discipline as hers: **no names, no inventory** — shapes only). He volunteers nothing she doesn't ask.
- **"Why aren't you with them anymore?"** — the honest pattern: schedules that never matched his (the offset: his lovers were at other institutional stages; they graduated, took jobs, moved), and under it the truer answer, delivered plain: *they eventually wanted a word I couldn't give yet.* **He has told her exactly how their own story ends, as history, and she hears it as history.** Second Cassandra beat, from the man himself. On reread: unbearable.
- **The live irony (never surfaced):** she audits his past-tense honestly answered while the present-tense answer sits across the brunch table at the plan's peak convergence. She asks the right question one ring too small. No lie is spoken.

## Movement three — "Once.", and the opening (the telling)

- **"Have you ever said it?" — "Once."** One word. The silence where his whole pattern says *sealed* — **and then he opens it.** The reader watches him choose to hand her the realest thing he has.
- **Content and register are owned by `meta-note-first-love.md`:** fifteen and alone on campus, the nineteen-year-old, two years, she left for ordinary reasons, and the boy who broke his silence and said the words was leveled. Contained but leaking — held, not spilled; the pain visible and Vee reads it; he tells it plain and undersells it, and she and the reader do the math. The heartbreak visible, the vow it bred never named.
- **Function:** maximum sympathy against maximum harm — she gets the realest thing he has, then the blindfold comes off. First read: she falls harder. Second read: the powerlessness is the seed of the control working on her right now, and *left for no reason I could name* is the blindness he's repeating. Candidate payoff of the failed *Peter* reach at First Weekend Back — Peter answers here, days before it's taken from her.

## Shape

Three trenches, deepening: his courtesy-dig (symptom-shaped courtesy), her pattern-dig (courtesy-shaped symptom), and the stratum that opens. Both of them perform "the normal couple conversation"; her real question she can't ask — and his realest answer, for once, he *gives*. That's the scene's turn: everywhere else in the book his grammar routes away from disclosure; here, asked plainly at the right depth, he opens. The capstone of the disclosure staircase (name → scar → this).

## Guards

- His delivery is *plain* — factual, unwounded, over-articulate if anything (his stress direction) — until the telling, where the register shifts per `meta-note-first-love.md`: contained but leaking, the containment itself the tell. Vee supplies the rest; he never performs the wound or frames himself as the boy-genius-minor.
- His equanimity in movement one must read as *maturity and security* on first read — half-admirable to the reader, half-admirable to Vee. The sting lives only in the gap between what she staged and what came back. No editorializing.
- The information-position layer is never surfaced. The displacement (probe-as-worry) is never named. The prophecy is never flagged as one. The vow is never named — the reader feels it light up.
- **Don't fuse with {{The Scar}}** (spring, sealed-flat — `meta-note-scar-reveal.md`): different temperatures by design; months apart.
- Body before mind: her worry arrives as behavior (the asking, the salting, the held breath at "Once.") — never as narrated analysis.
