# Condensed — The Scrunchies

*VIGNETTE · Unwritten · ~Oct 16–19 · two beats across two visits · Vee POV · ~½–1 page*

Provision answering a lack, never explained. First riser of the domestic staircase (second riser: The Top Drawer, several scenes later — the domesticity accretes as rhythm across the October scenes, never as a packaged beat).

## Beat one — the hair issue

A stay-over where she has no scrunchie — candidate staging: the Dress Pickup night, when the evening's styling didn't call for one so she didn't bring one. She improvises (low-key comic annoyance); he watches her manage; nothing is said. The beat is small on purpose — a need observed, not a problem raised.

## Beat two — the form on the sink

The following visit: a form of fifty scrunchies on the sink for her. No card, no words, no presentation — it's just there, the way the heat is on before she arrives.

**The color (canonical):** usual design, unusual color — a deep green chosen for her dark red hair, the kind you find online but not in the local stores. The dress skill running in the domestic register: he has matched a color to her coloring before, and the reader recognizes the eye at work without a word. He sought it out; provision with intention. (Palette note: burgundy is the dress's and stays the dress's; green is already in Vee's intimate-object register — the green satin panties of First Night. Coherence, never bound in prose.)

**Name the color lovingly here, once — and never adjective it again.** Every later appearance is "a scrunchie" or at most "the green." The reader's memory does all downstream work; any rarity-talk or camera-linger at a later sighting is prosecution (see grain #2 guardrails).

## What it installs

For Vee: feeling *chosen* — he noticed a need she never voiced and met it in her color. For the reader: the form, the green, and the register of his giving (deed, no words). For spring: the object the entire gaslight thread runs through, planted as pure care.
