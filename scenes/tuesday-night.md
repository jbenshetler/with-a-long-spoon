After, they lay on their backs in the wrecked bed, both of them slick with it, the room gone hot the way the room went hot, the cold October pressing at the windows and getting nowhere. Her hair was a black spill across the pillow and across his arm. She had one arm thrown across his chest, the hand resting open past his far shoulder, palm up, the fingers loose and slightly curled, taking nothing, holding nothing, just lying there open against the sheet.

He turned his head and looked at her.
