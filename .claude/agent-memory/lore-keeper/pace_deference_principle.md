Section added to `meta-arch-pace.md` after "The Chivalric Self-Narration — and 'Not Finally the Lady'" (lines 181-205).

Core principle: Pace leads by default and yields the moment a woman prefers something else in matters that cost him little and matter to her. The deference is genuine, runs on conviction (not tactic), and is domain-limited—it covers everything outside sex and love, where he holds firm instead. Same man in both registers: settled good manners in the kitchen, unwavering chivalric grammar in the bedroom. On the page: a reader should register this as effortless courtesy and ease, never weakness or power reversal.

Load-bearing detail: This principle clarifies why he is not submissive while also making clear his care is real—it's the same conviction in both domains, not a split between surface civility and hidden agenda.
