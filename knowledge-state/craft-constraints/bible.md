# Craft Constraints — Bible

## rendering [HIGH]
Every object associated with Randi must read as enviable on first read, with its calculated nature visible only through the pattern across many objects, never in a single one.
*Violation example:* A single curated object is immediately rendered as obviously cold or fake on its first appearance.

## rendering [HIGH]
Randi's objects must be rendered as public arguments curated for an absent audience, in deliberate contrast to Pace's objects which are private truths correct because he's unwatched.
*Violation example:* Randi's possessions are shown as private, unselfconscious truths chosen with no awareness of being seen.

## rendering [MEDIUM]
Randi's first-gen-wealth tell must be present everywhere: she buys the signifier rather than the thing, reading as taste to her peers but a half-beat off to anyone expert in the field.
*Violation example:* Randi unerringly buys the genuinely right thing with insider expertise.

## rendering [HIGH]
Each room or domain must contain exactly one un-curated, authentic object hidden amid the performance, in contrast to Pace's effortless authenticity everywhere.
*Violation example:* Randi's space is rendered as either uniformly authentic or with multiple obvious real objects per room.

## rendering [MEDIUM]
The final reveal must strip Randi to only the real thing — un-curated, mouth smeared, the reassembled bathroom face now made permanent.
*Violation example:* The ending leaves Randi still fully curated and composed rather than stripped to the single authentic self.

## rendering [HIGH]
Render Vee's warmth and her harm-doing to Randi as a single operation, with Vee unable to perceive the harm because the configuration's information-architecture places her where she cannot see it.
*Violation example:* Vee feels a flicker of guilt or suspicion that her lunch reports about Pace might be hurting Randi.

## rendering [HIGH]
Render the carriers' intensity as the required pitch of their constitution, never as excess they should have moderated.
*Violation example:* A scene frames a carrier's appetite as something they ought to have reined in or kept in check.

## pov [HIGH]
Randi's interior thoughts may open only when she is alone, exclusively in the mirror or the single room, and never in the presence of another character.
*Violation example:* Rendering Randi's private thoughts during a conversation with Pace or her sister.

## pov [HIGH]
Scenes in Randi's room with another character present must run in that character's POV (e.g., Vee's), since the rule governs interior access, not who crosses the threshold.
*Violation example:* A scene where Vee enters Randi's room but is narrated from Randi's interior.

## pov [HIGH]
Randi–Pace house scenes run in Pace's POV under the Scalding model, so the camera honestly inherits his blindness.
*Violation example:* Narrating a Randi–Pace scene inside the house from Randi's perspective.

## prohibition [HIGH]

*Violation example:* Engineering a convenient moment where a character just happens to discover the top drawer.

## prohibition [HIGH]

## rendering [MEDIUM]

## rendering [MEDIUM]
