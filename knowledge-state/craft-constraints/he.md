# Craft Constraints — He

## rendering [HIGH]
Render Sheri's smallness as motion and energy, never as frailty.
*Violation example:* Describing her as delicate, fragile, or implying an eating disorder.

## rendering [HIGH]
Convey her warmth through fierceness and loyalty rather than pleasantness or niceness.
*Violation example:* Writing her as sweet, gentle, and accommodating to show she cares.

## prohibition [HIGH]
Never dramatize her backstory; it should only shape how present interactions render and never appear as a scene or be told outright.
*Violation example:* A flashback scene depicting Pace's past kindness to her.

## rendering [HIGH]
Keep her perception Pace-specific and limited to a single-ping radar on Randi, distinct from Cassandra's broader perception.
*Violation example:* Giving her wide, Cassandra-like intuitive reads across multiple characters.

## rendering [MEDIUM]
Render her friendship with Pace through cost-free side-by-side configurations (the bike, the dance floor — moving, no eye contact), not face-to-face confessional scenes.
*Violation example:* Staging an intimate, eye-to-eye heart-to-heart conversation between Sheri and Pace.

## pov [MEDIUM]
Treat her as a secondary character rendered through effect and function, not standalone interiority.
*Violation example:* A passage dwelling on her private inner thoughts and feelings independent of Pace.
