# Craft Constraints — Cassie

## rendering [MEDIUM]
Depict Cassie as withholding her signals until earned, unlike Randi who offers them immediately.

## name_register [HIGH]
Use Cassie's flat single-word return of "Cassie" to decline the warm-coin name-bit and her dry "mm" as signature responses.

## prohibition [HIGH]
Reserve the unguarded "Oh, Vee" for one specific earned moment rather than using it in ordinary warm scenes.
*Violation example:* In the dinner scene Cassie said "Oh, Vee" and the dread remained only structural.

## rendering [HIGH]
Cassie says 'Goodbye, Vee' as a register-wrong warning-as-farewell and never explains the wrong word.
*Violation example:* Cassie explains what she meant by 'Goodbye, Vee' instead of letting the mismatched register stand.

## name_register [HIGH]
Cassie answers Randi's warm-coin name routine with only the flat word 'Cassie,' declining to complete the bit.
*Violation example:* Cassie plays along with the routine or supplies a follow-up line that completes it.

## rendering [HIGH]
Cassie observes that Pace looked at Randi the way one looks at somebody's great-aunt, conveying zero desire without any dehumanizing subtext.
*Violation example:* Cassie describes the look as seeing Randi as a chair or an object.

## prohibition [HIGH]
Cassie never says 'I told you so' after Vee acknowledges that Cassie saw the truth first.
*Violation example:* Cassie replies to Vee's 'You saw it and I didn't' with any version of 'I told you so.'

## rendering [HIGH]
Venues must read on first pass as natural expressions of Randi's taste, never as engineered tactics.
*Violation example:* The prose notes that the Greek coffee shop was chosen to impress or exclude.

## prohibition [HIGH]
Vee must never articulate the social mechanism, venue engineering, or implications of the sorting.
*Violation example:* Vee says or thinks that Cassie "isn't new" or that Randi functions as a gateway.

## rendering [MEDIUM]
Cassie's safety from corruption and her marginalization must be rendered as the same structural fact.
*Violation example:* The text presents Cassie's exclusion only as loss without also showing it as the condition that keeps her uncorrupted.

## prohibition [HIGH]
Do not manufacture additional Cassie–Randi confrontations or relationship scenes beyond the established contact points.
*Violation example:* A new scene is written in which Cassie and Randi directly negotiate their dynamic.

## rendering [HIGH]
Render Cassie's bone-dry humor through a slightly cocked eyebrow withheld for a beat before confirming, never through her laughing at her own jokes.
*Violation example:* Cassie made the joke and chuckled at her own wit.

## rendering [MEDIUM]
Show Cassie laughing readily only at others' humor, never humor aimed at herself or others.

## prohibition [HIGH]
Cassie asks questions and waits for answers without editorializing about Pace.
