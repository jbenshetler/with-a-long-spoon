# Craft Constraints — Cassie

## rendering [HIGH]
Cassie's farewell line "Goodbye, Vee" must appear exactly as written and stand unexplained, with no narration clarifying why she chose the wrong register word.
*Violation example:* Cassie said goodbye, then explained that it was really a warning about what was coming.

## rendering [HIGH]
Vee's line "It's coffee, weirdo" must dismiss Cassie's warning live and in real time as it happens.
*Violation example:* Vee only later realized Cassie's odd farewell had been a warning she should have heeded.

## rendering [HIGH]
When Randi runs the warm-coin name-bit, Cassie's reply must be the flat single word "Cassie" and she must decline to complete the routine.
*Violation example:* Cassie grinned and played along, tossing the name-bit back at Randi.

## rendering [HIGH]
The meet-cute observation must be rendered as Cassie's no-attraction observation — Pace showing Randi full personhood but zero desire (the not-a-prospect look) — not as a warning about Pace or a dehumanization of Randi.
*Violation example:* He looked at her like she was a chair, like she wasn't even a person.

## rendering [MEDIUM]
The not-a-prospect look should read as an anomaly attaching to the staging or to Randi, which Vee privately recognizes as the look men give Cassie.

## prohibition [HIGH]
After the reckoning, Vee says "You saw it and I didn't," and Cassie must NOT respond with "I told you so" or any equivalent.
*Violation example:* Cassie shrugged. "I told you so."

## name_register [HIGH]
The line "She's a lot" belongs to Sheri as her verdict on Randi and must never be attributed to Cassie.
*Violation example:* Cassie watched Randi go and muttered, "She's a lot."

## rendering [HIGH]
Venues like the Greek coffee shop and dress-code lunch must read on first pass as places Randi naturally goes for taste, with their tactical function visible only on reread.
*Violation example:* The narration notes that Randi chose the coffee shop specifically to make Cassie feel out of place.

## prohibition [HIGH]
Never let the prose flag a venue as a tactic, since doing so flips Randi from warm-operator to obvious-schemer and breaks the 'power wears warmth' architecture.
*Violation example:* A line describes Randi's restaurant pick as a calculated power move.

## pov [HIGH]
Vee never articulates or perceives the sorting mechanism, the venue-engineering, or the marginalization of Cassie, even internally.
*Violation example:* Vee thinks to herself that Randi is using these lunches to edge Cassie out.

## prohibition [HIGH]
Vee must never voice meta-observations like 'Cassie isn't new' or 'Randi is a gateway.'
*Violation example:* Vee says, 'Randi is basically a gateway to a whole different crowd.'

## rendering [HIGH]
Cassie's safety/un-corruptedness and her marginalization must always be rendered as the same structural fact, never one without the other.
*Violation example:* A scene shows Cassie staying pure without any sign that the same outsider status is getting her left behind.

## prohibition [MEDIUM]
Do not manufacture Cassie–Randi confrontations or scenes; their dynamic comes only from their few contacts like the diptych and the meet-cute.
*Violation example:* A chapter stages a direct argument between Cassie and Randi.

## rendering [HIGH]
Render Cassie's humor as bone-dry and never have her laugh at her own jokes.
*Violation example:* Cassie cracked the joke and burst out laughing at her own wit.

## rendering [MEDIUM]
Render her tell as a slightly cocked eyebrow that she withholds for a beat before confirming, resolving near-instantly only with Vee.
*Violation example:* Cassie immediately grinned and winked to make sure everyone caught her line.

## rendering [MEDIUM]
She laughs readily at others but never in a way that is aimed or mocking.
*Violation example:* Cassie laughed cruelly at his expense.

## prohibition [HIGH]
She asks questions and waits without editorializing about Pace.
*Violation example:* Cassie launched into a long opinion explaining what she thought about Pace.

## rendering [MEDIUM]
Keep her as the un-instrumented opposite of Randi: Cassie withholds her signal until it is earned rather than giving it up front to capture.
*Violation example:* Cassie broadcast her feelings openly the moment she walked in, fishing for a reaction.

## name_register [MEDIUM]
Use her flat single-word return — declining the warm-coin name-bit with a plain "Cassie".
*Violation example:* When teased with a cute nickname, Cassie played along warmly and offered a pet name back.

## rendering [LOW]
Employ her established verbal signatures: the dry "mm", "I'm being accurate", and pleasant-voiced needling that tips to comedy when she's enjoying herself.

## rendering [MEDIUM]
Keep her speech sparse and exact — she says little, and what she says is precise.
*Violation example:* Cassie rambled at length, padding her point with filler and digressions.

## prohibition [HIGH]
Reserve "Oh, Vee" as her single moment of unguarded response, used only when a beat genuinely earns it.
*Violation example:* Cassie casually said "Oh, Vee" in passing during an ordinary exchange.
